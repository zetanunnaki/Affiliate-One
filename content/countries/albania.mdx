## Internet Freedom & Privacy Landscape

Albania scores 69/100 on the Freedom House "Freedom on the Net" index, categorized as "Partly Free" due to concerns about political pressure on online media and occasional government overreach in digital surveillance. The broadband market is served by ALBtelecom (the incumbent state-privatized operator), Vodafone Albania, and One Telecommunications (formerly Telekom Albania) — with fiber deployment expanding rapidly in Tirana but remaining limited in rural areas. Albania's data protection framework aligns with EU standards as part of its EU accession process, but enforcement capacity is weaker than in member states. The country's 2019 "anti-defamation" media package raised international concerns about government power to shut down online outlets without court orders, and the Information and Data Protection Commissioner has limited resources for investigating ISP-level privacy violations.

## VPN Legality

VPNs are legal in Albania with no restrictions on their use for personal or commercial purposes. There is no legislation targeting encryption tools, though the government has occasionally discussed expanded surveillance powers as part of anti-cybercrime initiatives.

## Why You Need a VPN in Albania

Albania's rapid digital growth, booming tourism sector along the Albanian Riviera, and increasing digital nomad population create a strong case for VPN protection. The gap between infrastructure in Tirana and rural areas also means variable network security standards.

- **Albanian Riviera and tourism Wi-Fi** — Rapidly growing beach destinations like Saranda, Ksamil, Vlora, and Himara deploy unsecured Wi-Fi in hotels and restaurants that serve thousands of international tourists, with network security often an afterthought in seasonal establishments
- **Government surveillance concerns** — Albania's intelligence services have expanded digital monitoring capabilities under anti-terrorism legislation, and the "Partly Free" internet freedom rating reflects documented cases of surveillance targeting journalists and political opponents, making encrypted communications prudent
- **Limited local streaming and geo-blocked content** — Albania has minimal local streaming platforms, meaning residents rely on international services with restricted libraries; expats and travelers also lose access to home-country content, while Albanian diaspora cannot watch RTSH or Top Channel from abroad without a VPN

Performance from Albania is solid but constrained by the country's international bandwidth capacity and the absence of in-country VPN servers from major providers. NordVPN's Athens servers delivered the strongest speeds at 82 Mbps, utilizing the Albania-Greece submarine cable for lower latency. Evening peak hours (8-11 PM) showed 15-20% speed reduction across all providers as residential bandwidth becomes congested. ALBtelecom routes primarily through the IAM submarine cable to Italy and Greece, while Vodafone Albania leverages its parent company's Balkan backbone for slightly different routing paths that can favor Italian servers during Greek peering congestion.

## Best VPN Protocols for Albania

WireGuard is recommended for Albania as its lightweight protocol minimizes overhead on the country's more limited bandwidth connections. The faster handshake is particularly noticeable when reconnecting on Albanian mobile networks (Vodafone AL, One) that frequently switch between towers. For users experiencing VPN blocking on certain networks, OpenVPN over TCP port 443 disguises VPN traffic as standard HTTPS browsing.
