## Internet Freedom & Privacy Landscape

American Samoa is an unincorporated US territory in the South Pacific, approximately 2,600 miles southwest of Hawaii, with a population of about 49,000. As a US territory, it falls under United States internet governance and constitutional protections, though some federal regulations apply differently to unincorporated territories. The United States scores 75 out of 100 (Free) on Freedom House's Freedom on the Net index. The American Samoa Telecommunications Authority (ASTCA) is the primary ISP, operating the only submarine cable connection (the Hawaiki Cable system) and providing DSL, fiber, and mobile services. Bluesky Communications offers competing mobile services. Internet penetration is approximately 45%, limited by geographic isolation and infrastructure costs. There is no government censorship or content filtering — the challenge is purely one of infrastructure and bandwidth availability.

## VPN Legality

VPNs are completely legal in American Samoa under US law. First Amendment protections and federal privacy frameworks apply, and there are no restrictions on encryption or VPN usage. Both individuals and businesses freely use VPN services without any legal concern.

## Why You Need a VPN in American Samoa

While American Samoa enjoys US legal protections and no censorship, its isolated location and limited infrastructure create specific VPN use cases.

- **Access full US content libraries** — Streaming services sometimes geo-restrict American Samoa differently from the US mainland; connecting to a US server in Hawaii or the West Coast ensures access to complete content catalogs
- **Secure limited bandwidth connections** — ASTCA's network serves a small market with constrained bandwidth; public Wi-Fi at hotels and in Pago Pago harbor area requires encryption to prevent credential theft on shared connections
- **Privacy from ISP data collection** — With ASTCA as the dominant provider, internet activity is concentrated through a single entity; VPN encryption prevents browsing history collection regardless of US data retention policies

## Server Coverage & Speed Performance

No VPN provider operates servers in American Samoa. The nearest locations are in Hawaii, the US West Coast (Los Angeles), and New Zealand. The Hawaiki submarine cable connects the territory to Hawaii and onward to the US mainland. Baseline speeds through ASTCA average 5-25 Mbps on fiber connections in Pago Pago, with mobile data delivering 3-10 Mbps. VPN connections to Hawaiian servers add 50-80ms latency with 15-25% throughput reduction. US West Coast servers (Los Angeles) add approximately 90-130ms. For users primarily needing US content access, Hawaiian servers provide the best balance of low latency and US IP address. New Zealand servers offer an alternative at 40-60ms for accessing Oceanian content.

## Best VPN Protocols for American Samoa

With no censorship or protocol restrictions, users should optimize for performance on bandwidth-constrained Pacific connections. WireGuard's lightweight design minimizes overhead, preserving maximum throughput on ASTCA's limited international capacity. IKEv2 provides stable mobile connections when transitioning between Bluesky cellular and ASTCA Wi-Fi networks around the islands. OpenVPN (UDP) offers reliable fallback with good performance over the submarine cable link to Hawaii.
