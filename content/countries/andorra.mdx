## Internet Freedom & Privacy Landscape

Andorra is a European microstate of approximately 80,000 residents nestled in the Pyrenees mountains between France and Spain. Despite its small size, the principality boasts advanced telecommunications infrastructure driven by its popularity with digital nomads, skiers, and high-net-worth residents attracted by favorable tax policies. Andorra Telecom is the sole provider, operating a government-owned monopoly on all telecommunications services including fiber, mobile, and internet. Internet penetration exceeds 95% with fiber-to-the-home availability in most areas. Andorra is not an EU member state and therefore falls outside GDPR jurisdiction, though it has adopted its own data protection legislation (Llei 29/2021) that broadly aligns with European standards. There is no internet censorship, content filtering, or government-mandated surveillance.

## VPN Legality

VPNs are completely legal in Andorra. The principality's legal framework places no restrictions on encryption tools, privacy software, or VPN usage. The growing digital nomad community and international business sector rely heavily on VPN technology for remote work.

## Why You Need a VPN in Andorra

Andorra's non-EU status, monopoly ISP, and international resident population create practical VPN needs despite the free internet environment.

- **Access EU digital services** — As a non-EU state, Andorran IP addresses are sometimes blocked from EU-only platforms, digital government services, and content libraries subject to EU-only licensing; connecting to French or Spanish servers resolves these restrictions
- **Privacy from monopoly provider** — Andorra Telecom controls all internet access for the entire population through a single infrastructure; VPN encryption prevents the monopoly from building comprehensive browsing profiles
- **Secure ski resort and coworking networks** — Grandvalira, Vallnord, and Andorra la Vella's growing coworking spaces offer shared Wi-Fi used by international visitors; encryption protects financial data and remote work sessions from interception

## Server Coverage & Speed Performance

No VPN provider operates servers specifically in Andorra. The nearest locations are in France (Paris, Toulouse) and Spain (Barcelona, Madrid). Andorra connects internationally through both French and Spanish telecommunications networks, with fiber links to both countries. Baseline speeds on Andorra Telecom's network reach 100-600 Mbps on fiber. VPN connections to Spanish servers (Barcelona) deliver exceptional performance with latency under 15ms and only 5-10% throughput reduction. French servers in Toulouse or Paris add 15-30ms latency. For EU content access, any EU member state server works, with Spain and France providing the best performance due to direct fiber connectivity.

## Best VPN Protocols for Andorra

With no censorship or protocol interference, users should optimize purely for speed and convenience. WireGuard provides near-native speeds on Andorra's high-bandwidth fiber connections. OpenVPN offers corporate compatibility for digital nomads connecting to employer networks worldwide. IKEv2 handles transitions between Andorra Telecom's home fiber, 4G/5G mobile (for hikers and skiers), and coworking Wi-Fi without connection interruptions — particularly valuable in the mountain environment where mobile coverage varies with terrain.
