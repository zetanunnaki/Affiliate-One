## Internet Freedom & Privacy Landscape

Armenia scores 72 out of 100 in Freedom House's Freedom on the Net report, classified as Free. The country maintains one of the most open internet environments in the Caucasus and Central Asian region, with no systematic censorship or content filtering. The primary ISPs are Ucom (offering fiber and mobile), Veon Armenia (formerly Beeline), and Rostelecom Armenia, providing 4G LTE and expanding 5G coverage in Yerevan. Internet penetration exceeds 75%, with Yerevan boasting excellent fiber infrastructure that supports Armenia's thriving IT sector. The country has emerged as a significant tech hub, attracting remote workers and startups from across the former Soviet Union.

## VPN Legality

VPN use is fully legal in Armenia. The government does not restrict or monitor VPN usage, and there are no regulations targeting encrypted communications. Armenia's democratic framework and growing tech sector actively support digital privacy rights.

## Server Coverage & Speed Performance

Some VPN providers are beginning to offer servers in Armenia given the country's growing tech relevance, though coverage remains limited. The nearest major server locations are in Georgia (Tbilisi), Turkey (Istanbul), and various European cities. NordVPN and Surfshark offer connections through nearby countries with latency of 30-60ms to Georgian and Turkish servers. Yerevan's excellent fiber infrastructure means local internet speeds of 100-500 Mbps are common, so VPN performance is primarily limited by the international route rather than local bandwidth. European servers (Germany, Netherlands) deliver latency of 50-80ms with strong throughput.

## Best VPN Protocols for Armenia

WireGuard is the ideal protocol for Armenia, fully leveraging the country's fast fiber infrastructure with minimal overhead. The protocol delivers near-native speeds on Ucom and Veon's networks, easily supporting 4K streaming and large file transfers. OpenVPN (UDP) provides a reliable alternative for corporate environments requiring specific security compliance. Since Armenia does not employ DPI, VPN blocking, or traffic manipulation, obfuscation features are unnecessary, and users can prioritize speed-optimized protocols without compromise.
