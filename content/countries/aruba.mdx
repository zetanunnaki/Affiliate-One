## Internet Freedom & Privacy Landscape

Aruba operates under Dutch Caribbean legal frameworks, inheriting the Netherlands' strong tradition of internet freedom and digital rights. As a constituent country of the Kingdom of the Netherlands, Aruba benefits from European-standard data protection principles. The telecommunications market is served by SETAR (the government-owned provider) and Digicel, with SETAR providing the backbone infrastructure including fiber-optic connections. Internet penetration exceeds 85%, among the highest in the Caribbean, with robust 4G LTE coverage across the entire 180-square-kilometer island. Both fixed broadband and mobile internet perform well, supported by submarine cable connections to the South American mainland and the wider Caribbean.

## VPN Legality

VPNs are fully legal in Aruba. As part of the Kingdom of the Netherlands, Aruba follows Dutch-aligned digital rights frameworks that protect users' right to privacy and encrypted communications. There are no restrictions on VPN usage whatsoever.
