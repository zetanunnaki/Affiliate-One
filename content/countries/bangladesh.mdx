## Internet Freedom & Privacy Landscape

Bangladesh scores **39 out of 100** on Freedom House's Freedom on the Net index, placing it firmly in the "Partly Free" category. With over 130 million internet users — the vast majority connecting via mobile data through providers like Grameenphone, Robi, and Banglalink — the country has experienced rapid digital growth alongside increasing government control over online expression.

The Bangladesh Telecommunication Regulatory Commission (BTRC) has the authority to order ISPs to block websites and services on short notice. During the 2024 student-led protests, the government imposed one of the longest internet shutdowns in the country's history, cutting off mobile data and broadband services for multiple days. Social media platforms including Facebook, WhatsApp, and Messenger have been repeatedly blocked during periods of political unrest, with the BTRC directing operators to restrict access at the network level.

Surveillance capabilities have expanded in recent years. The government operates monitoring systems through the National Telecommunications Monitoring Centre (NTMC), which can intercept communications across all major carriers. Fixed-line broadband from providers like BTCL and private ISPs such as Amber IT and Link3 Technologies remains concentrated in Dhaka and Chittagong, while rural areas depend almost entirely on mobile connectivity.

## VPN Legality

VPN use is not explicitly illegal in Bangladesh, but the government has blocked VPN provider websites during shutdown periods and the 2006 ICT Act (amended in 2013) and the Digital Security Act of 2018 criminalize various forms of online expression. Using a VPN for lawful purposes remains technically legal, though the government's stance can shift rapidly during political crises.

## Why You Need a VPN in Bangladesh

A VPN is increasingly essential for anyone relying on internet access in Bangladesh:

- **Survive internet shutdowns** — During political unrest, pre-configured VPNs with obfuscation can sometimes maintain connectivity when standard connections fail, particularly through less commonly blocked protocols
- **Access blocked social media** — Facebook, WhatsApp, and Messenger are periodically restricted; a VPN restores access to these communication lifelines used by over 50 million Bangladeshis
- **Protect against NTMC surveillance** — Encrypt your traffic to prevent government monitoring of communications, financial transactions through bKash and Nagad, and browsing activity

## Server Coverage & Speed Performance

Bangladesh's average mobile broadband speed hovers around 10-20 Mbps on 4G networks, with fixed broadband in Dhaka reaching 50-100 Mbps on fiber connections. The nearest VPN server locations are typically in India (Mumbai, Chennai, Kolkata) and Singapore. Connecting to Indian servers adds minimal latency (30-50ms), making them ideal for everyday browsing and communication. Expect realistic throughput of 8-15 Mbps on mobile and 30-60 Mbps on fixed broadband when connected to nearby servers.

During active restriction periods, speeds drop significantly as obfuscation protocols add overhead, and some server locations become unreachable. Having servers in multiple nearby countries configured in advance provides crucial redundancy.

## Best VPN Protocols for Bangladesh

For normal conditions, WireGuard delivers the best speeds on Bangladesh's bandwidth-constrained networks. During active censorship periods, switch to obfuscated protocols — NordVPN's obfuscated servers (OpenVPN-based) and Surfshark's Camouflage mode disguise VPN traffic as regular HTTPS, making it harder for BTRC-level filtering to detect. Keep Shadowsocks configurations as a backup for severe crackdowns.
