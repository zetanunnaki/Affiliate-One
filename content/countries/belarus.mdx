## Internet Freedom & Privacy Landscape

Belarus scores just 11 out of 100 on Freedom House's Freedom on the Net index, making it one of the most repressive internet environments in Europe. The Lukashenko government maintains extensive control over telecommunications through state-owned Beltelecom, which operates the country's sole international internet gateway. Mobile operators A1 Belarus (Telekom Austria subsidiary), MTS Belarus, and life:) (Turkcell) all route traffic through government-controlled infrastructure. Since the disputed 2020 presidential election, authorities have intensified online surveillance, blocked hundreds of websites, and criminalized sharing of "extremist" content on social media. Internet penetration exceeds 80%, but access is heavily monitored and filtered. The government employs deep packet inspection technology to identify and block VPN protocols.

## VPN Legality

Belarus officially restricted VPN usage in 2015, and enforcement intensified after 2020. While casual VPN use is rarely prosecuted directly, authorities actively block VPN protocols at the network level. Many popular VPN services are inaccessible from within Belarus without obfuscation technology. Downloading VPN apps from standard app stores may be blocked, and VPN provider websites are routinely filtered. Users should configure VPN access before entering the country.

## Server Coverage & Speed Performance

No VPN providers maintain servers inside Belarus (doing so would require cooperation with authorities that would compromise user privacy). The nearest reliable options are Lithuania (Vilnius), Poland (Warsaw), and Ukraine (Kyiv). Belarusian internet speeds are generally good at 30-60 Mbps on fixed broadband and 15-40 Mbps on 4G. ## Best VPN Protocols for Belarus

Standard VPN protocols (OpenVPN, WireGuard, IKEv2) are actively detected and blocked by Belarusian DPI systems. Obfuscated protocols are mandatory. NordVPN's obfuscated servers remain the most reliable, disguising VPN traffic as regular HTTPS connections. Surfshark's Camouflage Mode provides a secondary option. Proton VPN's Stealth protocol, specifically engineered for censorship circumvention, offers another pathway. Users must configure multiple protocols before entering Belarus, as VPN provider websites and app store downloads may be inaccessible inside the country. Manual OpenVPN-over-TCP-443 configurations should be stored locally as an emergency fallback.
