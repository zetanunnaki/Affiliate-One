## Internet Freedom & Privacy Landscape

Belgium is not individually assessed by Freedom House's Freedom on the Net index, but as an EU member state it benefits from the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) framework, widely regarded as the world's strongest privacy standard. The Belgian Data Protection Authority (Autorité de protection des données / Gegevensbeschermingsautoriteit) has been notably active in GDPR enforcement, issuing significant fines against companies for consent violations and unlawful data processing. However, Belgium's 2016 Data Retention Law requires telecommunications providers to store metadata for 12 months, a measure justified on national security grounds given Brussels' status as the EU and NATO capital.

Local ISPs such as Proximus, Telenet, and Orange Belgium are subject to these data retention mandates and must store connection metadata including timestamps, IP addresses, and communication endpoints. Belgium's State Security Service (Veiligheid van de Staat / Sureté de l'Etat) has legal authority to request this data for intelligence purposes.

As a member of the Fourteen Eyes intelligence-sharing alliance (SIGINT Seniors Europe), Belgium participates in multilateral signals intelligence cooperation. This means data collected by Belgian intelligence services can potentially be shared with partner nations including the United States, United Kingdom, and other NATO allies. For diplomats, EU officials, and privacy-conscious residents, this alliance membership creates a compelling reason to encrypt all internet traffic.

Belgium's position as host to major EU institutions (European Commission, European Council, European Parliament) and NATO headquarters in Evere makes it a focal point for both state-sponsored and criminal cyber espionage. The Belgian Centre for Cybersecurity (CCB) has documented increasing advanced persistent threat (APT) activity targeting government networks and EU-related organizations. In 2023, Belgian intelligence services reported a 40% increase in attempted cyber intrusions against diplomatic missions in Brussels. The country's multilingual internet landscape — with content split between Dutch-speaking Flanders, French-speaking Wallonia, and the bilingual Brussels-Capital Region — also means ISPs maintain separate content delivery networks that can create regional performance differences.

## VPN Legality

VPNs are fully legal in Belgium with no restrictions on their use. Belgian law protects the right to use encryption tools for personal privacy. There are no registration requirements for VPN usage, and ISPs cannot legally discriminate against VPN traffic under EU net neutrality regulations.

## Why You Need a VPN in Belgium

Belgium's position as the institutional heart of Europe makes it a prime target for state-sponsored cyber espionage, corporate surveillance, and criminal hacking. The concentration of EU and NATO decision-makers in Brussels elevates the threat landscape beyond that of a typical Western European country.

- **Accessing geo-restricted content** — Belgian streaming services like VTM GO and RTBF Auvio are geolocked, while travelers abroad lose access to their Belgian subscriptions; a VPN also unlocks larger Netflix libraries from the US or UK
- **Securing public Wi-Fi** — The EU Quarter in Brussels, SNCB/NMBS train Wi-Fi, and public hotspots in Grand Place and shopping galleries are prime targets for credential theft given the high-value targets who connect to them
- **Avoiding ISP throttling** — Proximus and Telenet have been documented throttling peer-to-peer traffic and deprioritizing streaming during peak evening hours; VPN encryption prevents protocol-based discrimination
- **Protecting against espionage** — The concentration of high-value diplomatic, corporate (SWIFT, Euroclear), and institutional targets makes Belgian networks attractive to nation-state hackers; a VPN adds a critical encryption layer against man-in-the-middle attacks targeting sensitive communications
- **Accessing region-locked Belgian content** — VTM GO content differs between Flanders and Wallonia, and some RTBF programming is restricted outside Belgium; a VPN with Belgian servers ensures access regardless of your physical location within or outside the country

## Best VPN Protocols for Belgium

Belgium has no censorship or DPI filtering, so protocol selection should prioritize raw speed and stability. WireGuard (or NordVPN's NordLynx implementation) is the optimal choice, delivering the fastest throughput with minimal overhead. For users on corporate networks within EU institutions that may restrict VPN connections, OpenVPN over TCP port 443 provides reliable fallback by disguising VPN traffic as standard HTTPS. Proton VPN's Stealth protocol is recommended for users connecting from restricted corporate environments in the EU Quarter.

When choosing a VPN for Belgium, prioritize providers with local server infrastructure in Belgian data centers rather than those routing through Amsterdam or Frankfurt. While neighboring servers still deliver good performance, a Belgian server ensures you retain access to local content and benefit from the shortest possible route to domestic services. For EU institution staff and diplomatic personnel, verify that the provider operates outside the Fourteen Eyes jurisdiction and offers multi-hop routing to add jurisdictional separation between your traffic and Belgian intelligence collection capabilities.
