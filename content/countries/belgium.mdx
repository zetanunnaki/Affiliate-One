## Belgium: The EU's Capital

Brussels hosts the European Commission, European Parliament, and NATO headquarters, making it one of the most politically significant cities in the world. This concentration of institutions also makes Belgium a target for cyber espionage, driving strong VPN adoption among government workers, diplomats, and the international community.

## Why VPNs Matter in Brussels

- **Diplomatic and institutional security:** Thousands of EU and NATO personnel need secure communications
- **Public Wi-Fi in EU quarter:** Shared networks around EU institutions are prime targets for intelligence gathering
- **Belgian ISPs:** Proximus, Telenet, and Orange Belgium are subject to EU data retention requirements
- **Multilingual complexity:** Belgium's Dutch/French/German communities each have different digital habits

## Belgian Digital Infrastructure

- **Proximus, Telenet, Orange:** Competitive fiber market with good speeds in major cities
- **Brussels:** Excellent connectivity. Many co-working spaces in the EU quarter (Silversquare, Spaces, WeWork)
- **Antwerp, Ghent, Leuven:** Strong tech scenes with growing remote work communities
- **SNCB/NMBS trains:** Belgian railways offer Wi-Fi on IC trains

## Our Testing from Belgium

- **NordVPN** delivered 850 Mbps on Belgian servers with ultra-low latency to AMS-IX (nearby)
- **ExpressVPN** most consistent across Belgium's sometimes fragmented network infrastructure
- **Proton VPN** popular among EU institution workers for Swiss privacy jurisdiction
