## Internet Freedom & Privacy Landscape

Belize has not been formally assessed by Freedom House for internet freedom, but the country generally maintains an open internet environment without systematic censorship or content filtering. The telecommunications market is served by three main providers: Belize Telemedia Limited (BTL), Smart (owned by Speednet Communications), and DigiCell. BTL dominates the fixed-line broadband market while mobile data services from Smart and DigiCell provide coverage in population centers along the coast and major towns. Internet penetration continues to grow, particularly in tourist areas like Ambergris Caye, San Pedro, and Caye Caulker, though rural and jungle regions still experience limited connectivity.

## VPN Legality

VPNs are completely legal in Belize. There are no laws restricting their use, and both residents and visitors regularly use VPN services without any legal concerns.

## Server Coverage & Speed Performance

No major VPN provider operates servers physically located in Belize. The nearest server locations are typically in Mexico (Cancun/Mexico City), the United States (Miami), or Costa Rica. Connecting to Miami servers generally delivers the best latency for Belizean users, with ping times averaging 40-60ms depending on your local connection quality. Users on BTL fiber in Belize City or San Pedro can expect download speeds of 30-60 Mbps through a VPN, while mobile data connections will see more modest throughput due to underlying network limitations.

## Best VPN Protocols for Belize

WireGuard is the recommended protocol for Belize, delivering the best balance of speed and security on the country's moderate bandwidth connections. For users on slower rural connections or mobile data, WireGuard's lightweight handshake and low overhead provide noticeable performance advantages over OpenVPN. IKEv2 is a solid alternative for mobile devices that frequently switch between Wi-Fi and cellular networks.
