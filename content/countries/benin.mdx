## Internet Freedom & Privacy Landscape

Benin scores 72 out of 100 on Freedom House's Freedom on the Net index, classified as "Free." This ranking places Benin among the more open internet environments in West Africa. However, the country's track record is not unblemished — the government shut down the internet entirely during the April 2019 elections and has imposed social media restrictions during subsequent political events. These incidents demonstrate that even relatively free internet environments in Africa can face sudden restrictions during politically sensitive periods.

The country's telecommunications sector is served primarily by MTN Benin, Moov Africa (formerly Moov Benin), and the state-owned Benin Telecoms (Libercom). MTN and Moov dominate the mobile market, providing 4G coverage in Cotonou, Porto-Novo, and other urban centers. Fixed broadband remains limited outside major cities, with average speeds of 15-30 Mbps in Cotonou and significantly less in rural areas. The country's connectivity relies heavily on submarine cables landing at Cotonou, including the SAT-3/WASC and ACE systems.

## VPN Legality

VPN use is legal in Benin. There are no specific laws restricting VPN software for personal or business purposes. However, the government has shown willingness to restrict internet access entirely during political events, making it advisable to have VPN software pre-installed and configured before periods of potential disruption.

## Why You Need a VPN in Benin

Despite Benin's relatively free internet environment, several factors make VPN use prudent for residents and visitors.

- **Protection during political shutdowns** — The 2019 internet shutdown and subsequent social media blocks during elections and protests demonstrate that access can be cut without warning; a VPN with obfuscation capabilities provides the best chance of maintaining connectivity during these events
- **Public Wi-Fi security in urban areas** — Cotonou's growing cafe culture, hotels, and shared workspaces offer free Wi-Fi that lacks encryption, exposing financial transactions including mobile money (MTN MoMo, Moov Money) to interception
- **Access geo-restricted content** — French-language streaming platforms, European banking services, and international content libraries often restrict access from Beninese IP addresses; a VPN allows connection through French or European servers

## Server Coverage & Speed Performance

No major VPN provider operates physical servers in Benin. The nearest server locations are typically in Nigeria (Lagos), Ghana (Accra), and France (Paris). Due to Benin's submarine cable connections routing primarily through West African coastal infrastructure, expect latency of 15-30 ms to Lagos servers, 25-40 ms to Accra, and 60-90 ms to Paris. Given Benin's baseline broadband speeds of 15-30 Mbps, VPN connections through nearby African servers typically retain 70-85% of base speed, while European connections may reduce throughput to 50-70% of baseline. For users on mobile data (which many Beninese rely upon), speeds through VPN will be lower but generally adequate for browsing and messaging.

## Best VPN Protocols for Benin

WireGuard is the recommended protocol for Benin under normal conditions, delivering the best speed-to-overhead ratio on the country's moderate bandwidth connections. Since Benin does not routinely deploy deep packet inspection for VPN detection, standard protocols work reliably most of the time. However, during political events when the government has historically imposed restrictions, having OpenVPN with obfuscation (TCP port 443) pre-configured provides critical fallback capability. Users should configure both standard and obfuscated profiles before elections or protests.
