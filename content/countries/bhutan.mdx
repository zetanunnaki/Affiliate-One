## Internet Freedom & Privacy Landscape

Bhutan has not been formally assessed by Freedom House in its Freedom on the Net report, but the kingdom maintains a relatively open internet environment with few documented restrictions. The country's primary ISPs are Bhutan Telecom (state-owned) and TashiCell (private), both offering 4G LTE coverage in urban centers like Thimphu, Paro, and Punakha. Internet penetration remains modest at around 55%, with rural and mountainous areas largely unserved due to the challenging Himalayan terrain.

## VPN Legality

VPN use is fully legal in Bhutan. There are no laws prohibiting the use of virtual private networks, and the government has not taken steps to restrict or regulate VPN traffic. Both residents and visitors can freely use VPN services without legal concern.

## Server Coverage & Speed Performance

No major VPN provider operates servers inside Bhutan due to its small market and limited data center infrastructure. The nearest server locations are typically in India (Mumbai, Delhi) or Singapore. Expect baseline latency of 80-150ms to Indian servers and 120-200ms to Singapore, which is adequate for browsing, streaming at HD quality, and video calls. NordVPN and Surfshark both maintain large server fleets in India that deliver the best speeds for Bhutan-based users.

## Best VPN Protocols for Bhutan

WireGuard is the recommended protocol for Bhutan given its efficiency on lower-bandwidth connections. The lightweight protocol reduces overhead compared to OpenVPN, which matters on TashiCell and Bhutan Telecom's mobile networks where bandwidth can be limited. IKEv2 is a solid fallback for mobile devices due to its seamless reconnection when switching between cell towers during travel. Since Bhutan does not employ deep packet inspection or VPN blocking, obfuscation features are unnecessary here.
