## Internet Freedom & Privacy Landscape

Botswana has not been assessed in Freedom House's Freedom on the Net report, but the country is widely regarded as one of Africa's most stable democracies with an open internet environment. The primary ISPs are Botswana Telecommunications Corporation (BTC), Mascom Wireless, and Orange Botswana, providing 4G LTE coverage across urban areas including Gaborone, Francistown, and Maun. Internet penetration stands at approximately 65%, though connectivity drops significantly in remote safari regions like the Okavango Delta and Central Kalahari.

## VPN Legality

VPN use is fully legal in Botswana. There are no restrictions on VPN technology, and the government has not enacted legislation targeting encrypted communications. Both citizens and visitors may use VPNs freely for personal and business purposes.

## Server Coverage & Speed Performance

No major VPN provider currently operates servers within Botswana. The closest server locations are South Africa (Johannesburg), which offers excellent connectivity thanks to direct fiber links between the two countries. Users can expect latency of 30-50ms to Johannesburg servers, delivering speeds sufficient for 4K streaming and video conferencing. NordVPN operates 60+ servers in South Africa, while Surfshark maintains a strong presence in the region, ensuring reliable connections from Botswana.

## Best VPN Protocols for Botswana

WireGuard is the optimal protocol choice for Botswana, delivering maximum speed with minimal overhead on both fiber and mobile connections. For users in safari areas relying on satellite internet with higher latency and packet loss, IKEv2 provides better stability and faster reconnection. OpenVPN (UDP) remains a reliable fallback for older devices. Since Botswana does not implement DPI or VPN blocking, obfuscation tools are not required.
