## Internet Freedom & Privacy Landscape

Bulgaria receives a 70/100 score from Freedom House for internet freedom, placing it in the "Free" category though with noted concerns about media ownership concentration and occasional political pressure on digital outlets. The broadband market is highly competitive, served by Vivacom (the largest fixed-line operator), A1 Bulgaria, and Yettel (formerly Telenor Bulgaria), with fiber-to-the-home available in most urban areas at remarkably low prices. As an EU member, Bulgaria enforces GDPR protections, though the Commission for Personal Data Protection has faced criticism for limited enforcement resources and the country's intelligence services maintain lawful interception programs under the Electronic Communications Act.

## VPN Legality

VPN usage is completely legal in Bulgaria for all purposes. There are no restrictions on encryption technology, and Bulgarian businesses across the IT outsourcing sector routinely deploy VPNs for secure client communications with Western European and American companies.

## Why You Need a VPN in Bulgaria

Bulgaria's combination of ultra-cheap internet, growing digital nomad communities in Sofia and along the Black Sea coast, and EU membership creates a unique environment where excellent infrastructure meets elevated privacy concerns.

- **Coworking and cafe Wi-Fi exposure** — Sofia's thriving coworking scene (Puzl CowOrKing, Betahaus, SOHO) and popular cafes along Vitosha Boulevard deploy shared networks serving hundreds of remote workers daily, creating high-value targets for credential harvesting attacks
- **ISP data retention under Bulgarian law** — Vivacom, A1, and Yettel retain user traffic metadata for 6 months under Bulgaria's transposition of EU data retention requirements, with Bulgarian security services (DANS) having documented access to this data for national security investigations
- **Restricted streaming libraries** — Despite EU membership, Bulgaria receives significantly smaller content catalogs on Netflix, Disney+, and Amazon Prime compared to Western European markets; a VPN enables access to US or UK libraries with thousands of additional titles, while Bulgarian expats can access BNT and bTV content from abroad

## Best VPN Protocols for Bulgaria

WireGuard is optimal for Bulgaria given the high-speed fiber connections and availability of local or nearby servers. On Vivacom and A1 connections exceeding 500 Mbps, WireGuard's efficiency becomes apparent compared to OpenVPN's higher CPU overhead. For mobile users switching between Yettel's 4G/5G network and fixed broadband, IKEv2's seamless reconnection makes it the superior protocol choice.
