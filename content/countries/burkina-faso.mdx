## Internet Freedom & Privacy Landscape

Burkina Faso scores 50 out of 100 in Freedom House's Freedom on the Net report, placing it at the boundary between Partly Free and Not Free. The military government that took power in 2022 has suspended social media platforms and imposed internet shutdowns during political transitions and security operations. The primary ISPs are Orange Burkina Faso, Moov Africa Burkina, and Telecel Faso, providing 4G coverage primarily in Ouagadougou, Bobo-Dioulasso, and other major urban centers. Internet penetration remains around 25%, with mobile internet accounting for the vast majority of connections in this French-speaking Sahelian nation.

## VPN Legality

VPN use remains legal in Burkina Faso, though the political situation has created uncertainty. The military government has not enacted specific legislation banning VPNs, but has demonstrated willingness to disrupt internet services during coups and security operations. Users should exercise caution and stay informed about the evolving regulatory environment.

## Server Coverage & Speed Performance

No VPN provider operates servers within Burkina Faso. The nearest options are Europe (Paris, France) via submarine cables connecting through coastal West African nations, or South African servers. Paris connections typically deliver latency of 100-150ms through the ACE and MainOne cable systems that reach landlocked Burkina Faso via neighboring countries. Given the country's limited bandwidth, even these moderate latencies are acceptable for most use cases. NordVPN and Surfshark provide extensive European coverage that serves Burkinabe users adequately.

## Best VPN Protocols for Burkina Faso

During normal periods, WireGuard provides the best speed-to-security ratio on Orange and Moov mobile networks. However, during active shutdowns and political crises, obfuscated protocols become essential. OpenVPN over TCP port 443 disguises VPN traffic as HTTPS, while providers like NordVPN offer dedicated obfuscated servers designed to bypass government-level blocking. Surfshark's Camouflage mode and Proton VPN's Stealth protocol are specifically engineered for these high-censorship scenarios. Users in Burkina Faso should configure their VPN with obfuscation as the default, given the unpredictable timing of shutdowns.
