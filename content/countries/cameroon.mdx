## Internet Freedom & Privacy Landscape

Cameroon scores 31 out of 100 on Freedom House's Freedom on the Net index, classified as "Partly Free." The country gained international notoriety for imposing a 240-day internet shutdown on its anglophone Northwest and Southwest regions in 2017-2018, one of the longest shutdowns in African history. Periodic disruptions have continued in these regions since then as the anglophone crisis persists. The two dominant ISPs â€” MTN Cameroon and Orange Cameroon â€” comply with government shutdown orders. Connectivity is concentrated in Douala (economic capital) and Yaounde (political capital), with average speeds of 10-25 Mbps on 4G. The country connects internationally through the SAT-3/WASC and WACS submarine cables at Douala.

## VPN Legality

VPN use is legal in Cameroon. No specific legislation prohibits VPN software, and VPN adoption surged dramatically during the anglophone shutdown, with millions of citizens in affected regions using VPNs to maintain any internet access. The government has not prosecuted individuals for VPN use itself, though online speech remains subject to criminal penalties under anti-terrorism and cybercrime laws.

## Best VPN Protocols for Cameroon

Obfuscated protocols are strongly recommended for Cameroon, particularly for users in the anglophone regions. During shutdown periods, authorities deploy DPI to identify and block VPN traffic, making standard WireGuard or OpenVPN detectable. NordVPN's obfuscated servers, Surfshark's Camouflage Mode, and Proton VPN's Stealth protocol disguise traffic as regular HTTPS, significantly improving the chances of maintaining access. All VPN apps and configurations should be pre-installed before any anticipated disruption.
