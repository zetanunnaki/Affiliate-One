## Canada: Five Eyes Member with Evolving Privacy Laws

Canada is a founding member of the Five Eyes intelligence alliance and has been updating its privacy framework to address digital age concerns. PIPEDA (Personal Information Protection and Electronic Documents Act) governs private-sector data handling, while Bill C-27 aims to modernize Canadian privacy law.

## Why Canadians Use VPNs

- **Five Eyes surveillance:** As a Five Eyes member, Canada participates in intelligence sharing. Privacy-conscious Canadians use VPNs to add an encryption layer
- **ISP monitoring:** Canadian ISPs can track browsing activity. Bell, Rogers, and Telus have been involved in data controversies
- **Geographic content access:** Canadian Netflix has a different library than US Netflix. Canadians traveling abroad need VPNs to access Canadian banking and services
- **Public Wi-Fi:** Tim Hortons, Starbucks, libraries, and malls offer free Wi-Fi across the country

## Remote Work Across Canada

Canada's vast geography makes remote work both common and challenging:

- **Urban centres:** Toronto, Vancouver, Montreal, and Ottawa have excellent broadband and co-working infrastructure
- **Rural Canada:** Internet quality drops significantly outside major cities. Starlink has become popular in rural and northern communities
- **Northern territories:** Yukon, NWT, and Nunavut have limited internet infrastructure. Satellite internet is often the only option
- **Co-working:** WeWork, Spaces, and many independent co-working spaces in major cities
