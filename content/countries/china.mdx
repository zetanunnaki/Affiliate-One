## The Great Firewall of China

China operates the world's most sophisticated internet censorship system, commonly called the Great Firewall (GFW). It blocks access to thousands of websites and services that most of the world takes for granted:

**Blocked in China:**
- Google (Search, Gmail, Maps, Drive, YouTube)
- Facebook, Instagram, WhatsApp
- Twitter/X
- Wikipedia (most languages)
- Most Western news sites
- Many VPN provider websites

For remote workers, this means your work tools — Gmail, Slack, Google Drive, Notion, GitHub — may be completely inaccessible without a VPN.

## VPN Legality in China

This is a nuanced topic. The Chinese government has declared unauthorized VPN services illegal, but enforcement primarily targets VPN providers and sellers, not individual users. Millions of Chinese citizens and virtually all foreign businesses use VPNs daily.

**Important:** We provide this information for educational purposes. We are not legal advisors. Research current regulations before traveling to China.

Practical reality:
- Foreign business travelers routinely use VPNs without issues
- International hotels expect guests to use VPNs
- Enforcement intensifies around politically sensitive dates
- Corporate VPNs for business use are generally tolerated

## Essential Preparation: Before You Arrive

This is critical: **download and configure your VPN before entering China.** Most VPN provider websites are blocked within China, making it extremely difficult to download apps after arrival.

**Pre-travel checklist:**
1. Subscribe to a VPN known to work in China (see below)
2. Download apps on all your devices
3. Configure multiple protocols (some work better than others in China)
4. Download the VPN's manual configuration files as backup
5. Set up multiple server locations (Hong Kong, Japan, Singapore, US)
6. Test that the VPN connects successfully before your trip

## Daily Life with a VPN in China

- **Morning:** Connect VPN before opening any work apps. Gmail, Slack, and Google Drive all require VPN
- **WeChat:** China's super-app works without a VPN. You'll need it for local communication, payments, and navigation
- **Hotel Wi-Fi:** Most international hotels have Wi-Fi but it's subject to the GFW. Use your VPN
- **Mobile data:** Purchase a China SIM or use a Hong Kong SIM with roaming. Data through Chinese carriers is subject to the GFW
- **Payment:** Set up Alipay or WeChat Pay before arrival if possible. International credit cards have limited acceptance. Cash (CNY) is your backup

## Performance Expectations

VPN performance in China is significantly slower than in open-internet countries due to the distance traffic must travel and GFW interference:

- Expect 20-50 Mbps through the VPN (vs 100+ Mbps without in Chinese cities)
- Video calls are possible but may have occasional quality drops
- Connecting to Hong Kong or Japan servers provides the best speed/reliability balance
- Speed varies by time of day — evenings are typically slower
- During politically sensitive periods, VPN reliability may drop temporarily
