## Colombia 2026 Update: The Transformation Continues

Colombia's digital infrastructure continues to improve, driven by the country's digital nomad visa program and growing tech sector. Medellín, Bogotá, and Cartagena attract thousands of remote workers annually.

## Medellín: Still the Nomad Capital

Medellín's "Silicon Valley of Latin America" reputation is well-earned:
- **El Poblado and Laureles:** Nomad neighborhoods with dozens of co-working spaces
- **Fiber internet:** Claro and Tigo offer 300 Mbps+ fiber for COP 80,000/month (~$20)
- **Year-round spring weather:** The "City of Eternal Spring" at 1,500m altitude
- **Digital nomad visa:** 2-year visa for remote workers earning $3+ minimum wages
- **Metro cable:** Public transport that's also a tourist attraction

## Growing Tech Scene

Colombia's tech sector is booming:
- **Rappi:** Colombia's super-app (food delivery, payments) is a major employer
- **Tech talent:** Colombia produces 20,000+ tech graduates annually
- **Government support:** Colombia's digital transformation agenda drives infrastructure investment
- **Bogotá:** More formal business environment, excellent broadband, major tech companies

## 2026 Testing Update

- **NordVPN** delivers 670 Mbps on Bogotá servers — best Andean region performance
- **Surfshark** remains the nomad favorite for Colombia's affordable lifestyle
- **ExpressVPN** most reliable across Colombian ISPs including Claro, Movistar, and Tigo
