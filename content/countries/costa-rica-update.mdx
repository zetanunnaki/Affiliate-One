## Costa Rica 2026 Update: The Nomad Visa Effect

Costa Rica's Rentista digital nomad visa has been a massive success, attracting thousands of remote workers from the US, Canada, and Europe. This influx has driven connectivity improvements, co-working space growth, and better digital infrastructure across the country.

## Growing Nomad Hotspots

- **San José:** Still the connectivity hub with the best broadband. Hundreds of cafés and co-working spaces
- **Santa Teresa:** Surfing + working community has driven fiber internet deployment. Several dedicated co-working spaces
- **Tamarindo:** More developed beach town with reliable internet and growing nomad infrastructure
- **Puerto Viejo:** Caribbean coast alternative with a laid-back vibe and improving connectivity
- **Nosara:** Yoga + surf + remote work. Starlink growing in this area

## Connectivity Improvements

Since the nomad visa launched:
- Fiber broadband expanding beyond San José
- Starlink available in rural areas
- Cafés increasingly competing on Wi-Fi quality
- Co-working spaces opening in beach towns
- Mobile data from Kolbi, Claro, and Movistar improving 4G coverage
