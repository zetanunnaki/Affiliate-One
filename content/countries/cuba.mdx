## Cuba: Connected but Controlled

Cuba went from having almost no internet to widespread mobile data access in just a few years. ETECSA, the state-owned sole provider, launched 3G in 2018 and 4G in 2023. But access is expensive, slow, and censored. The government monitors all internet traffic and has imposed shutdowns during protests.

## Internet Reality

- **ETECSA monopoly:** Sole provider for all telecommunications. No competition
- **Mobile data:** Available but expensive relative to Cuban salaries ($7 for 1GB when average salary is ~$30/month)
- **Wi-Fi hotspots:** Public Wi-Fi in parks and plazas ($1-2/hour). Requires ETECSA account
- **Home internet:** Nauta Hogar home broadband expanding but still limited
- **Speeds:** Very slow by global standards. 1-5 Mbps typical

## Censorship

- **Social media:** Has been shut down during protests (July 2021 protests led to multi-day social media blackout)
- **News:** Independent media blocked
- **VPN blocking:** Government blocks VPN services periodically
- **Surveillance:** All ETECSA traffic is monitored

## VPN Preparation

Cuba requires maximum preparation:
1. Download multiple VPN apps before arriving
2. Have Psiphon (specifically designed for censorship circumvention) as backup
3. Download manual configuration files
4. Prepay for VPN — no international payment infrastructure in Cuba
5. Consider a Cuban eSIM or Cubacel SIM for data access

## Our Testing for Cuba

- **FastestVPN** most reliable (6/10) — works through Cuban restrictions when available
- **Psiphon** (free, designed for censorship) — good backup option
- Internet speeds are so slow that VPN overhead is noticeable
- During shutdown periods, no VPN can help if the internet itself is off
