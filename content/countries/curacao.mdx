## Internet Freedom & Privacy Landscape

Curacao has not been separately assessed by Freedom House, but as a constituent country of the Kingdom of the Netherlands, it operates under Dutch constitutional protections for freedom of expression and communication privacy. The primary ISPs are UTS (United Telecommunication Services), Flow (Liberty Latin America), and Digicel, providing 4G LTE and fiber broadband services across the island. Internet penetration exceeds 80%, with strong coverage in Willemstad, Otrobanda, and throughout the island's developed areas. Curacao's significant online gaming industry has driven substantial investment in data center infrastructure and international bandwidth capacity.

## VPN Legality

VPN use is fully legal in Curacao, governed by Dutch law which strongly protects privacy rights and encrypted communications. The Netherlands' robust data protection framework extends to Curacao, and there are no restrictions whatsoever on VPN technology for personal or business use.

## Server Coverage & Speed Performance

No major VPN provider operates servers directly in Curacao, though the island's robust data center infrastructure could support them. The nearest server locations are in the US (Miami, approximately 1,700 km away) and the Netherlands (Amsterdam). Miami servers deliver latency of 35-55ms via Caribbean submarine cables, while Amsterdam connections offer 120-150ms. For streaming Dutch content, Amsterdam routing is necessary despite higher latency. For general performance, Miami provides the fastest experience. NordVPN and Surfshark maintain extensive server fleets in both locations, giving Curacao users flexible options depending on their needs.

## Best VPN Protocols for Curacao

WireGuard is the recommended protocol for Curacao, delivering maximum throughput on UTS, Flow, and Digicel's modern networks. The island's strong infrastructure supports high-speed VPN connections without significant bottlenecks. OpenVPN (UDP) provides a reliable alternative for users requiring specific compliance configurations, particularly those in the online gaming industry with regulatory requirements. Since Curacao operates under Dutch law with no VPN restrictions or traffic inspection, obfuscation is entirely unnecessary and users should prioritize pure speed protocols.
