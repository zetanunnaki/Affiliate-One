## Denmark: Scandinavian Digital Excellence

Denmark consistently ranks among the most digitally advanced countries in the world. Nearly cashless, highly connected, and with universal high-speed broadband, Denmark offers an ideal environment for remote work. Copenhagen's tech scene rivals Stockholm and Helsinki, and the country's digital government infrastructure means nearly every public service is online — making digital privacy all the more consequential.

## Danish Internet and Privacy

Denmark balances strong digital infrastructure with Scandinavian privacy values. However, as an EU member, Danish ISPs are subject to data retention requirements under the Logningsbekendtgoerelsen (Session Logging Order), which mandates that ISPs retain connection metadata for up to one year. The Danish intelligence service PET (Politiets Efterretningstjeneste) and military intelligence FE have broad surveillance capabilities, and Denmark has been implicated in NSA-related surveillance controversies, including the 2021 revelations that FE helped the NSA spy on European politicians.

Danish users benefit from GDPR protections enforced by the Danish Data Protection Agency (Datatilsynet), which has been particularly active in enforcement actions against tech companies. But a VPN adds a critical privacy layer that prevents ISP-level monitoring of your browsing activity, something GDPR alone cannot accomplish.

## Why Use a VPN in Denmark

- **Data retention:** Danish ISPs retain connection data under national and EU requirements, and this data is accessible to law enforcement with minimal judicial oversight
- **Intelligence sharing:** Denmark participates in the Nine Eyes intelligence alliance, meaning surveillance data may be shared with the US, UK, Canada, Australia, and other partner nations
- **MobilePay everywhere:** Denmark's nearly cashless economy — where MobilePay dominates transactions — means nearly every purchase creates a digital footprint. A VPN protects the browsing and research activity surrounding your financial decisions
- **Excellent public Wi-Fi:** Copenhagen, Aarhus, and Odense have extensive free Wi-Fi networks in libraries, public spaces, and cafes. All are shared and unencrypted
- **DSB trains:** Danish State Railways offers Wi-Fi on intercity and S-tog services, but it is shared among all passengers and lacks encryption

## Remote Work in Denmark

Denmark has one of Europe's highest rates of remote and hybrid work. Co-working spaces like Talent Garden, Republikken, and Founders House in Copenhagen serve a growing freelance and startup community. Internet speeds on typical Danish broadband plans from TDC, Norlys, or Stofa range from 300 Mbps to 1 Gbps for 200-400 DKK per month. For digital nomads, the cost of living is high, but the infrastructure is impeccable.
