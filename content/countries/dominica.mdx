## Internet Freedom & Privacy Landscape

Dominica has not been assessed by Freedom House for internet freedom, but the island nation generally maintains an unrestricted internet environment without government censorship or content filtering. The telecommunications market is served by two main providers: Flow (owned by Liberty Latin America, formerly Cable & Wireless) and Digicel. Flow provides both fixed-line broadband and mobile services, while Digicel competes primarily in mobile communications. Following the devastation of Hurricane Maria in 2017, significant infrastructure rebuilding has improved connectivity in Roseau and Portsmouth, though mountainous interior areas and remote eco-tourism zones still experience limited or satellite-only access. As a small island developing state, Dominica's international bandwidth is delivered via submarine cable, making it susceptible to disruption during hurricane season.

## VPN Legality

VPNs are completely legal in Dominica. There are no restrictions on VPN use, and both residents and visitors can freely use VPN services for personal and business purposes.

## Server Coverage & Speed Performance

No VPN providers operate servers in Dominica. The nearest available server locations are in the US (Miami), other Caribbean islands, and South America. Miami servers provide the best latency for Dominican users at approximately 40-60ms from Roseau. Flow broadband subscribers can expect VPN speeds of 15-40 Mbps depending on their plan and time of day, while Digicel mobile users will see more modest throughput of 5-15 Mbps. During hurricane season, international connectivity may experience disruptions that affect VPN reliability regardless of provider choice.

## Best VPN Protocols for Dominica

WireGuard is the recommended protocol for Dominica, offering the best speed efficiency on the island's submarine cable-dependent connections. For mobile users moving between Wi-Fi hotspots and Digicel cellular coverage, IKEv2 provides seamless handoff without dropping connections. OpenVPN serves as a reliable fallback when network conditions are unstable during tropical weather events.
