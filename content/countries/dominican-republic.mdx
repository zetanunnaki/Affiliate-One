## Internet Freedom & Privacy Landscape

The Dominican Republic scores 62/100 on Freedom House's Freedom on the Net index, placing it in the "Partly Free" category. While the country generally maintains an open internet without systematic censorship, the government has expanded digital surveillance capabilities in recent years. The 2018 Law on Cybercrime and the Organic Law on Intelligence grant authorities broad powers to monitor online communications, often without adequate judicial oversight or transparency.

Local ISPs: Altice Dominicana (formerly Orange), Claro Dominicana, and Viva (Trilogy International Partners) control the telecommunications market. Altice holds the largest share of fixed broadband subscribers, while Claro leads in mobile data coverage across rural areas. ISPs comply with government data requests from INDOTEL (Instituto Dominicano de las Telecomunicaciones) and law enforcement agencies, retaining subscriber browsing metadata for extended periods.

## VPN Legality

VPNs are fully legal in the Dominican Republic. There are no laws prohibiting the use of VPNs, encryption tools, or privacy software. The government does not block VPN protocols or interfere with VPN connections. Given the large tourism and expat community, VPN usage is common and accepted without any legal risk.

## Best VPN Protocols for Dominican Republic

WireGuard is the recommended protocol for the Dominican Republic, providing the best speeds through Miami hub servers on both Altice fiber and Claro mobile networks. For resort environments where network administrators occasionally implement restrictive firewalls, OpenVPN over TCP port 443 provides reliable connectivity that cannot be distinguished from regular HTTPS browsing traffic. IKEv2 is optimal for mobile users moving between cellular and Wi-Fi networks throughout resort complexes.
