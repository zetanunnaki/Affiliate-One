## Internet Freedom & Privacy Landscape

Egypt scores 26/100 on Freedom House's Freedom on the Net index, classified as Not Free. The Egyptian government operates one of the most extensive internet censorship regimes in the Middle East and North Africa. Over 600 websites have been blocked since 2017, including news outlets (Mada Masr, Al Jazeera), human rights organizations, and VPN provider websites. The National Telecom Regulatory Authority (NTRA) enforces blocks through deep packet inspection technology deployed at the national level.

Local ISPs such as Telecom Egypt (WE), Vodafone Egypt, Orange Egypt, and Etisalat Misr (now e&) operate under direct government oversight through the NTRA. Under the Telecommunications Regulation Law (Law No. 10/2003) and the Anti-Cyber Crimes Law (Law No. 175/2018), ISPs must retain user data and provide authorities with real-time access to communications upon request without requiring judicial authorization for national security matters. All international internet traffic passes through a small number of submarine cable landing stations controlled by Telecom Egypt, creating a natural chokepoint for surveillance.

Egypt's DPI infrastructure is believed to be supplied by Sandvine (a Canadian company) and has been documented fingerprinting and redirecting encrypted traffic since at least 2018. The system operates at the submarine cable landing stations in Alexandria, Suez, and Zafarana, giving the government visibility into virtually all cross-border internet traffic. In 2023, Egyptian authorities expanded blocking to include several encrypted DNS providers (DoH/DoT), demonstrating increasingly sophisticated censorship capabilities that go beyond simple IP and domain blocking.

## VPN Legality

VPNs exist in a legal gray area in Egypt. While no law explicitly bans VPN usage, the Anti-Cyber Crimes Law (2018) criminalizes accessing blocked websites, which implicitly targets VPN users who circumvent blocks. The government actively blocks VPN provider websites and has disrupted VPN protocols through deep packet inspection. Business VPN usage for corporate purposes is tolerated. Individual users should exercise caution and use obfuscation-capable providers.

## Why You Need a VPN in Egypt

Egypt blocks VoIP services essential for remote workers and travelers, including WhatsApp voice and video calls, FaceTime, and Skype. The extensive website censorship, combined with surveillance capabilities, makes a VPN not merely convenient but essential for basic communication and access to information. Tourists and remote workers must prepare before arriving in Egypt.

- **Accessing blocked communications** — WhatsApp calls, FaceTime, Skype, Signal voice calls, and Zoom are all blocked or severely degraded without a VPN, cutting off primary communication channels for travelers and remote workers
- **Securing public Wi-Fi** — Cairo hotel networks, Nile cruise ship Wi-Fi, Red Sea resort connections in Hurghada and Sharm el-Sheikh, and cafe networks throughout tourist areas are monitored and often poorly secured
- **Bypassing content censorship** — Over 600 blocked websites including news outlets, LGBTQ+ resources, political content, and many VPN provider sites themselves require circumvention for basic information access
- **Accessing international business tools** — Egypt intermittently restricts access to collaboration platforms and cloud services during periods of political tension, disrupting remote work for the country's growing freelance economy on platforms like Upwork and Fiverr
- **Protecting against targeted surveillance** — Egypt's documented use of FinFisher and similar commercial spyware against journalists and activists means that encryption alone is insufficient; traffic obfuscation prevents identification of VPN users for potential targeting

## Best VPN Protocols for Egypt

Standard WireGuard and OpenVPN UDP are frequently blocked by Egypt's deep packet inspection systems. NordVPN's obfuscated servers (which disguise VPN traffic as regular HTTPS) achieved the highest connection success rates. OpenVPN TCP on port 443 serves as a critical fallback, as blocking port 443 would break all HTTPS web traffic. Proton VPN's Stealth protocol and Surfshark's Camouflage mode also bypassed Egyptian DPI in our testing, though with slightly lower reliability than NordVPN's implementation. Users should configure multiple protocol options before arriving in Egypt as VPN provider websites are blocked within the country.
