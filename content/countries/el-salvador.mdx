## Internet Freedom & Privacy Landscape

El Salvador scores 55/100 on the Freedom House "Freedom on the Net" index, classified as "Partly Free" with significant concerns about government surveillance, spyware deployment against journalists, and the erosion of press freedom under the current administration. The telecommunications market is served by Tigo (Millicom), Claro (America Movil), and Movistar (Telefonica), with broadband primarily available in San Salvador and major urban centers. The government's adoption of Bitcoin as legal tender has driven digital infrastructure investment, but has also been accompanied by documented use of Pegasus spyware against journalists and civil society organizations, making El Salvador one of Central America's most concerning environments for digital privacy.

## VPN Legality

VPN usage is technically legal in El Salvador with no specific laws prohibiting encryption tools. However, the government's expanded surveillance powers under the ongoing state of exception and documented spyware deployment create an environment where digital privacy tools face an ambiguous political climate despite their legal status.
