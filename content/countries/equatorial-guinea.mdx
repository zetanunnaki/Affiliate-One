## Internet Freedom & Privacy Landscape

Equatorial Guinea scores 14 out of 100 on the Freedom House Freedom on the Net index, classifying it as "Not Free" â€” one of the most restrictive internet environments in Africa. The government exercises extensive control over telecommunications infrastructure and has been documented blocking websites, restricting social media, and conducting surveillance on citizens. The market is controlled by GETESA (state-owned telecommunications company) and HiTs Telecom (GEOSnet), with GETESA maintaining a near-monopoly on international bandwidth. Internet penetration is extremely low despite the country's oil wealth, concentrated primarily in Malabo (Bioko Island) and Bata (mainland). The regime of President Teodoro Obiang, Africa's longest-serving head of state, uses control of communications infrastructure as a tool to suppress dissent and monitor opposition activity.

## VPN Legality

VPNs are not explicitly illegal in Equatorial Guinea, but the heavily controlled internet environment means authorities may view VPN use with suspicion. The government's extensive surveillance apparatus and control over GETESA mean VPN traffic may be identified and potentially throttled, though enforcement against individual users is not widely documented.

## Server Coverage & Speed Performance

No VPN providers operate servers in Equatorial Guinea. The nearest available servers are in Cameroon, Nigeria (Lagos), and Spain (Madrid, important due to colonial and linguistic ties). ## Best VPN Protocols for Equatorial Guinea

Obfuscated protocols are essential in Equatorial Guinea's heavily controlled internet environment. Standard VPN protocols may be identified and throttled or blocked by GETESA's deep packet inspection capabilities. NordVPN's obfuscated servers, Surfshark's Camouflage mode, and Proton VPN's Stealth protocol all disguise VPN traffic as regular HTTPS, making detection significantly more difficult. Users should configure obfuscation by default and pre-install all VPN software before entering the country.
