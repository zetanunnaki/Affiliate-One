## Internet Freedom & Privacy Landscape

Eritrea scores a devastating 4 out of 100 on Freedom House's Freedom on the Net index, making it the least free internet environment in Africa and among the worst globally. The government maintains absolute control over telecommunications through EriTel, the sole state-owned provider operating all fixed-line, mobile, and internet services. Internet penetration remains below 7% — one of the lowest rates worldwide — with access largely restricted to Asmara and a handful of secondary cities. The government deliberately limits infrastructure expansion to maintain information control. Independent media is entirely banned, social media platforms face persistent blocking, and the regime conducts extensive surveillance of all digital communications. Eritrea has been called the "North Korea of Africa" for its information blackout policies.

## VPN Legality

There is no specific legislation addressing VPN use in Eritrea, but the authoritarian government's approach to information control makes using any circumvention tool inherently risky. EriTel actively blocks VPN protocols through deep packet inspection, and security services monitor internet cafes (the primary access point for most users). Foreign diplomats, UN personnel, and aid workers use VPNs regularly, but Eritrean nationals caught using circumvention tools face potential detention. Users should configure VPN software before entering the country, as VPN provider websites are blocked.

## Why You Need a VPN in Eritrea

In Eritrea's surveillance state, a VPN represents the difference between having access to independent information and being trapped within the government's information bubble. The regime blocks virtually all independent news and communication platforms.

- **Access blocked platforms** — Social media, independent news sites, and messaging apps including WhatsApp are routinely blocked; a VPN is the only way to reach these services from within Eritrea
- **Protect against state surveillance** — EriTel monitors all unencrypted traffic on behalf of security services, and individuals have been imprisoned based on intercepted communications
- **Secure sensitive communications** — Journalists, activists, and diaspora contacts require encrypted channels that cannot be intercepted at the ISP level, making VPN encryption critical for personal safety

## Server Coverage & Speed Performance

No VPN provider operates servers in Eritrea, and the country's isolation means the nearest reliable server locations are in Kenya, the UAE, or Saudi Arabia. EriTel's infrastructure delivers painfully slow speeds — typically 0.5-3 Mbps on the best available connections in Asmara's internet cafes. VPN overhead reduces already minimal throughput by 30-50%, often resulting in sub-1 Mbps effective speeds. Latency to Kenyan or UAE servers ranges from 150-300ms. Mobile data on EriTel's 3G network (where available) rarely exceeds 1 Mbps. Users should expect VPN connections suitable for text-based communication and basic web browsing, not streaming or large downloads.

## Best VPN Protocols for Eritrea

EriTel deploys DPI technology to identify and block standard VPN protocols. Standard OpenVPN and WireGuard handshakes are detected and terminated. Obfuscated protocols are mandatory for any chance of reliable connectivity. NordVPN's obfuscated servers, Surfshark's Camouflage Mode, and Proton VPN's Stealth protocol wrap VPN traffic in TLS encryption indistinguishable from normal HTTPS browsing. Users must pre-install apps and download manual OpenVPN configuration files (obfuscated) before entering the country, as no VPN website is accessible from within Eritrea.
