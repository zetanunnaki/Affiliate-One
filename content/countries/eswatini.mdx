## Internet Freedom & Privacy Landscape

Eswatini (formerly Swaziland) has not received a formal Freedom House internet freedom score, but the country has experienced documented internet shutdowns and social media blocks during pro-democracy protests, most significantly in June-July 2021 when authorities restricted connectivity as citizens demanded political reform. The telecommunications sector is served by MTN Eswatini (the dominant provider) and Swazi Mobile (Eswatini Mobile). Internet penetration is approximately 47%, relatively high for the region, with mobile data as the primary access method. Coverage is concentrated in Mbabane (administrative capital), Manzini (commercial hub), and the Ezulwini Valley corridor between them. As Africa's last absolute monarchy, the government maintains significant control over communications infrastructure.

## VPN Legality

VPNs are technically legal in Eswatini. No specific legislation prohibits their use or installation. However, during the 2021 protests, authorities ordered ISPs to block social media and messaging platforms, and some VPN services were also targeted. Outside of active political crises, VPN usage is unrestricted.

## Best VPN Protocols for Eswatini

Standard protocols work without interference during normal conditions. WireGuard provides the best performance for everyday use. However, during political crises when authorities have blocked VPN traffic, obfuscated protocols become necessary. Users should pre-configure NordVPN's obfuscated servers or Surfshark's Camouflage Mode as fallback options in case of future shutdown events. Having manual OpenVPN configuration files on TCP port 443 provides an additional backup.
