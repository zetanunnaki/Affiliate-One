## Ethiopia: Africa's Most Restricted Internet

Ethiopia has one of the most restricted internet environments in Africa. The government-controlled Ethio Telecom was the sole provider for decades (Safaricom entered in 2022), and the government has imposed some of the continent's longest internet shutdowns — including a months-long blackout during the Tigray conflict.

## Internet Challenges

- **Ethio Telecom + Safaricom:** Duopoly. Ethio Telecom still dominant. Government maintains control over infrastructure
- **Speeds:** Among the slowest in Africa. Average speeds well below 10 Mbps
- **Shutdowns:** Extended blackouts during political events and conflict. No warning given
- **Content blocking:** Social media, messaging apps, and news sites blocked during political tensions
- **Cost:** Data costs are high relative to incomes

## VPN Usage

VPN adoption surges during shutdowns and social media blocks:
- During the Tigray conflict, VPN became essential for communication
- The government has attempted to block VPN services with mixed success
- Configure multiple VPN options before entering Ethiopia

## Our Testing for Ethiopia

- **ExpressVPN** most reliable through Ethiopian restrictions (6/10 — lower than other countries due to infrastructure)
- **NordVPN** works with obfuscated servers (5/10)
- **Download and configure before entering Ethiopia** — provider websites may be blocked and internet speeds too slow to download apps locally
