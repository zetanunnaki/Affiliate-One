## Internet Freedom & Privacy Landscape

The Faroe Islands are a self-governing territory within the Kingdom of Denmark, comprising 18 islands in the North Atlantic between Norway, Iceland, and Scotland. Denmark scores 95 out of 100 on Freedom House's Freedom on the Net index, among the highest globally, and this liberal framework extends to the Faroes. Faroese Telecom (Foroya Tele) is the primary ISP, complemented by smaller providers. Despite the remote location, the territory boasts remarkably strong broadband infrastructure thanks to submarine cables connecting to Denmark, Iceland, and the UK. Internet penetration exceeds 95% across the approximately 54,000 population, with fiber optic availability in most towns. The Faroes fall outside the EU (and therefore technically outside GDPR), but Danish data protection standards apply through the kingdom's legal framework.

## VPN Legality

VPNs are entirely legal in the Faroe Islands. Danish and Faroese law places no restrictions on encryption tools, privacy software, or VPN usage. The territory's liberal approach to digital rights means residents and visitors can use any VPN service without concern.

## Why You Need a VPN in Faroe Islands

The Faroe Islands present no censorship issues, but their unique geographic and jurisdictional position creates genuine VPN use cases for the local population and visitors.

- **Access Danish and EU streaming content** — Being outside the EU means some services restrict Faroese IP addresses differently than Danish ones; connecting to Copenhagen servers provides full access to Danish content libraries
- **Remote work security** — The growing number of remote workers and digital nomads attracted by the Faroes' stunning landscapes need secure connections to corporate networks across Europe
- **Privacy on shared island networks** — Small communities and shared infrastructure in villages mean local network administrators could theoretically observe traffic; VPN encryption ensures privacy

## Server Coverage & Speed Performance

No VPN provider maintains servers in the Faroe Islands specifically. The nearest locations are Denmark (Copenhagen), Iceland (Reykjavik), Norway (Oslo), and the UK (London). Submarine cable connections to Denmark provide excellent performance, with latency to Copenhagen servers averaging 25-40ms. Iceland servers offer similar latency at 20-35ms. Baseline Faroese broadband speeds reach 50-200 Mbps on fiber connections, with VPN overhead reducing throughput by only 5-15% to Nordic servers. The FARICE and SHEFA submarine cables provide redundant routing, ensuring reliable connectivity even if one path experiences issues.

## Best VPN Protocols for Faroe Islands

No protocol restrictions exist in the Faroes, so users should select based on performance and use case. WireGuard provides the fastest speeds, ideal for streaming and downloads over the submarine cable links. OpenVPN (UDP) offers broader compatibility for corporate VPN configurations. IKEv2 handles the frequent transitions between home fiber and mobile data (via Foroya Tele's 4G network) that occur when moving between islands on the Faroese ferry system.
