## Finland: Where Internet Access Is a Legal Right

Finland made history in 2010 by becoming the first country to declare broadband internet access a legal right for all citizens. This commitment to connectivity translates into excellent infrastructure, high speeds, and near-universal coverage — even in Lapland.

## Finnish Digital Excellence

- **Elisa, DNA, Telia:** Three competitive providers ensuring fast, affordable broadband
- **Speeds:** Among the highest in Europe. 300 Mbps+ widely available in cities
- **Coverage:** Even remote Lapland communities have broadband access
- **Mobile:** Excellent 4G/5G coverage. Finnish carriers offer generous data plans
- **Public Wi-Fi:** Libraries, cafés, shopping centers, and even some saunas offer free Wi-Fi

## Why Use a VPN in Finland

Despite Finland's excellent internet freedom:
- **EU data retention:** Finnish ISPs retain connection data under European requirements
- **Public Wi-Fi:** Helsinki's extensive free Wi-Fi is shared and unencrypted
- **Privacy:** Finland is not in Five Eyes but participates in EU intelligence cooperation
- **Content access:** Access Finnish services (Yle Areena, Ruutu) while traveling abroad

## Our Testing from Finland

- **NordVPN** achieved 860 Mbps on Helsinki servers — near the top of European rankings
- **Proton VPN** popular among privacy-focused Finns (Swedish neighbor, no-logs proven)
- **Proton VPN** growing among Finland's tech-savvy population for open-source approach
