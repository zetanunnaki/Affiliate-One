## Internet Freedom & Privacy Landscape

French Polynesia is a French overseas collectivity that operates under French law regarding internet governance, privacy, and telecommunications regulation. The internet is unrestricted and benefits from French and EU-influenced data protection standards. The telecommunications market is dominated by OPT (Office des Postes et Telecommunications), the public monopoly operator that provides both fixed-line broadband and mobile services under the Vini brand. While OPT has faced criticism for limited competition and relatively high prices, it has invested in submarine cable infrastructure connecting Tahiti to Hawaii and New Zealand, improving international bandwidth. Connectivity is strong on Tahiti and other Society Islands (Bora Bora, Moorea, Raiatea) but degrades significantly on more remote Tuamotu atolls, Marquesas Islands, and Gambier Islands, where satellite connections are often the only option.

## VPN Legality

VPNs are fully legal in French Polynesia. As a French territory, internet governance follows French law, and there are no restrictions on VPN use or encrypted communications.

## Server Coverage & Speed Performance

No VPN providers maintain servers in French Polynesia. The nearest server locations are in New Zealand (Auckland), Australia (Sydney), and the United States (Los Angeles/Hawaii). Due to submarine cable routing, US West Coast servers (Los Angeles) often provide the best latency at 70-100ms from Tahiti, while Auckland servers offer 50-80ms via the Manatua cable. OPT fiber subscribers on Tahiti can expect VPN speeds of 20-50 Mbps, sufficient for HD streaming through encrypted tunnels. Remote atolls relying on satellite connections will experience much higher latency (500ms+) regardless of VPN server choice, making real-time applications challenging.

## Best VPN Protocols for French Polynesia

WireGuard is strongly recommended for French Polynesia, where the inherent latency of transpacific connections means protocol efficiency significantly impacts perceived performance. WireGuard's minimal handshake overhead and stateless design handle high-latency connections more gracefully than OpenVPN. For users on remote atolls with satellite internet, WireGuard's efficient reconnection is particularly valuable when dealing with intermittent connectivity.
