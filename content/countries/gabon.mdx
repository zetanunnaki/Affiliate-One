## Internet Freedom & Privacy Landscape

Gabon has not been formally assessed by Freedom House for internet freedom, but the country has a documented history of government-ordered internet shutdowns during politically sensitive events, most notably during the 2016 presidential election crisis and the 2023 military coup. The telecommunications market is served by three major mobile operators: Airtel Gabon (Bharti Airtel), Moov Africa (Maroc Telecom), and Libertis (Gabon Telecom, state-affiliated). Airtel holds the largest mobile subscriber base, while Gabon Telecom dominates fixed-line infrastructure. Internet access is concentrated in Libreville, Port-Gentil, and Franceville, with the vast equatorial rainforest interior having minimal or no connectivity. French is the official language and most online activity connects to French-language services.

## VPN Legality

VPNs are legal in Gabon. There are no specific laws prohibiting their use, though internet access itself may be restricted during government-imposed shutdowns that affect all traffic including VPN connections.

## Server Coverage & Speed Performance

No VPN providers maintain servers in Gabon. The closest server locations include South Africa (Johannesburg), Nigeria (Lagos), and France (Paris, culturally relevant due to colonial ties). Paris servers provide access to French content and services at approximately 100-130ms latency, while South African servers offer lower latency at 60-80ms for general browsing. Airtel 4G users in Libreville can expect VPN speeds of 8-20 Mbps during off-peak hours, with performance degrading during evening peak times when network congestion is highest.
