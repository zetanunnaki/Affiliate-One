## Internet Freedom & Privacy Landscape

Gambia scores 50 out of 100 on Freedom House's Freedom on the Net index, placing it in the "Partly Free" category. The country's internet freedom reputation was severely damaged by the complete internet shutdown ordered by former President Jammeh during the December 2016 presidential election â€” a three-week blackout that drew international condemnation. Under the Barrow government since 2017, conditions have improved but concerns remain around potential backsliding. The telecommunications market is served by Africell (the market leader), Qcell, and Comium, providing mobile data services that account for nearly all internet access. Internet penetration is approximately 40%, concentrated in the Greater Banjul area and the coastal tourism zone. Fixed broadband is virtually nonexistent for consumers.

## VPN Legality

VPNs are legal in Gambia. The post-Jammeh government has not enacted legislation restricting VPN usage or encrypted communications. However, given the country's precedent of total internet shutdowns, the legal environment could shift during political crises. VPN usage is openly practiced without prosecution.

## Server Coverage & Speed Performance

No VPN providers operate servers inside Gambia. The nearest options are Senegal (Dakar, which essentially neighbors Gambia geographically), with other choices including France (Paris) and the UK (London). Africell 4G delivers 8-20 Mbps in the Greater Banjul area, with VPN connections to Dakar adding just 20-35ms latency due to the geographic proximity. Given Gambia's English-speaking population, UK servers are popular for accessing British streaming content and services.
