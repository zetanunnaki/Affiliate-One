## Internet Freedom & Privacy Landscape

Georgia scores 60/100 on Freedom House's Freedom on the Net index, classified as Partly Free. While the internet remains largely uncensored, the country has experienced concerning developments in surveillance legislation. The 2017 amendments to the Law on Electronic Communications expanded the State Security Service's authority to conduct bulk metadata collection without judicial oversight. The controversial "foreign agents" law passed in 2024 raised international alarm about press freedom and civil society monitoring.

Major ISPs include Magticom, Silknet (formerly Geocell), and Beeline Georgia. The market is competitive with affordable 100-500 Mbps fiber available in Tbilisi and Batumi for $8-15/month. Georgia's internet infrastructure routes primarily through Turkey and submarine cables in the Black Sea, with secondary routes through Azerbaijan and Russia. ISPs are legally required to retain metadata for two years and provide authorities with access to communications data upon request from the State Security Service.

## VPN Legality

VPNs are fully legal in Georgia with no restrictions on usage, downloads, or provider access. There are no known cases of individuals being prosecuted for VPN use. The government does not block VPN protocols or provider websites. Business and personal use are both unrestricted, making Georgia one of the most VPN-friendly countries in the Caucasus region.

## Why You Need a VPN in Georgia

Georgia's open internet and thriving digital nomad scene might suggest a VPN is unnecessary, but several factors make one valuable for long-term residents and visitors alike.

- **Protecting remote work data** — Georgia's affordable co-working spaces and cafés (Terminal, Impact Hub, Loft Tbilisi) offer open Wi-Fi networks that lack enterprise-grade security, putting client data and corporate credentials at risk for the thousands of remote workers based in Tbilisi and Batumi
- **Countering metadata surveillance** — The State Security Service's bulk metadata collection capability means your browsing patterns, communication metadata, and connection logs are retained by ISPs for two years without your knowledge or consent
- **Accessing geo-restricted services** — Digital nomads in Georgia often need to access home-country banking portals, streaming libraries, and professional services that block Georgian IP addresses or trigger fraud alerts from unfamiliar locations

## Best VPN Protocols for Georgia

WireGuard is the optimal protocol for Georgia given the absence of DPI-based blocking or protocol interference. The protocol delivers maximum speed on Georgia's fast fiber infrastructure with minimal overhead. OpenVPN UDP serves as a reliable fallback for compatibility with older devices. IKEv2 performs well on mobile connections when switching between Magticom/Silknet cellular networks and Wi-Fi in Tbilisi's many café workspaces. Users traveling to neighboring countries with restrictions (Turkey, Russia, Azerbaijan) should pre-configure obfuscated server connections before crossing borders.
