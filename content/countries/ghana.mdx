## Internet Freedom & Privacy Landscape

Ghana scores 70/100 on Freedom House's Freedom on the Net index, placing it in the "Free" category and making it one of the highest-rated countries in Sub-Saharan Africa for internet freedom. The country has a strong democratic tradition and generally respects online speech. However, the Cybersecurity Act of 2020 grants law enforcement broad surveillance powers, and the Electronic Communications Act requires ISPs to cooperate with government data requests without always requiring court orders.

Local ISPs: MTN Ghana, Vodafone Ghana, and AirtelTigo (now consolidated under AT Ghana) dominate both mobile and fixed broadband. MTN holds approximately 55% market share for mobile data. Fixed broadband penetration remains low at around 4%, with most internet access occurring via mobile networks. ISPs maintain records of subscriber activity and comply with National Communications Authority (NCA) data requests.

## VPN Legality

VPNs are fully legal in Ghana. The government does not restrict VPN usage, and there are no laws prohibiting encryption tools or privacy software. Ghana's strong press freedom tradition extends to digital communications, though the government did briefly throttle social media during the 2024 elections before public pressure reversed the decision.

## Best VPN Protocols for Ghana

WireGuard is strongly recommended for Ghana due to its low overhead, which maximizes throughput on bandwidth-constrained international links. The lightweight protocol also reduces battery drain on mobile devices, which is important given that most Ghanaians access internet via smartphones. IKEv2 serves as an excellent alternative for mobile users who switch between MTN and Vodafone networks, as it handles network transitions seamlessly without dropping connections.
