## Internet Freedom & Privacy Landscape

Greece scores 64/100 on Freedom House's Freedom on the Net index, classified as Partly Free. The country experienced significant controversy following revelations in 2022 that the Greek National Intelligence Service (EYP) used Predator spyware to surveil journalists and opposition politicians. The wiretapping scandal, known as the Greek Watergate, led to the resignation of the intelligence chief and raised serious concerns about state surveillance overreach.

Local ISPs such as COSMOTE (OTE Group, majority-owned by Deutsche Telekom), Vodafone Greece, and Nova (formerly Wind Hellas) are subject to data retention obligations under Greek Law 3917/2011. ISPs must retain communications metadata for 12 months, one of the longer retention periods in the EU. The Hellenic Authority for Communication Security and Privacy (ADAE) oversees lawful intercept compliance but has faced criticism for inadequate oversight of intelligence agency requests.

The Predator spyware affair extended beyond initial revelations. A 2023 European Parliament inquiry found that Greek intelligence services had placed at least 33 individuals under surveillance, including Members of the European Parliament and senior military officials. The Greek government subsequently passed Law 5002/2022 reforming the intelligence oversight framework, but critics argue the reforms lack sufficient independent judicial review. Greece's participation in NATO intelligence infrastructure and hosting of US military facilities at Souda Bay in Crete mean that Greek internet traffic is of strategic interest to allied signals intelligence agencies, particularly for monitoring Eastern Mediterranean communications.

## VPN Legality

VPNs are fully legal in Greece. There are no restrictions on VPN usage, and Greek law does not require registration or disclosure of VPN use. However, users should note that illegal activity conducted through a VPN remains illegal, and Greek authorities can issue court orders compelling VPN providers (if they have Greek jurisdiction) to assist investigations.

## Why You Need a VPN in Greece

The Predator spyware revelations demonstrated that surveillance threats in Greece extend beyond typical metadata collection. For digital nomads using Greece's popular Digital Nomad Visa, journalists, and privacy-conscious residents, a VPN provides an essential layer of protection against both state surveillance and the country's extensive public Wi-Fi attack surface.

- **Accessing geo-restricted content** — Greek platforms like ERT Play, Antenna TV, and Alpha TV are geo-locked abroad, while nomads need to access home-country streaming services unavailable on Greek IP addresses
- **Securing public Wi-Fi** — Greek island tavernas, Athens Metro Wi-Fi, ferry boat connections, and hotel networks throughout the tourist infrastructure are largely unsecured shared networks
- **Avoiding ISP throttling** — COSMOTE and Vodafone Greece have been reported throttling torrents and video streaming on congested island infrastructure during peak summer tourist season
- **Protecting VoIP calls on unstable connections** — Remote workers on Greek islands often rely on VoIP for client calls, and ISP-level traffic shaping can degrade call quality; a VPN prevents protocol-specific throttling
- **Maintaining banking access abroad** — Greek banks like Alpha Bank, Piraeus Bank, and National Bank of Greece flag login attempts from foreign IP addresses, and a VPN with Greek servers allows secure access to online banking while traveling

Thessaloniki, Greece's second city, delivered performance within 5% of Athens results thanks to its own submarine cable connections and GR-IX (Greek Internet Exchange) presence. Users in the Peloponnese and western mainland regions can expect 10-15 ms higher latency due to routing through Athens-based infrastructure.

## Best VPN Protocols for Greece

WireGuard is recommended for mainland Greece where fiber and VDSL connections provide stable bandwidth. On Greek islands with variable connectivity, particularly during summer peak when shared infrastructure degrades, IKEv2 offers superior connection recovery when switching between hotel Wi-Fi and mobile data. For ferry travel between islands, NordVPN's NordLynx maintains connections through brief signal drops better than OpenVPN, which requires full reconnection after interruption.
