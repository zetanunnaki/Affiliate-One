## Internet Freedom & Privacy Landscape

Greenland is an autonomous territory within the Kingdom of Denmark, spanning over 2 million square kilometers with just 56,000 residents — the world's least densely populated territory. Denmark scores 95 out of 100 (Free) on Freedom House's Freedom on the Net index, and Greenland benefits from this liberal framework. Tusass (formerly TELE Greenland/Telepost) is the sole telecommunications provider, operating a monopoly on all fixed, mobile, and internet services across the territory. Nuuk, the capital, has fiber optic connectivity, while coastal towns connect through the Greenland Connect submarine cable system linking to Iceland and Canada. Remote settlements rely on satellite connections with severely limited bandwidth. Internet penetration is approximately 70% in towns connected by the submarine cable, but drops dramatically in isolated communities.

## VPN Legality

VPNs are entirely legal in Greenland under Danish legal framework. There are no restrictions on encryption, privacy tools, or VPN usage. The territory's liberal Scandinavian legal traditions fully support digital privacy rights.

## Why You Need a VPN in Greenland

Greenland's extreme remoteness and monopoly ISP situation create unique privacy and access needs.

- **Privacy from monopoly provider** — With Tusass as the sole ISP, all internet activity passes through a single entity; VPN encryption prevents comprehensive browsing profiling regardless of Greenlandic or Danish data retention policies
- **Access Danish and international content** — Some streaming services treat Greenlandic IP addresses separately from Danish ones due to different content licensing territories; connecting to Copenhagen servers provides full Danish content access
- **Secure satellite connections** — Remote settlements using satellite internet transmit data over radio links more vulnerable to interception; VPN encryption adds protection on inherently less secure satellite paths

## Server Coverage & Speed Performance

No VPN provider operates servers in Greenland. The nearest locations are in Iceland (Reykjavik), Denmark (Copenhagen), and Canada (eastern provinces). The Greenland Connect submarine cable provides primary connectivity to both Iceland and Canada, making Icelandic servers the lowest-latency option at 30-50ms from Nuuk. Copenhagen servers add 60-90ms through the Iceland-to-Europe routing. Canadian servers offer 40-70ms via the western branch of the cable system. Baseline speeds in Nuuk reach 20-100 Mbps on Tusass fiber, while coastal towns on the submarine cable network average 10-30 Mbps. Satellite-dependent settlements experience 500ms+ latency where VPN overhead becomes a significant additional burden, though encryption remains technically functional for basic messaging.

## Best VPN Protocols for Greenland

With no censorship or protocol restrictions, users should optimize for their connection type. WireGuard provides the fastest experience on Nuuk's fiber network, minimizing overhead on submarine cable links. For satellite users in remote settlements, WireGuard's efficiency is even more critical — its lightweight protocol adds less latency overhead than OpenVPN on already high-latency connections. IKEv2 handles transitions between Tusass mobile (available in larger towns) and fixed connections reliably.
