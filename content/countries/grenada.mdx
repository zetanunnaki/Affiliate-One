## Internet Freedom & Privacy Landscape

Grenada has not been assessed by Freedom House's Freedom on the Net report, but the country maintains a fully open and unrestricted internet environment consistent with its democratic governance traditions. The telecommunications market is served by Flow (Liberty Latin America) and Digicel, which provide both mobile and fixed broadband services across the tri-island state of Grenada, Carriacou, and Petite Martinique. Internet penetration is approximately 60%, with a mix of mobile data and fixed broadband connections. St. George's (the capital) and the Grand Anse area have the strongest connectivity, while rural parishes and the smaller islands experience slower speeds. As an English-speaking Caribbean nation, most online content consumed is from the United States and United Kingdom.

## VPN Legality

VPNs are completely legal in Grenada. There are no restrictions on VPN usage, and the government does not engage in any form of internet censorship or VPN blocking. Users can freely install and operate any VPN service.
