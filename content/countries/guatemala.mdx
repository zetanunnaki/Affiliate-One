## Internet Freedom & Privacy Landscape

Guatemala scores 45 out of 100 on Freedom House's Freedom on the Net index, classifying it as "Partly Free." The internet market is served by three major providers — Tigo (Millicom), Claro (América Móvil), and Movistar (Telefónica) — with Tigo holding the largest market share for both mobile and fixed broadband. Concerns include government surveillance capabilities, journalist harassment in digital spaces, and a 2020 cybercrime law that expanded authorities' ability to request user data from ISPs without robust judicial oversight. In 2024, multiple journalists and NGO workers reported receiving Israeli-manufactured Pegasus-style spyware targeting, and SIT (the Special Anti-Impunity Tribunal) confirmed that government agencies had purchased commercial surveillance tools. The regulatory body SIT (Superintendencia de Telecomunicaciones) lacks independence from political pressure, leaving ISP data handling practices with minimal oversight.

## VPN Legality

VPNs are legal and unrestricted in Guatemala. There are no laws prohibiting VPN use for personal or business purposes, and ISPs do not block or throttle VPN protocols. Many businesses and NGOs operating in the country routinely use VPN technology for security.

## Why You Need a VPN in Guatemala

Guatemala's partly-free internet environment, combined with variable infrastructure quality across the country, makes VPN protection particularly important for both visitors and residents. From Antigua's digital nomad cafes to Guatemala City's business district, encrypted connections provide essential security.

- **Cybercrime law and surveillance concerns** — Guatemala's legal framework allows authorities to request connection data from Tigo, Claro, and Movistar with limited judicial oversight; a VPN prevents your ISP from building a browsing profile that could be disclosed
- **Unsafe public networks in tourist areas** — Antigua, Lake Atitlan, and Flores attract travelers who rely on cafe and hostel Wi-Fi that lacks enterprise-grade security, exposing credentials and financial data to local network attacks
- **Journalist and activist protection** — Guatemala's partly-free classification reflects real digital threats including doxxing and surveillance of civil society; VPN encryption adds a critical layer of protection for sensitive communications

## Best VPN Protocols for Guatemala

WireGuard delivers optimal performance on Guatemalan networks, particularly on Tigo's fiber infrastructure where the protocol's efficiency translates directly to speed gains. OpenVPN remains important as a fallback for areas with older network equipment around Lake Atitlan and rural zones where Claro's legacy ADSL connections sometimes drop WireGuard handshakes. Guatemala does not employ DPI or active VPN blocking, so standard protocol configurations work reliably across all three major ISPs. For mobile users on Movistar LTE, IKEv2 handles the frequent tower handoffs between Guatemala City's dense urban cells more gracefully than WireGuard.
