## Internet Freedom & Privacy Landscape

Guinea-Bissau is a small West African nation of approximately 2 million people, marked by political instability including frequent coup attempts and government collapses. Despite chronic governance challenges, the internet remains relatively unregulated — successive governments have lacked both the technical capacity and political will to implement systematic censorship. Orange Bissau and MTN Guinea-Bissau are the primary mobile operators, providing most internet access through 3G and limited 4G services. Internet penetration remains under 15%, among the lowest in West Africa, concentrated in the capital Bissau and a handful of secondary towns. The ACE submarine cable provides international connectivity through a landing point in Bissau, though domestic distribution infrastructure is severely underdeveloped. Portuguese-speaking Guinea-Bissau has no documented history of deliberate internet shutdowns, though service interruptions from infrastructure failures are common.

## VPN Legality

VPNs are legal in Guinea-Bissau. The country lacks specific legislation addressing VPN technology, and the general absence of internet regulation means VPN use faces no restrictions. International organizations, NGOs, and businesses use VPNs freely without any government interference.

## Why You Need a VPN in Guinea-Bissau

Guinea-Bissau's underdeveloped infrastructure and political instability create security concerns that VPN protection helps address.

- **Secure unreliable networks** — Basic network infrastructure with limited security standards means connections are vulnerable to interception; VPN encryption adds protection that the underlying networks cannot guarantee
- **Access Portuguese-language content** — Portuguese streaming services and platforms geo-restrict content to Portugal and Brazil; VPN connections to Lisbon unlock full Portuguese content catalogs for the Lusophone population
- **Protection during political instability** — Guinea-Bissau's history of coups and political crises creates unpredictable periods where communications monitoring increases; VPN encryption provides security during sensitive political transitions

## Server Coverage & Speed Performance

No VPN provider operates servers in Guinea-Bissau. The nearest locations are in Senegal (Dakar), Portugal (Lisbon), and other West African nations. The ACE submarine cable connects Bissau to Europe via the West African coast. Baseline speeds in Bissau average 2-8 Mbps on 3G/4G mobile connections, with limited fixed broadband available. VPN connections to Senegalese servers add 20-40ms latency with 20-35% throughput reduction. Portuguese servers deliver 50-80ms latency, leveraging the direct ACE cable route to Europe — particularly useful for accessing Portuguese-language content. Outside Bissau, connectivity drops to near-unusable levels in most areas.

## Best VPN Protocols for Guinea-Bissau

No censorship or protocol blocking exists in Guinea-Bissau. The priority is minimizing overhead on already severely bandwidth-constrained connections. WireGuard's lightweight design adds minimal latency and preserves maximum throughput on slow 3G connections. IKEv2 provides stable mobile connectivity on Orange and MTN networks with frequent signal drops. OpenVPN (UDP) should be avoided on very slow connections due to higher overhead — WireGuard is strongly preferred in this environment.
