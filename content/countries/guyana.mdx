## Internet Freedom & Privacy Landscape

Guyana has not been formally assessed by Freedom House for internet freedom, but the country generally maintains an unrestricted internet environment without government-imposed censorship or content blocking. The telecommunications market is primarily controlled by two providers: Guyana Telephone & Telegraph Company (GTT), which holds the dominant position in fixed-line broadband and has historically operated as a near-monopoly, and Digicel, which competes strongly in mobile services. The recent oil boom has accelerated infrastructure investment, particularly in Georgetown and coastal areas, though the vast interior and Amazonian regions remain largely unconnected. Internet speeds have improved in recent years but remain modest by international standards.

## VPN Legality

VPNs are fully legal in Guyana. There are no laws or regulations restricting VPN use for personal or business purposes, and the government does not interfere with encrypted communications.

## Server Coverage & Speed Performance

No major VPN providers maintain servers within Guyana. The nearest server locations are in Trinidad and Tobago, Brazil (Sao Paulo), and the United States (Miami). Miami servers typically provide the best overall experience for Guyanese users, with latency of 60-90ms from Georgetown. GTT fiber subscribers in the capital can achieve VPN speeds of 20-40 Mbps, while Digicel mobile users will see more variable performance depending on local tower congestion. Interior regions connected via satellite or microwave links will experience significantly higher latency regardless of VPN server choice.

## Best VPN Protocols for Guyana

WireGuard is strongly recommended for Guyana, where bandwidth conservation matters due to relatively limited connection speeds. The protocol's efficient design minimizes overhead and delivers noticeably better performance than OpenVPN on the same connection. For users on congested mobile networks, Lightway (ExpressVPN) or NordLynx (NordVPN) offer similarly optimized experiences built on WireGuard foundations.
