Honduras has not been formally assessed by Freedom House's Freedom on the Net report, but the country generally maintains an open internet environment with limited content filtering. The telecommunications market is dominated by Tigo (Millicom), Claro (America Movil), and smaller regional providers serving urban centers. Internet penetration hovers around 50%, with mobile data accounting for the majority of connections. ## VPN Legality

VPNs are fully legal in Honduras. There are no restrictions on downloading, installing, or using VPN software. The government does not engage in VPN blocking or protocol interference, making Honduras straightforward for VPN users whether residents or visitors.

## Best VPN Protocols for Honduras

Standard WireGuard and OpenVPN protocols work without issues in Honduras since there is no deep packet inspection or protocol blocking. WireGuard is the recommended choice for its superior speed and lower battery drain on mobile devices. OpenVPN (UDP) serves as a reliable fallback. IKEv2 is also well-suited for mobile users who frequently switch between cellular and Wi-Fi connections in transit.
