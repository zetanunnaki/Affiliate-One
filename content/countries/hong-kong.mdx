## Hong Kong: Changed Landscape Since 2020

Hong Kong's internet landscape has fundamentally changed since the 2020 National Security Law. Once among Asia's freest internet environments, the city now has website blocks, content restrictions, and increased government surveillance capabilities. VPN adoption has surged among residents, businesses, and visitors.

## What's Changed

- **Website blocks:** Some pro-democracy and opposition websites blocked under the NSL
- **Self-censorship:** Media outlets and individuals increasingly self-censor online content
- **Surveillance concerns:** Enhanced government surveillance capabilities under the NSL
- **VPN demand:** VPN downloads spiked 200%+ after the NSL was enacted and remain high
- **Business impact:** International businesses use VPNs for secure communications and data protection

## Still World-Class Infrastructure

Despite political changes, Hong Kong's internet infrastructure remains world-class:
- **Among the fastest speeds globally** — average exceeding 250 Mbps
- **HKT, CMHK, SmarTone, 3HK:** Highly competitive market
- **Wi-Fi everywhere** — MTR stations, cafés, malls, and the free Wi-Fi.HK network
- **5G coverage:** Extensive throughout the territory
