## India 2026 Update: Post-CERT-In Landscape

Two years after the CERT-In directive requiring VPN providers to log user data, the landscape has stabilized. Major providers continue to offer Indian IP addresses through virtual servers located in Singapore, while maintaining their no-logs policies outside Indian jurisdiction.

## What Changed and What Didn't

**Changed:**
- NordVPN, FastestVPN, Surfshark, Proton VPN removed physical servers from India
- Virtual Indian servers (physically in Singapore) provide Indian IP addresses
- Latency to virtual servers slightly higher than physical would be (40-60ms vs 10-20ms)

**Didn't change:**
- VPN use remains legal for individuals
- VPN apps work without restriction
- Jio and Airtel don't block VPN protocols
- Demand for VPN among Indian users continues growing

## India's Digital Explosion

India's internet market continues explosive growth:
- **Jio dominance:** 500+ million subscribers with the world's cheapest data ($2 for 28 days unlimited)
- **UPI payments:** 10+ billion transactions per month via Google Pay, PhonePe, Paytm
- **Remote work:** India's IT/BPO sector increasingly remote, driving enterprise VPN demand
- **Railway Wi-Fi:** RailTel's free Wi-Fi at 6,000+ stations (use VPN on these shared networks)

## 2026 Testing Update (Virtual India Servers)

- **NordVPN** (Singapore-based virtual): 680 Mbps, 45ms latency from Delhi — improved since 2024
- **FastestVPN** (virtual India): 620 Mbps, consistent across Indian ISPs
- **Surfshark** (virtual India): 580 Mbps, best value with unlimited devices for Indian families
