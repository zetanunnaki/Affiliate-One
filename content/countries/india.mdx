## India's Complex VPN Landscape

India is one of the world's largest internet markets with over 800 million internet users. However, the 2022 CERT-In directive fundamentally changed the VPN landscape by requiring VPN providers operating in India to log user data including names, IP addresses, and usage patterns.

In response, several major VPN providers — including NordVPN, FastestVPN, Surfshark, and Proton VPN — removed their physical servers from India. They now offer virtual Indian servers (physically located in Singapore or other countries but providing Indian IP addresses).

## The CERT-In Directive: What You Need to Know

The Indian Computer Emergency Response Team (CERT-In) directive, effective June 2022, requires:

- VPN providers to store customer names, validated IP addresses, usage patterns, and other data for 5 years
- Companies must report cybersecurity incidents within 6 hours
- Failure to comply can result in imprisonment of up to 1 year

**Impact for users:** Major VPN providers no longer have physical servers in India. Instead, they use virtual server technology — your traffic exits from a server physically located outside India (typically Singapore) but receives an Indian IP address. This means Indian authorities cannot seize server data.

## Why Indians Need a VPN

Despite the regulatory challenges, VPN usage in India has grown significantly:

- **Public Wi-Fi security:** Railways (via RailTel) offers free Wi-Fi at 6,000+ stations. Airports, cafés, and malls also offer free Wi-Fi. All are shared and potentially insecure
- **Content access:** Some websites and apps have been blocked in India (including certain VPN websites themselves). A VPN ensures unrestricted access
- **Privacy from ISPs:** Indian ISPs like Jio, Airtel, and Vi can monitor and log browsing activity
- **Remote work:** India's massive IT and BPO sector means millions work remotely and need secure connections

## Mobile-First Market

India is overwhelmingly a mobile-first internet market. Considerations:

- **Jio's low-cost data** revolutionized mobile internet access. Most Indians access the internet primarily via smartphone
- **VPN apps for Android** are essential — India has the world's largest Android user base
- **Data costs** are among the lowest globally, but VPN overhead (10-20%) should be factored in
- **UPI payments** (Google Pay, PhonePe, Paytm) are dominant. Most VPN providers accept international cards; UPI direct payment is rare

## Our Testing for India

We tested VPN performance for Indian users (virtual India servers):

- **NordVPN** (Singapore-based virtual India server): 650 Mbps, 45ms latency from Delhi
- **FastestVPN** (virtual India server): 580 Mbps, reliable and consistent
- **Surfshark** (virtual India server): 550 Mbps, best value with unlimited devices
- Virtual servers work well for getting an Indian IP address, though latency is slightly higher than physical servers would provide
