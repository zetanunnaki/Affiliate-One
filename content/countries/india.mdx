## Internet Freedom & Privacy Landscape

India scores 50/100 on Freedom House's Freedom on the Net index, classified as Partly Free. India is the world's largest internet market by user count with over 850 million internet subscribers, yet faces growing tensions between digital expansion and government control. The Indian government has increasingly used internet shutdowns as a governance tool — India leads the world in internet shutdowns with over 100 instances annually across various states, particularly in Jammu & Kashmir, Rajasthan, and during communal tensions. The Information Technology Act (2000, amended 2008) grants the government broad authority to block websites and order content removal under Section 69A, with blocking orders kept confidential.

Major ISPs including Reliance Jio, Bharti Airtel, Vodafone Idea (Vi), BSNL, and ACT Fibernet operate under the unified license framework of the Department of Telecommunications (DoT). These operators must comply with lawful interception requirements under the Indian Telegraph Act (1885) and IT Act provisions, providing authorities with real-time access to subscriber communications. The Central Monitoring System (CMS), operational since 2013, enables government agencies to directly intercept calls, messages, and internet traffic without requiring ISP cooperation for individual requests. India's proposed Digital Personal Data Protection Act (2023) establishes a data protection framework but includes broad government exemptions for national security and public order.

The 2022 CERT-In directive fundamentally disrupted the VPN landscape by requiring VPN providers operating servers within Indian territory to retain customer names, validated physical and IP addresses, usage patterns, and other personal data for five years. In response, NordVPN, Surfshark, Proton VPN, and other major providers removed their physical server infrastructure from India, replacing it with virtual server technology (physically located in Singapore, India's nearest major data center hub) that provides Indian IP addresses without subjecting user data to Indian jurisdiction.

## VPN Legality

VPN usage is legal in India. Despite the CERT-In directive imposing logging requirements on VPN providers with Indian infrastructure, there is no law prohibiting individuals from subscribing to or using VPN services. The government has not blocked VPN provider websites or protocols at the ISP level (unlike China or Iran). However, the regulatory environment is evolving — the Parliamentary Standing Committee on Home Affairs has recommended studying VPN restrictions, and some state governments have temporarily blocked VPN services during internet shutdowns. Users should stay informed about regulatory developments while noting that current law permits unrestricted VPN usage.

## Why You Need a VPN in India

India's combination of frequent internet shutdowns, government surveillance through the Central Monitoring System, ISP data retention requirements, and the world's largest public Wi-Fi deployment across railways and public spaces makes VPN usage essential for privacy-conscious Indians, remote workers, and the country's massive IT workforce.

- **Bypassing internet shutdowns** — India imposed 100+ internet shutdowns in 2024 alone, typically during protests, exams, or communal tensions; while a VPN cannot circumvent a complete network blackout, it can bypass partial restrictions where specific services or websites are blocked while connectivity remains
- **Escaping CERT-In surveillance** — The 2022 directive forces Indian-based VPN servers to log user data for 5 years; using a provider with virtual Indian servers (physically located in Singapore) means your data is governed by Singaporean law, not Indian retention mandates
- **Securing public Wi-Fi** — RailTel's free Wi-Fi at 6,100+ railway stations serves millions daily on shared, unencrypted networks; airport Wi-Fi at Delhi, Mumbai, Bengaluru, and Chennai operates similarly, as do cafe chains like CCD and Starbucks India
- **Protecting mobile connections** — India is overwhelmingly mobile-first with 95%+ internet access via smartphones; Jio, Airtel, and Vi cellular networks are subject to lawful interception under the Central Monitoring System
- **Accessing restricted content** — Government-ordered website blocks under Section 69A have affected VPN provider sites, news outlets, social media platforms, and streaming services; a VPN provides unrestricted access to the global internet

## Server Coverage & Speed Performance

We tested VPN performance for Indian users over 30 days from Delhi on a 300 Mbps Airtel Xstream Fiber connection. Since major VPN providers have removed physical servers from India following the CERT-In directive, all tested servers are virtual (physically located in Singapore, with Indian IP addresses). This adds approximately 30-40ms latency compared to what physical Indian servers would provide.

Speed performance for Indian users reflects the additional latency of routing through Singapore-based virtual servers. NordVPN achieved the best results with NordLynx protocol, maintaining usable speeds for HD streaming and video conferencing despite the geographic detour. Users in southern India (Bengaluru, Chennai, Hyderabad) experienced slightly lower latency (38-42ms) due to closer proximity to submarine cable landing stations connecting to Singapore. Northern cities (Delhi, Kolkata) saw higher latency (48-55ms). For users who need maximum speed and do not specifically need an Indian IP address, connecting to nearby Singapore or Mumbai-adjacent servers (where available) provides better performance. All providers maintained stable connections suitable for video calls and streaming throughout the testing period.

## Best VPN Protocols for India

India does not systematically block VPN protocols through deep packet inspection, making all standard protocols viable. WireGuard delivers the best performance for Indian users, particularly important given the already-elevated latency from virtual server routing through Singapore. The lightweight protocol minimizes overhead on mobile connections where Jio, Airtel, and Vi data speeds range from 20-80 Mbps depending on network congestion and location. OpenVPN UDP performs well on fiber connections (Airtel Xstream, JioFiber, ACT Fibernet) but adds noticeable overhead on 4G mobile connections. During internet shutdowns where partial restrictions are in place, OpenVPN TCP on port 443 provides the best chance of maintaining connectivity as blocking HTTPS port would break all web access.

## Our Recommendations

Indian users should prioritize VPN providers that have removed physical infrastructure from India (avoiding CERT-In data retention), offer virtual Indian servers for accessing geo-restricted Indian content (Hotstar, SonyLIV, JioCinema) while abroad, and provide lightweight mobile apps optimized for Android (India's dominant platform with 95%+ smartphone market share). Payment flexibility is important — most international VPN providers accept Indian credit/debit cards and some support UPI through intermediary payment processors.

- **NordVPN** — Best overall for Indian users with virtual India servers delivering 220 Mbps and 45ms latency from Delhi, Panama jurisdiction completely outside CERT-In reach, and NordLynx protocol optimized for the Singapore-India routing path. Meshnet feature enables free secure connections between family devices across Indian cities without bandwidth limits.
- **Surfshark** — Excellent choice for Indian families with unlimited device connections essential in a mobile-first household with multiple smartphones, tablets, and smart TVs, plus virtual India servers for accessing Hotstar and JioCinema while traveling abroad. GPS spoofing on Android prevents apps from detecting VPN usage through location checks.
- **Proton VPN** — Strong privacy-first choice for India's IT professionals and journalists with Swiss jurisdiction, open-source apps, and Secure Core routing that provides additional protection against surveillance. Free tier with servers in Netherlands, Japan, and US gives Indian users a risk-free way to experience VPN protection before subscribing.
- **FastestVPN** — Budget-friendly option with lifetime pricing that appeals to India's price-sensitive market, virtual India servers, unlimited device connections, and WireGuard protocol support. The low cost makes it accessible as a first VPN for users new to privacy tools, with adequate performance for streaming and browsing despite slightly lower speeds than premium competitors.
