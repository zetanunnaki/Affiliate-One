## Indonesia: Bali's Digital Nomad Paradise (With Restrictions)

Indonesia — particularly Bali — is one of the world's top digital nomad destinations. Ubud and Canggu have massive expat communities with excellent co-working spaces, affordable living, and a lifestyle that attracts remote workers from every continent. However, Indonesia blocks a significant number of websites and social media platforms have been throttled during political unrest, making a VPN essential for unrestricted internet access.

## What's Blocked

Indonesia's Ministry of Communication and Information Technology (Kominfo) maintains an extensive blocklist enforced across all ISPs:

- **Reddit** — Completely blocked since 2014
- **Adult content** — Extensively blocked with tens of thousands of domains on the blocklist
- **Gambling sites** — Comprehensively blocked
- **Some streaming and file-sharing platforms** — Occasionally restricted
- **Social media** — Has been throttled during political protests (notably during the 2019 Papua unrest when WhatsApp, Instagram, and Facebook were slowed to prevent the spread of images)
- **Steam and gaming platforms** — Briefly blocked in 2022 over registration compliance, highlighting the unpredictability of Indonesian content regulation

VPN use is legal and extremely common among Indonesia's tech-savvy population and massive expat community. Kominfo has not attempted to restrict VPN services, and surveys consistently show Indonesia as one of the highest-VPN-usage countries globally.

## The Bali Remote Work Scene

Bali is digital nomad central, with infrastructure purpose-built for remote workers:

- **Canggu:** The epicenter of nomad culture. Dozens of co-working spaces (Dojo Bali, Outpost, Tropical Nomad, B-Work) compete for members. Excellent cafe Wi-Fi at spots like Crate Cafe and Machinery. Monthly co-working memberships run 1.5-3 million IDR (100-200 USD)
- **Ubud:** More zen, fewer parties. Good co-working options (Hubud's legacy continues through successors like Outpost Ubud). Surrounded by rice terraces and spiritual culture
- **Seminyak/Kuta:** Tourist-focused but workable, with newer co-working entries
- **Internet:** Fiber from IndiHome (Telkom Indonesia) available in popular areas. 50-100 Mbps is typical for fixed broadband. Consistency can vary, especially during rainy season — having a mobile data backup through Telkomsel or XL Axiata is essential

## Beyond Bali

Jakarta, Yogyakarta, and Bandung also attract remote workers with lower costs and authentic Indonesian culture. Jakarta's infrastructure rivals any Southeast Asian capital, with co-working spaces from GoWork and WeWork. The country's new digital nomad visa (B211A) allows stays of up to 180 days, though the application process requires patience.

## Security Considerations

Indonesia's Electronic Information and Transactions Law (UU ITE) gives the government broad powers over digital content and communications. The 2022 Personal Data Protection Law (UU PDP) was a positive step, but enforcement mechanisms are still developing. ISPs retain connection data, and Kominfo has the authority to demand data from service providers operating in Indonesia. A VPN based outside Indonesian jurisdiction ensures your browsing activity remains private from both ISP monitoring and government data requests.
