## Iran: Among the World's Most Censored Internets

Iran operates one of the most restrictive internet environments globally. The government controls the National Information Network (NIN/SHOMA), uses sophisticated deep packet inspection (DPI), and regularly imposes complete internet shutdowns during protests. An estimated 40-80% of Iranian internet users rely on VPNs.

## What's Blocked

The list is extensive:
- **Social media:** Facebook, Twitter/X, YouTube, Instagram (periodically), TikTok
- **Messaging:** Telegram (Iran's most popular app — officially blocked but widely used via VPN), Signal
- **Western services:** Many Google services, BBC, VOA, and international news
- **VPN services:** The government actively blocks VPN protocols and provider websites
- **During protests:** Complete internet shutdowns lasting days or weeks

## VPN Usage in Iran

VPN use is technically illegal but universal:
- Estimated 40-80% of internet users use VPNs
- Government enforcement targets VPN providers and sellers, not individual users
- Iranians are highly VPN-savvy — they regularly share working VPN configurations
- Multiple VPN options are essential as individual services get blocked and unblocked

## Critical: Prepare Before Arrival

- Download multiple VPN apps before entering Iran
- Have Shadowsocks, V2Ray, and other censorship circumvention tools as backups
- Download manual VPN configuration files
- International payment infrastructure is blocked by sanctions — prepay everything
- Mobile data from Irancell or MCI is subject to the same censorship as broadband
