## Internet Freedom & Privacy Landscape

Iran scores a devastating **10 out of 100** on Freedom House's Freedom on the Net index, placing it among the three or four most repressive internet environments on Earth. The Islamic Republic operates one of the most technically sophisticated censorship systems outside of China, combining deep packet inspection (DPI), protocol-level blocking, bandwidth throttling, and complete internet shutdowns during political unrest — all managed through the centralized National Information Network (NIN/SHOMA).

Iran's internet infrastructure passes through government-controlled chokepoints managed by the Telecommunication Infrastructure Company (TIC). Major ISPs including Irancell (MTN), MCI (Hamrah-e Aval), and Rightel for mobile, along with Shatel, ParsOnline, and Asiatech for fixed broadband, all route traffic through TIC's filtering apparatus. The government employs DPI capable of identifying and blocking VPN protocols, including WireGuard, OpenVPN, and even some obfuscated connections. During the Mahsa Amini protests in 2022, authorities imposed near-total internet shutdowns lasting weeks, throttled remaining connections to unusable speeds, and specifically targeted VPN infrastructure.

Fixed broadband from providers like Shatel and ParsOnline delivers 10-50 Mbps in Tehran and other major cities, while mobile 4G from Irancell and MCI provides 5-30 Mbps. However, international bandwidth is deliberately throttled, and connections to foreign servers face additional latency due to routing through government inspection infrastructure. An estimated 40-80% of Iranian internet users employ some form of circumvention tool — VPN use is technically illegal but so widespread that enforcement targets sellers and distributors rather than individual users.

**WARNING: VPN use is illegal in Iran. While millions use VPNs and individual enforcement is rare, selling VPN services, distributing VPN configurations, and promoting VPN use carry serious criminal penalties. Travelers should assess risk carefully.**

## VPN Legality

VPN use is explicitly illegal under Iranian law. The Computer Crimes Law (2009) and subsequent regulations criminalize unauthorized access to filtered content. The government primarily enforces these laws against VPN sellers, network administrators who facilitate access, and activists — rather than individual users among the estimated tens of millions who use circumvention tools daily. However, during crackdowns, possession of VPN applications can compound charges against detained individuals. Foreign visitors face lower risk but should understand the legal reality.

## Why You Need a VPN in Iran

Despite the legal risk, VPN use is practically universal because the alternative is complete isolation from the global internet:

- **Access virtually all social media and communication** — Instagram (Iran's most-used remaining platform, periodically blocked), Telegram (officially blocked but used by 45+ million Iranians via VPN), WhatsApp, YouTube, Facebook, and Twitter/X are all inaccessible without circumvention
- **Reach international news and information** — BBC Persian, VOA Persian, Iran International, and hundreds of news sites are blocked; VPN is the only path to journalism not controlled by state media (IRIB)
- **Protect against FATA surveillance** — Iran's Cyber Police (FATA) actively monitors online activity to identify dissidents, LGBTQ+ individuals, and critics of the regime; VPN encryption provides a critical protection layer

## Server Coverage & Speed Performance

Iran's deliberate throttling of international bandwidth makes VPN performance challenging. Fixed broadband (10-50 Mbps domestic) drops to 3-15 Mbps on international connections before VPN overhead. Through a VPN, realistically expect 2-10 Mbps on fixed broadband and 1-8 Mbps on mobile. The nearest server locations are in Turkey (Istanbul, 50-80ms), the UAE (Dubai, 60-90ms), and Armenia (Yerevan, 40-70ms).

Turkish servers offer the best balance of proximity and reliability. During crackdowns, server availability fluctuates dramatically — what works Monday may be blocked by Wednesday. Auto-connect features that cycle through multiple servers are essential. Iran's DPI is sophisticated enough to block specific server IPs quickly once identified, so providers with large server fleets and rapid IP rotation demonstrate the highest long-term reliability.

## Best VPN Protocols for Iran

Standard VPN protocols are comprehensively blocked in Iran. WireGuard is detected and blocked. Standard OpenVPN is blocked. Only heavily obfuscated protocols have any chance of working. NordVPN's obfuscated servers (XOR-patched OpenVPN over TCP 443) work intermittently. V2Ray with WebSocket transport is currently one of the most reliable circumvention methods used inside Iran. Shadowsocks with AEAD ciphers provides another working option. Always maintain multiple tools — commercial VPN, V2Ray, Shadowsocks, and Tor with obfs4 bridges — because Iran's censorship apparatus adapts within days.
