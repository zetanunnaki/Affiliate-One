## Ireland: Europe's Tech Capital

Ireland hosts the European headquarters of Apple, Google, Meta, Microsoft, Twitter/X, and dozens of other tech giants. This has made the Irish Data Protection Commission (DPC) one of the most powerful privacy regulators in the world — and one of the most controversial, with critics arguing it's too lenient on Big Tech.

## The DPC and Your Privacy

As the lead GDPR supervisory authority for most US tech companies operating in Europe, the Irish DPC's decisions affect hundreds of millions of people. Notable actions include fines against Meta (€1.2 billion) and investigations into Google and Apple.

For Irish residents, this means strong theoretical privacy protections — but practical enforcement has been debated.

## Remote Work in Ireland

Ireland has fully embraced remote and hybrid work:
- **Dublin:** Expensive but well-connected tech hub. Hundreds of co-working spaces
- **Galway, Cork, Limerick:** Growing tech scenes with lower cost of living
- **Rural Ireland:** Government-backed remote working hubs in smaller towns. Broadband improving via National Broadband Plan
- **Café culture:** Strong Wi-Fi culture in Irish pubs, cafés, and hotels
