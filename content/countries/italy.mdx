## Internet Freedom & Privacy Landscape

Italy scores 76 out of 100 on the Freedom House Freedom on the Net index, categorized as "Free." Internet access is broadly available, with the government's Italia a 1 Giga plan driving fiber expansion to underserved regions. Major ISPs — TIM (Telecom Italia), Vodafone Italia, WindTre, and Fastweb — compete aggressively, offering speeds from 100 Mbps to 1 Gbps in most cities. Open Fiber's wholesale network is extending gigabit connectivity to smaller towns across Tuscany, Puglia, and Sicily.

However, Italy has one of the longest data retention periods in the EU. Under Decreto Legislativo 109/2008 and subsequent anti-terrorism amendments, Italian ISPs must retain connection metadata for up to six years — including which IP addresses you connect to, timestamps, and session duration. This far exceeds the standard elsewhere in Europe. The Garante per la protezione dei dati personali actively enforces GDPR against private companies (it was the first authority to temporarily ban ChatGPT in 2023), while AGCOM maintains a blacklist of over 10,000 blocked domains primarily targeting piracy and unlicensed gambling sites.

## VPN Legality

VPN usage is fully legal in Italy. No Italian law restricts, prohibits, or requires registration for VPN services. Citizens and visitors can use any VPN provider without legal risk. Italian courts have consistently upheld that using encryption tools is a legitimate privacy measure. The only caveat is that using a VPN to facilitate activities that are themselves illegal (such as accessing child exploitation material or conducting fraud) does not provide legal immunity — but the VPN technology itself carries no legal restrictions whatsoever.

## Why You Need a VPN in Italy

- **Six-year data retention** — Italian ISPs including TIM, Vodafone Italia, WindTre, and Fastweb are legally required to store your connection metadata for six years, creating an extensive browsing history profile accessible to prosecutors and intelligence services
- **AGCOM content blocking** — Over 10,000 domains are blocked at the ISP level; a VPN bypasses these DNS-based restrictions transparently
- **Public Wi-Fi exposure** — Train stations (Trenitalia, Italo lounges), airports (Fiumicino, Malpensa, Linate), piazzas, and thousands of cafes and restaurants offer open, unencrypted Wi-Fi vulnerable to interception
- **Digital nomad visa security** — Italy's nomad visa (2024) requires minimum 28,000 EUR income, attracting remote workers who handle sensitive client data on shared networks in Milan, Rome, Florence, and coastal towns
- **Banking protection** — Italian online banking through Intesa Sanpaolo, UniCredit, and Poste Italiane requires protection on public networks, especially given Italy's growing problem with SIM-swap attacks and credential theft
- **Regional streaming access** — Access RAI Play, Mediaset Infinity, and DAZN Italia while traveling abroad, or unlock international content not available on Italian IP addresses

## Best VPN Protocols for Italy

Italy does not employ deep packet inspection or protocol-level VPN blocking, so all standard protocols work without issue. WireGuard is the recommended choice for maximum speed on Italy's fast fiber connections, delivering the lowest overhead and best throughput. OpenVPN (UDP) provides a proven alternative with broader compatibility across older devices and routers. For mobile users on TIM, Vodafone, or WindTre networks who move between Wi-Fi and cellular frequently, IKEv2 offers seamless connection handoff without drops. Users particularly concerned about the six-year data retention law should consider Proton VPN's Secure Core, which routes traffic through privacy-friendly jurisdictions (Switzerland, Iceland) before reaching the destination, making traffic correlation by Italian authorities significantly more difficult.
