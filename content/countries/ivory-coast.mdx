## Internet Freedom & Privacy Landscape

Ivory Coast (Cote d'Ivoire) scores 51 out of 100 in Freedom House's Freedom on the Net report, classified as Partly Free. The government has a documented history of restricting social media and messaging platforms during politically sensitive periods, including elections and protests. The primary ISPs are Orange Cote d'Ivoire (the market leader), MTN Cote d'Ivoire, and Moov Africa, providing 4G LTE coverage across Abidjan and major cities like Bouake, Yamoussoukro, and San Pedro. Internet penetration stands at approximately 45%, driven primarily by mobile access in this French-speaking West African nation.

## VPN Legality

VPN use is legal in Ivory Coast. Despite the government's occasional restrictions on social media platforms, there are no specific laws prohibiting VPN technology. Users can employ VPNs for privacy, security, and accessing restricted platforms during shutdowns without facing legal penalties.

## Server Coverage & Speed Performance

No major VPN provider operates servers within Ivory Coast. The nearest server locations are typically in South Africa (Johannesburg) or Europe (Paris, France), with some providers offering servers in nearby West African countries. Given Ivory Coast's strong historical ties to France, Paris-routed connections often deliver competitive latency of 80-120ms via submarine cables like MainOne and ACE. NordVPN and Surfshark both maintain large European server fleets that serve Ivorian users reliably.

## Best VPN Protocols for Ivory Coast

WireGuard provides the best performance for everyday use on Orange, MTN, and Moov networks. During active internet shutdowns or platform blocks, OpenVPN with TCP on port 443 can help disguise VPN traffic as regular HTTPS browsing. While Ivory Coast does not typically employ sophisticated deep packet inspection, having obfuscation-capable protocols available ensures connectivity during politically sensitive periods when authorities may attempt broader VPN interference.
