## Japan's World-Class Internet

Japan boasts some of the fastest and most reliable internet infrastructure in the world. With average download speeds exceeding 200 Mbps, widespread fiber-to-the-home deployment through NTT East/West, and comprehensive 5G coverage in urban areas, Japan is a paradise for bandwidth-intensive remote work. The country's tech-forward culture extends to every convenience store, train station, and cafe, creating a dense web of connectivity — and a correspondingly large attack surface for anyone operating on shared networks without encryption.

## Why Use a VPN in Japan

Japan has minimal internet censorship and reasonably strong consumer protections under the Act on the Protection of Personal Information (APPI), amended in 2022 to strengthen individual rights. However, several factors make VPN use essential:

- **Public Wi-Fi is everywhere but insecure:** Convenience stores (7-Eleven, FamilyMart, Lawson — over 55,000 locations combined), train stations, department stores, and cafes all offer free Wi-Fi. Nearly all of these networks are open and unencrypted, creating opportunities for credential theft and session hijacking
- **Accessing home services while visiting:** Japan attracts over 30 million international visitors annually. If you are a tourist or business traveler, you need a VPN to access streaming services, banking apps, and corporate resources from your home country
- **Privacy from ISPs:** Japanese ISPs (NTT, SoftBank, au/KDDI) comply with government requests for subscriber data under the Wiretapping Act and telecommunications regulations. The Act on Prevention of Unauthorized Computer Access gives law enforcement broad authority. A VPN prevents your ISP from logging your browsing activity
- **Remote work security:** Japan's growing remote work culture — accelerated by government initiatives promoting workation and regional telework — means more professionals working from cafes, shared offices, and hotel lobbies on shared networks

## Public Wi-Fi in Japan

Japan has invested heavily in public Wi-Fi infrastructure, particularly for the tourism industry:

- **Japan Connected-free Wi-Fi:** App that aggregates free Wi-Fi access points across Japan. Over 200,000 hotspots nationwide
- **Station Wi-Fi:** JR East, JR West, Tokyo Metro, Osaka Metro, and other rail operators offer free Wi-Fi at major stations. Coverage extends to platforms and concourses
- **Convenience store Wi-Fi:** 7-Eleven, FamilyMart, and Lawson all provide free Wi-Fi (typically time-limited to 20-60 minutes per session, but easily renewed)
- **Starbucks Japan and Tully's Coffee:** Free Wi-Fi, popular with remote workers. Many Japanese cafe chains have followed suit
- **Pocket Wi-Fi rentals:** Portable 4G/5G hotspots are extremely popular with tourists, available at airports and online. Renting one gives you a more secure personal network than public Wi-Fi, but a VPN still adds encryption that the rental device does not provide

While convenient, every one of these networks is shared among strangers. A VPN is non-negotiable.

## Japan-Specific Considerations

- **NHK and Japanese streaming:** Access TVer, Abema, U-NEXT, dTV, and NHK World from abroad with a VPN connected to a Japanese server. Conversely, access your home Netflix, Hulu, or BBC iPlayer library while in Japan
- **Gaming:** Japan's gaming culture means low latency matters. WireGuard and NordLynx protocols offer the best gaming performance with minimal overhead. Connecting to nearby servers (Tokyo, Osaka) keeps ping under 10ms for domestic gaming
- **Payment:** Credit cards are increasingly accepted, and IC cards (Suica, PASMO) are ubiquitous for transit. VPN subscriptions should be purchased online before arrival. Japanese payment methods like Konbini payment (convenience store cash payment) are generally not supported by international VPN providers
- **Language:** Most VPN apps have Japanese language support. NordVPN, Surfshark, and Proton VPN all offer Japanese-language interfaces and customer support
- **Corporate use:** Many Japanese companies require employees to use corporate VPNs for remote access. If you are working for a Japanese company, check whether your corporate VPN conflicts with a personal VPN before subscribing
