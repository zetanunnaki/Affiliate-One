## Jordan: The Middle East's Internet Oasis

Jordan stands out in the Middle East for its relatively open internet. Unlike neighboring countries, VoIP services generally work, and content restrictions are limited. Amman has a growing tech startup ecosystem and is increasingly popular with remote workers.

## Why Jordan Is Different

While UAE, Saudi Arabia, and Egypt restrict VoIP and filter content heavily, Jordan:
- **VoIP works:** WhatsApp calls, FaceTime, and Skype function normally
- **Minimal filtering:** Much less content blocking than regional neighbors
- **Growing tech hub:** Amman hosts several tech incubators and startups
- **English widely spoken:** Especially in the tech and business community

## Internet Infrastructure

- **Orange, Zain, Umniah:** Three competitive carriers with good 4G coverage
- **Amman:** Best connectivity with fiber expanding. Co-working spaces growing (The Tank, Zinc)
- **Aqaba:** Red Sea resort city with decent connectivity and growing digital nomad presence
- **Tourist sites:** Petra, Wadi Rum, Dead Sea have variable Wi-Fi (hotels yes, desert no)
