## Kazakhstan: Digital Growth With Government Control

Kazakhstan has the most developed internet infrastructure in Central Asia, but the government periodically restricts access during political events. The January 2022 protests led to a nationwide internet shutdown that lasted several days. VPN awareness remains high.

## Internet Landscape

- **Beeline, Kcell, Tele2:** Major mobile carriers with good urban 4G coverage
- **Kazakhtelecom:** Dominant broadband provider. Fiber expanding in Almaty and Astana (Nur-Sultan)
- **Kaspi:** Kazakhstan's super-app (banking, e-commerce, payments) dominates digital life
- **Speeds:** 50-200 Mbps in major cities. Rural steppe areas have limited connectivity

## Content Restrictions

Kazakhstan practices selective internet censorship:
- **Political content:** Opposition websites and critical media may be blocked
- **Social media:** Periodically restricted during political unrest (WhatsApp, Telegram throttled during 2022 protests)
- **VPN services:** Government has blocked some VPN providers. Most premium services still work
- **Surveillance:** Government has attempted to implement HTTPS interception (Qaznet certificate) — blocked by browsers
