## Kenya: Silicon Savannah

Kenya is East Africa's tech hub, with Nairobi earning the nickname "Silicon Savannah." The city hosts major tech companies, startup incubators (iHub, Nailab), and one of Africa's most innovative digital economies driven by M-Pesa mobile money.

## The M-Pesa Factor

Kenya pioneered mobile money with M-Pesa, which now processes more transactions than PayPal in Africa. Almost everything in Kenya can be paid via M-Pesa, from groceries to rent. This mobile-first digital economy means securing your mobile connection is especially important.

## Internet Infrastructure

- **Safaricom:** Dominant provider (also operates M-Pesa). Good 4G coverage in urban areas, expanding fiber
- **Airtel, Telkom Kenya:** Alternatives with growing coverage
- **Nairobi:** Good broadband and mobile coverage. Co-working spaces (Nairobi Garage, iHub) offer reliable Wi-Fi
- **Mombasa and coastal areas:** Decent coverage in tourist zones, weaker inland
- **Safari parks:** Very limited or no connectivity
