## Internet Freedom & Privacy Landscape

Laos scores just 20 out of 100 on Freedom House's Freedom on the Net index, classified as "Not Free." The single-party communist government maintains tight control over media and online expression. Authorities monitor internet activity and have arrested citizens for social media posts deemed critical of the government or party. ## Server Coverage & Speed Performance

No VPN providers operate physical servers in Laos due to the country's legal environment and limited infrastructure. The nearest server locations are in Thailand (Bangkok, with excellent proximity), Vietnam (Ho Chi Minh City, Hanoi), and Singapore. Bangkok servers offer the best performance from Vientiane with latency of just 20-35 ms thanks to direct cross-border fiber links. Singapore adds 50-70 ms. Given Laos's moderate base speeds, connecting to Bangkok servers typically preserves 50-65% of your original speed, which is sufficient for streaming and video calls.

## Best VPN Protocols for Laos

Given the government's surveillance capabilities and monitoring infrastructure, obfuscated protocols are strongly recommended. Standard WireGuard connections are identifiable through traffic analysis, and while not currently blocked, could be flagged for closer monitoring. NordVPN's obfuscated servers, Surfshark's Camouflage Mode, or Proton VPN's Stealth protocol make your VPN traffic indistinguishable from regular HTTPS browsing. This approach provides both access and plausible deniability on monitored networks.
