## Internet Freedom & Privacy Landscape

Latvia is not separately assessed by Freedom House's Freedom on the Net report, but as an EU member state it operates under GDPR and the European Electronic Communications Code, providing strong baseline privacy protections. The broadband market is served by Tet (formerly Lattelecom, the dominant fixed-line provider), Tele2 (mobile and fixed), and LMT (Latvijas Mobilais Telefons, the largest mobile operator). Latvia's fiber penetration is among the highest in the EU, with Tet offering gigabit connections across Riga and most regional cities. The country's Data State Inspectorate (DVI) actively enforces GDPR compliance.

## VPN Legality

VPNs are fully legal in Latvia as in all EU member states. There are no restrictions on encrypted communications, and Latvia's strong digital rights framework explicitly protects the use of privacy-enhancing technologies. ISPs cannot legally block or throttle VPN traffic.

## Why You Need a VPN in Latvia

Despite strong EU privacy protections, Latvia's internet users face specific situations where VPN encryption provides meaningful additional security. The country's position as a growing tech hub and its proximity to Russia add unique considerations.

- **EU data retention obligations** — While GDPR protects personal data, Latvia's Electronic Communications Law still permits metadata retention by Tet, Tele2, and LMT under certain conditions for law enforcement; a VPN adds a privacy layer beyond what regulation alone provides
- **Extensive public Wi-Fi in Riga** — Riga's Old Town, central railway station, and Vermanitis Park offer extensive free Wi-Fi networks that attract both tourists and cybercriminals; VPN encryption protects banking and personal data on these shared connections
- **Cross-border content access** — Latvian IP addresses trigger geo-restrictions on many international streaming services, and some Latvian residents need access to Russian-language content platforms that have restricted Baltic access since 2022

## Best VPN Protocols for Latvia

WireGuard is the ideal protocol for Latvia's high-speed fiber networks, where its minimal overhead allows maximum throughput on Tet's gigabit connections. NordVPN's NordLynx (WireGuard-based) achieved 840 Mbps — demonstrating how efficiently the protocol operates on quality infrastructure. OpenVPN remains available for compatibility with older corporate VPN gateways. Latvia does not employ any form of DPI or traffic inspection that would interfere with VPN protocols.
