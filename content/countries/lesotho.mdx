## Internet Freedom & Privacy Landscape

Lesotho has not been formally assessed by Freedom House's Freedom on the Net report, but the country generally maintains an unrestricted internet environment without systematic censorship or surveillance. The telecommunications market is served by Vodacom Lesotho and Econet Telecom Lesotho (ETL), which provide both mobile and limited fixed-line services. Internet penetration stands around 35%, with mobile data as the primary access method. Lesotho's geography as a landlocked kingdom entirely surrounded by South Africa means all international internet traffic transits through South African backbone networks.

## VPN Legality

VPNs are completely legal in Lesotho. There are no laws, regulations, or government policies restricting VPN usage. The government does not engage in VPN blocking or protocol interference of any kind.

## Server Coverage & Speed Performance

No VPN providers maintain servers in Lesotho. All connections route through South Africa, with Johannesburg servers providing the obvious nearest option. ## Best VPN Protocols for Lesotho

All standard VPN protocols work without issues in Lesotho. WireGuard is the recommended choice for its speed efficiency on mobile connections, which matters given relatively expensive data costs. OpenVPN (UDP) provides a reliable alternative with broader device support. IKEv2 is well-suited for users moving between Vodacom and Econet coverage areas, as it reconnects quickly after network changes.
