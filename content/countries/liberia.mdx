## Internet Freedom & Privacy Landscape

Liberia scores 51 out of 100 in Freedom House's Freedom on the Net report, classified as Partly Free. While the country generally respects freedom of expression, there have been incidents of social media manipulation, harassment of journalists online, and proposals for restrictive cybercrime legislation. The primary ISPs are Lonestar Cell MTN (the market leader) and Orange Liberia, providing 4G LTE coverage in Monrovia and major county capitals. Internet penetration is low at approximately 20%, with mobile data as the primary access method. Connectivity outside Monrovia drops significantly, and many rural counties remain largely offline.

## VPN Legality

VPN use is legal in Liberia. There are no laws prohibiting VPN technology or encrypted communications. Both citizens and visitors can use VPNs freely for privacy, security, and accessing content without facing legal consequences.

## Server Coverage & Speed Performance

No VPN provider operates servers in Liberia. The nearest server locations are in Europe (London, UK, and Paris, France) and South Africa (Johannesburg), accessible via the ACE submarine cable that lands near Monrovia. UK servers typically deliver the best latency at 90-130ms given Liberia's English-speaking orientation and direct ACE cable routing to Europe. This is adequate for web browsing, messaging, email, and standard-definition video. NordVPN operates hundreds of UK servers, while Surfshark maintains strong coverage across both UK and South African locations that serve Liberian users.

## Best VPN Protocols for Liberia

WireGuard is essential for Liberia given the country's limited bandwidth, typically 3-15 Mbps on Lonestar and Orange networks. The protocol's minimal overhead preserves every available kilobit for actual content rather than encryption processing. IKEv2 offers a strong mobile alternative with efficient reconnection as signal quality fluctuates across Monrovia's variable coverage. Since Liberia does not employ DPI or systematic VPN blocking, obfuscation is generally unnecessary, though users should be aware that the political environment can shift and having obfuscation-capable configurations as a backup is prudent.
