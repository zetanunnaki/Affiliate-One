## Internet Freedom & Privacy Landscape

Lithuania scores 77/100 on Freedom House's Freedom on the Net index, placing it firmly in the "Free" category. As an EU member state, Lithuania benefits from GDPR protections and a strong legal framework for digital privacy. However, the country's proximity to Russia and ongoing cybersecurity threats have led to expanded state surveillance powers under the Intelligence Law. Lithuania blocks Russian propaganda outlets (RT, Sputnik) and certain Belarusian state media websites, representing the most notable content restrictions.

Local ISPs: Telia Lietuva (market leader), Tele2 Lithuania, and Bite Lithuania provide comprehensive broadband and mobile coverage. Lithuania boasts one of Europe's highest fiber penetration rates at over 75% of households, with average speeds exceeding 100 Mbps nationally. ISPs comply with EU data retention directives and Lithuanian intelligence service requests, maintaining metadata records as required by the Electronic Communications Law.

## VPN Legality

VPNs are fully legal in Lithuania and widely used. As the home country of Nord Security (NordVPN's parent company), Lithuania has a particularly VPN-friendly legal and cultural environment. The government does not restrict encryption tools or VPN protocols, and usage is common among both tech professionals and general consumers. Lithuania's large cybersecurity sector actively promotes VPN adoption as a privacy best practice.
