## Internet Freedom & Privacy Landscape

Luxembourg is not individually rated by Freedom House's Freedom on the Net report, but the country consistently ranks among the top performers in the Reporters Without Borders Press Freedom Index (top 20 globally). As an EU member state, Luxembourg benefits from strong GDPR protections, and the country hosts the European Court of Justice, reinforcing its commitment to digital rights. Luxembourg's data protection commission (CNPD) is among Europe's most active, issuing significant fines to tech companies for privacy violations.

Local ISPs: POST Luxembourg (state-owned), Tango (part of Proximus Group), and Orange Luxembourg provide broadband and mobile services. POST dominates fixed broadband with its extensive fiber network, while all three compete in mobile services. Luxembourg's small size means near-universal fiber coverage, with average speeds exceeding 200 Mbps. ISPs comply with EU data retention directives and Luxembourg's national surveillance laws, which authorize metadata collection under judicial oversight.

## VPN Legality

VPNs are fully legal in Luxembourg with no restrictions whatsoever. The country's role as a major European financial and technology hub means VPN usage is not only accepted but expected in professional settings. Many EU institutions based in Luxembourg (European Court of Justice, European Investment Bank, Eurostat) mandate VPN usage for their staff, further normalizing the technology.

## Best VPN Protocols for Luxembourg

WireGuard is the optimal protocol for Luxembourg, maximizing throughput on the country's high-speed fiber and 5G infrastructure. The protocol's efficiency means virtually no perceptible difference between VPN and non-VPN connections for most activities. OpenVPN remains relevant for financial sector professionals whose corporate security policies mandate its use over newer protocols, particularly in Luxembourg's regulated banking environment where compliance requirements take precedence.
