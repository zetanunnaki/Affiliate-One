## Internet Freedom & Privacy Landscape

Madagascar scores 45 out of 100 in Freedom House's Freedom on the Net report, classified as Partly Free. The government has restricted social media during political crises and elections, and a 2020 cybercrime law introduced broad surveillance powers. The primary ISPs are Telma (the dominant fixed and mobile provider), Orange Madagascar, and Airtel Madagascar, offering 4G coverage primarily in Antananarivo, Toamasina, Mahajanga, and other provincial capitals. Internet penetration remains low at approximately 20%, with mobile data as the primary access method. Outside major cities, connectivity is sparse or nonexistent, particularly in national parks and rural areas.

## VPN Legality

VPN use is legal in Madagascar. There are no specific laws prohibiting VPN technology, and users can employ encrypted connections for privacy and security without facing legal consequences. However, the broad cybercrime legislation means users should avoid activities that could attract unwanted government attention regardless of VPN use.

## Server Coverage & Speed Performance

No VPN provider operates servers in Madagascar. The nearest server locations are South Africa (Johannesburg) and France (Paris), connected via the METISS and EASSy submarine cables that land on Madagascar's east coast. South African servers typically deliver latency of 50-80ms, while French servers offer 100-140ms, both adequate for browsing, messaging, and standard-definition streaming. NordVPN and Surfshark maintain extensive server fleets in both South Africa and France. Given Madagascar's French-speaking population and historical ties, Paris-routed connections often feel natural for accessing French-language services.

## Best VPN Protocols for Madagascar

WireGuard is the ideal protocol for Madagascar, critical for maximizing limited bandwidth on Telma, Orange, and Airtel networks where mobile speeds typically range from 5-15 Mbps. The protocol's minimal overhead preserves usable bandwidth for actual content rather than encryption overhead. During active internet restrictions, OpenVPN over TCP port 443 provides basic circumvention capability. Surfshark's Camouflage mode and Proton VPN's Stealth protocol offer additional obfuscation for periods when the government attempts to block VPN connections.
