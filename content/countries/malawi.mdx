## Internet Freedom & Privacy Landscape

Malawi has not received a formal Freedom House internet freedom assessment, but the country has experienced social media throttling during election periods, most notably during the contested 2019-2020 presidential elections. The telecommunications sector is dominated by Airtel Malawi and Telekom Networks Malawi (TNM), which together serve over 95% of mobile subscribers. Internet penetration sits around 25%, concentrated in Lilongwe, Blantyre, and Zomba, with most users accessing the internet via mobile data on 3G and expanding 4G networks. Fixed broadband remains extremely limited and expensive relative to average incomes. Lake Malawi tourism lodges increasingly offer satellite or fixed-wireless internet, though speeds are inconsistent.

## VPN Legality

VPNs are legal in Malawi. There are no laws prohibiting VPN usage, and the government does not actively block VPN protocols or providers. Users can freely download and operate VPN software without legal consequences.

## Server Coverage & Speed Performance

No major VPN providers maintain servers in Malawi, so connections route to South Africa (Johannesburg) as the nearest hub, with some users opting for Kenya (Nairobi) as an alternative. Mobile 4G speeds in Lilongwe average 10-20 Mbps, with VPN connections to Johannesburg adding 40-70ms latency. This results in adequate performance for web browsing, messaging, and standard-definition streaming. High-definition video may buffer during peak hours. Data costs remain relatively high, so lightweight protocols that minimize overhead are particularly beneficial.
