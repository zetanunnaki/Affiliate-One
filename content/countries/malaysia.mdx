## Malaysia: Affordable Asian Hub With Content Controls

Malaysia offers excellent value for remote workers — affordable cost of living, modern infrastructure in KL, English widely spoken, and a diverse food scene. However, the government filters internet content and has occasionally restricted social media.

## Content Filtering

The Malaysian Communications and Multimedia Commission (MCMC) filters various content categories:
- **Gambling sites** — Blocked
- **Adult content** — Blocked
- **Some political content** — Occasionally restricted
- **LGBTQ+ content** — Filtered
- **VPN use** — Legal and unrestricted. Many Malaysians use VPNs to access blocked content

## Kuala Lumpur for Remote Work

KL is one of Southeast Asia's most underrated remote work cities:
- **Co-working:** Co-labs, Common Ground, WeWork. $100-200/month for hot desks
- **Internet:** Maxis, Celcom (now CelcomDigi), and Unifi provide fiber up to 1 Gbps. 300 Mbps plans for ~RM150/month ($35)
- **Café culture:** Strong. Most cafés in KL and Penang offer free Wi-Fi
- **Penang:** Growing nomad destination with lower costs than KL
