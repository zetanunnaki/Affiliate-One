## Internet Freedom & Privacy Landscape

The Maldives is not formally assessed by Freedom House's Freedom on the Net report, but the country maintains notable content restrictions enforced through its two ISPs — Dhiraagu (partially state-owned) and Ooredoo Maldives. The Communications Authority of Maldives (CAM) mandates filtering of content deemed contrary to Islam (the state religion), including pornography, LGBTQ+ content, and materials critical of Islamic doctrine. VoIP services have historically been restricted or throttled to protect telecom revenue. The 2016 Defamation and Freedom of Expression Act criminalizes speech that contradicts Islamic tenets, creating legal risk around certain online activities.

## VPN Legality

VPNs are legal in the Maldives for personal and business use. There are no laws explicitly prohibiting VPN technology, and both Dhiraagu and Ooredoo do not block VPN protocols. However, using any technology to access content that is illegal under Maldivian law (particularly content deemed anti-Islamic) remains a criminal offense regardless of the method used. In practice, VPN usage is common among tourists, expatriates, and locals.

## Why You Need a VPN in Maldives

The Maldives' combination of content filtering, resort-based internet infrastructure, and VoIP restrictions makes a VPN essential for most visitors and beneficial for residents seeking unrestricted internet access.

- **VoIP throttling and blocking** — Dhiraagu and Ooredoo have historically restricted WhatsApp calls, Skype, FaceTime, and other voice services to protect their international calling revenue; a VPN encrypts this traffic so the ISP cannot identify and throttle it, restoring free communication for tourists and workers
- **Resort Wi-Fi security vulnerabilities** — Maldivian resorts provide island-wide Wi-Fi shared among all guests, and the isolated atoll infrastructure means all traffic routes through a single connection point; without VPN encryption, other guests or compromised network equipment can intercept sensitive data including banking and email credentials
- **Government content filtering** — CAM-mandated blocks extend beyond what many Western tourists expect, occasionally affecting news sites, social media content, and streaming services with religious or political content; a VPN tunnels past these restrictions transparently

## Best VPN Protocols for Maldives

WireGuard is recommended for the Maldives where bandwidth efficiency matters most — resort connections often have limited backhaul capacity to the main island, so the protocol's low overhead maximizes usable speed. OpenVPN (UDP) works reliably as a fallback. The Maldives does not employ sophisticated DPI for VPN detection, though some resort networks use basic application-layer filtering that OpenVPN TCP (port 443) can bypass by mimicking HTTPS traffic. IKEv2 is also effective for mobile devices switching between resort Wi-Fi zones.
