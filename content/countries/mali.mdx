## Internet Freedom & Privacy Landscape

Mali scores 41 out of 100 on Freedom House's Freedom on the Net index, placing it in the "Partly Free" category. The military junta that took power in 2020 and consolidated control in 2021 has repeatedly ordered internet shutdowns and social media blocks during political transitions, protests, and security operations. The telecommunications market is served primarily by Orange Mali and Moov Africa (formerly Malitel), with mobile data providing the vast majority of internet access. Internet penetration remains below 35%, heavily concentrated in Bamako and secondary cities like Sikasso and Mopti. Northern regions face severe connectivity gaps due to ongoing security instability and damaged infrastructure.

## VPN Legality

VPNs are technically legal in Mali, with no specific legislation prohibiting their use. However, during government-ordered internet shutdowns, authorities have occasionally attempted to block VPN traffic at the ISP level. Users are not prosecuted for VPN usage, but effectiveness may be reduced during active censorship events.

## Server Coverage & Speed Performance

No VPN providers operate servers inside Mali, so users connect to nearby locations in Senegal (Dakar), Ivory Coast (Abidjan), or France (Paris). Orange Mali 4G delivers 8-18 Mbps in Bamako, with VPN connections to Dakar adding approximately 30-50ms latency. Connections to Paris add 80-120ms but offer access to more server options. During government-imposed restrictions, speeds may degrade significantly as ISPs throttle encrypted traffic. Pre-downloading VPN configuration files is strongly recommended before any anticipated political events.

## Best VPN Protocols for Mali

Standard WireGuard and OpenVPN work well under normal conditions. However, during shutdown events, obfuscated protocols become necessary as authorities have deployed basic DPI to identify and block VPN connections. NordVPN's obfuscated servers and Surfshark's Camouflage Mode provide the most reliable connections during censorship events. Users should configure multiple fallback protocols in advance, as app updates may be impossible during active restrictions.
