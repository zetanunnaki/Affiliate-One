## Internet Freedom & Privacy Landscape

The Marshall Islands is a remote Pacific island nation of approximately 42,000 people spread across 29 coral atolls. Under a Compact of Free Association with the United States, the country operates as a sovereign democracy with strong press freedom protections. The National Telecommunications Authority (NTA) has been the primary ISP, though competition has emerged through private providers. Internet access is concentrated in the capital Majuro and the secondary center of Ebeye (Kwajalein Atoll), while outer atolls have minimal or no connectivity. Internet penetration remains around 40%, primarily through mobile and satellite connections. There is no government-mandated internet censorship, content filtering, or surveillance infrastructure. The primary challenge is infrastructure limitation rather than political restriction.

## VPN Legality

VPNs are completely legal in the Marshall Islands. As a democratic nation with US-aligned legal traditions, there are no restrictions on encryption, privacy tools, or VPN software. The government does not monitor VPN usage or restrict any protocols.

## Why You Need a VPN in Marshall Islands

While the Marshall Islands has no censorship concerns, the limited infrastructure and reliance on satellite and submarine cable connections create specific security and access needs.

- **Secure satellite connections** — Many users in outer atolls rely on satellite internet that transmits data over radio frequencies inherently more vulnerable to interception; VPN encryption adds critical protection
- **Access US and international content** — Despite the US association, Marshall Islands IP addresses are often not recognized by streaming services and may be geo-blocked from US content libraries; a US server connection resolves this
- **Public network protection** — Hotels, government offices, and cafes in Majuro share limited bandwidth on basic network infrastructure where traffic interception is straightforward without encryption

## Server Coverage & Speed Performance

No VPN provider operates servers in the Marshall Islands. The nearest options are in Hawaii, Guam, Australia, and Japan. The territory connects internationally through the HANTRU submarine cable and satellite links. Baseline speeds in Majuro average 3-15 Mbps on the best available connections, with satellite-dependent outer atolls receiving under 2 Mbps. VPN connections to Hawaiian servers add 80-120ms latency with 20-35% throughput reduction. Guam servers may offer slightly lower latency at 60-90ms depending on routing. Users should expect VPN-connected speeds suitable for email, web browsing, and standard-definition video rather than high-bandwidth applications.

## Best VPN Protocols for Marshall Islands

With no censorship or protocol interference, the priority is minimizing overhead on already-constrained connections. WireGuard's lightweight protocol adds minimal latency compared to heavier alternatives, making it the best choice for bandwidth-limited Pacific island connections. IKEv2 provides stable mobile connections that handle the frequent signal transitions common on Marshallese mobile networks. OpenVPN should be configured in UDP mode to reduce overhead on high-latency satellite links.
