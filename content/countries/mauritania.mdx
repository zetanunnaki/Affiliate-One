## Internet Freedom & Privacy Landscape

Mauritania scores approximately 35 out of 100 on Freedom House's Freedom on the Net index, placing it in the "Partly Free" category with significant restrictions. The government exercises considerable control over internet access through Mauritel (majority-owned by Maroc Telecom) and secondary operators Mattel and Chinguitel. Internet penetration remains around 25%, concentrated in the capital Nouakchott and the commercial center Nouadhibou, while vast Saharan interior regions lack any connectivity. The government has imposed internet shutdowns during political events including elections and protests, and maintains blasphemy laws that extend to online speech. Social media platforms have faced temporary blocks during sensitive periods, and authorities monitor online content that criticizes the government or violates Islamic norms.

## VPN Legality

VPNs are not explicitly banned in Mauritania, and many businesses and international organizations use them freely. However, during government-imposed internet restrictions, authorities have attempted to block VPN protocols. The legal environment is ambiguous — while no law criminalizes VPN use, broader cybercrime legislation could theoretically be applied against circumvention tools if authorities chose to do so.

## Why You Need a VPN in Mauritania

Mauritania's combination of periodic shutdowns, content restrictions, and limited ISP competition makes VPN protection important for both privacy and access.

- **Bypass social media blocks** — The government periodically blocks Facebook, Twitter, and messaging platforms during elections and political unrest; VPN connections maintain access to communication tools
- **Protection during shutdowns** — When authorities impose partial restrictions targeting specific platforms rather than complete blackouts, encrypted VPN tunnels bypass protocol-specific filtering
- **Shield online activity from surveillance** — Authorities monitor online speech under blasphemy and national security laws; VPN encryption prevents ISP-level tracking of browsing activity and communications

## Server Coverage & Speed Performance

No VPN provider operates servers within Mauritania. The nearest locations are in Morocco (Casablanca), Senegal (Dakar), and France (Paris). Mauritania connects internationally through the ACE submarine cable system landing at Nouakchott, providing the primary international link to Europe. Baseline speeds in Nouakchott average 3-12 Mbps on 4G mobile connections, with fixed broadband limited to select areas. VPN connections to Moroccan servers add 30-60ms latency with 20-30% throughput reduction. Senegalese servers offer similar performance. French servers deliver 60-100ms latency but provide access to French-language content important for Mauritania's Francophone population. Outside Nouakchott and Nouadhibou, speeds drop dramatically.

## Best VPN Protocols for Mauritania

During normal periods, standard VPN protocols function without interference on Mauritanian networks. However, during government-imposed restrictions, ISPs deploy basic blocking that targets common VPN signatures. Obfuscated protocols serve as essential fallback. NordVPN's obfuscated servers, Surfshark's Camouflage Mode, and Proton VPN's Stealth protocol provide reliable access during censorship events. WireGuard operates well during unrestricted periods for maximum speed. Users should maintain pre-configured obfuscated options ready for activation when restrictions are imposed.
