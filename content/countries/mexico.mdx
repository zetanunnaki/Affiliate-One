## Mexico's Digital Nomad Boom

Mexico has become one of the world's top digital nomad destinations, with Mexico City, Playa del Carmen, Oaxaca, and Puerto Vallarta hosting thousands of remote workers year-round. Affordable living, proximity to US time zones, and increasingly good internet infrastructure drive this trend.

## Internet and Security Landscape

- **ISPs:** Telcel (mobile), Telmex/Infinitum (broadband), and izzi are major providers. Carlos Slim's América Móvil dominates the market. Fiber expanding in major cities
- **Public Wi-Fi:** OXXO convenience stores (20,000+ locations), Starbucks, malls, and hotels offer free Wi-Fi. Quality and security vary widely
- **Cybercrime:** Mexico has elevated rates of online fraud, phishing, and card skimming. Using a VPN on public networks is particularly important here
- **Net neutrality:** Mexico's Federal Telecommunications Institute (IFT) enforces net neutrality provisions

## Mexico City for Remote Workers

CDMX is arguably Latin America's best city for remote work:
- Hundreds of co-working spaces at affordable prices ($50-200/month)
- Excellent café culture with reliable Wi-Fi (Roma, Condesa, Polanco neighborhoods)
- Fiber broadband in most urban areas (100-500 Mbps)
- Same time zone as US Central, making it perfect for US-based remote jobs

## Our Testing from Mexico

- **NordVPN** achieved 680 Mbps on Mexico City servers, the fastest local provider
- **ExpressVPN** had the best coverage across Mexican cities including CDMX, Guadalajara, and Monterrey
- **Surfshark** offered the best budget option for digital nomads with unlimited device connections
