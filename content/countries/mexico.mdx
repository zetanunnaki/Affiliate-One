## Mexico's Digital Nomad Boom

Mexico has become one of the world's top digital nomad destinations, with Mexico City, Playa del Carmen, Oaxaca, Puerto Vallarta, and San Miguel de Allende hosting thousands of remote workers year-round. Affordable living, proximity to US time zones, and increasingly good internet infrastructure drive this trend. Mexico's temporary resident visa is straightforward for remote workers, and the country imposes no specific digital nomad tax for those not earning Mexican-source income.

## Internet and Security Landscape

- **ISPs:** Telcel (mobile), Telmex/Infinitum (broadband), and izzi are major providers. Carlos Slim's America Movil dominates the market, though competitors like Totalplay and Megacable are expanding fiber rapidly in major cities. Fiber plans offering 200-500 Mbps are available in CDMX, Guadalajara, and Monterrey for 500-900 MXN per month
- **Public Wi-Fi:** OXXO convenience stores (20,000+ locations), Starbucks, malls, airports (AICM, Cancun, Guadalajara), and hotels offer free Wi-Fi. Quality and security vary widely — many networks are completely open with no encryption
- **Cybercrime:** Mexico has elevated rates of online fraud, phishing, and card skimming. Banking trojans targeting Mexican financial institutions are common, and man-in-the-middle attacks on public Wi-Fi are a documented risk. Using a VPN on public networks is not optional — it is essential
- **Net neutrality:** Mexico's Federal Telecommunications Institute (IFT) enforces net neutrality provisions, but ISP throttling of streaming and VoIP services has been reported by users

## Surveillance and Press Freedom

Mexico's government has been involved in high-profile digital surveillance scandals. The Pegasus spyware was extensively used against journalists, activists, and political figures, as documented by Citizen Lab and international press organizations. While VPN encryption does not protect against device-level spyware, it does prevent network-level monitoring and protects your browsing activity from ISP-level surveillance and data requests. For journalists and activists operating in Mexico, a VPN is one component of a broader security strategy.

## Mexico City for Remote Workers

CDMX is arguably Latin America's best city for remote work:
- Hundreds of co-working spaces at affordable prices (1,000-4,000 MXN per month). WeWork, Spaces, and independent operators like Homework and Centraal serve different neighborhoods
- Excellent cafe culture with reliable Wi-Fi in Roma, Condesa, Polanco, and Coyoacan neighborhoods
- Fiber broadband in most urban areas reaching 100-500 Mbps
- Same time zone as US Central, making it ideal for US-based remote jobs
- Growing tech startup ecosystem with increasing venture capital activity
