## Internet Freedom & Privacy Landscape

The Federated States of Micronesia (FSM) is a Pacific island nation comprising four states — Yap, Chuuk, Pohnpei, and Kosrae — spread across more than 2,500 kilometers of ocean. With approximately 105,000 residents across over 600 islands, FSM operates under a Compact of Free Association with the United States. The FSM Telecommunications Corporation (FSMTC) is the primary provider, supplemented by private operator Belize Telecom International (operating as Micronesian Telecommunications Corporation). Internet access is concentrated in the state capitals, particularly Palikir (the national capital on Pohnpei) and Weno (Chuuk). The HANTRU submarine cable provides international connectivity, though bandwidth remains limited. Internet penetration is approximately 35%, with outer islands relying on satellite or having no connectivity at all. There is no government censorship or content filtering — infrastructure limitations are the sole constraint.

## VPN Legality

VPNs are completely legal in Micronesia. US-aligned legal traditions and FSM's democratic framework place no restrictions on encryption, privacy tools, or VPN software. The government does not monitor or restrict VPN usage in any way.

## Why You Need a VPN in Micronesia

FSM's limited infrastructure and remote Pacific location create specific needs for secure and unrestricted internet access.

- **Access US content and services** — Despite the US association, Micronesian IP addresses are frequently geo-blocked from American streaming services, banking platforms, and digital services; US server connections resolve these access issues
- **Secure limited infrastructure** — FSMTC's network serves a small market with basic security standards; VPN encryption adds protection that the underlying network infrastructure may not provide
- **Protect on government and hotel networks** — State capitals offer shared networks in government buildings, hotels, and dive resort operations where traffic interception is straightforward without encryption

## Server Coverage & Speed Performance

No VPN provider operates servers in Micronesia. The nearest options are in Guam, Hawaii, Japan, and Australia. The HANTRU submarine cable connects to Guam, making Guam-adjacent or Japanese servers the lowest-latency options. Baseline speeds in Pohnpei and other state capitals average 2-10 Mbps on the best available connections, with mobile data rarely exceeding 5 Mbps. VPN connections to Guam or Japanese servers add 30-60ms latency with 20-35% throughput reduction. Hawaiian servers offer 80-120ms latency. US West Coast servers deliver approximately 100-150ms. Given the constrained bandwidth, users should expect VPN-connected speeds suitable for web browsing, email, and standard-definition video rather than bandwidth-intensive applications.

## Best VPN Protocols for Micronesia

With no censorship or protocol interference, the priority is minimizing overhead on severely bandwidth-constrained connections. WireGuard's lightweight protocol adds minimal latency and uses less bandwidth than alternatives — critical when baseline speeds are under 10 Mbps. IKEv2 provides stable connections on FSMTC's mobile network with its frequent signal variations between islands. OpenVPN should be configured in UDP mode to reduce overhead on high-latency Pacific connections.
