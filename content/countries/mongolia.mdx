## VPN Legality

VPN use is fully legal in Mongolia with no restrictions. The government does not block VPN protocols or penalize users for encrypting their traffic. Business use of VPNs is widespread, particularly among international companies operating in the mining sector.

## Server Coverage & Speed Performance

No major VPN provider operates physical servers in Mongolia. The nearest options are in Russia (Novosibirsk, Moscow), Japan (Tokyo), South Korea (Seoul), and Hong Kong. Due to fiber links running through Russian territory, Moscow and Novosibirsk servers may offer the lowest latency at 50-80 ms, but privacy-conscious users may prefer routing through Japan (80-120 ms) or South Korea (70-100 ms) to avoid Russian transit surveillance. Base speeds in Ulaanbaatar average 20-40 Mbps on 4G/LTE, and users can expect to retain 45-60% when connecting to Asian-Pacific servers.

## Best VPN Protocols for Mongolia

WireGuard is recommended for Mongolia, where every bit of speed optimization matters given the moderate base bandwidth and high-latency international links. Mongolia does not employ DPI or VPN blocking, making obfuscation unnecessary for normal use. However, for users whose traffic routes through Russian infrastructure, the encryption provided by any VPN protocol adds meaningful protection against en-route surveillance. OpenVPN serves as a stable backup on networks where WireGuard's UDP packets face interference.
