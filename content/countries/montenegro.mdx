## Internet Freedom & Privacy Landscape

Montenegro is not formally assessed by Freedom House's Freedom on the Net report, but the country maintains generally open internet access without systematic content filtering. The telecommunications market is served by three operators — Crnogorski Telekom (Deutsche Telekom subsidiary), m:tel (Telekom Srbija subsidiary), and One (e&/Etisalat). Montenegro uses the euro as its currency and is an EU candidate country, but has not yet implemented GDPR-equivalent data protection standards. Press freedom organizations have noted concerns about media pressure and surveillance of journalists, though these issues primarily affect traditional media rather than broad internet censorship.

## VPN Legality

VPNs are fully legal in Montenegro with no restrictions on personal or commercial use. ISPs do not block or throttle VPN protocols, and there are no regulatory requirements that limit encrypted communications. VPN usage is common among the country's growing digital nomad population.

## Why You Need a VPN in Montenegro

Montenegro's stunning Adriatic coastline attracts thousands of digital nomads and remote workers annually, particularly to Budva, Kotor, and Podgorica. The country's pre-EU data protection environment and tourist-heavy infrastructure create specific needs for VPN protection.

- **Tourist-area shared networks** — Kotor's Old Town, Budva's beach hotels, and Tivat's marina all provide free Wi-Fi that handles thousands of daily tourists, creating ideal conditions for credential theft and session hijacking without encryption
- **Pre-GDPR data protection** — Montenegro's Personal Data Protection Agency exists but lacks the enforcement power of EU data authorities; ISPs like Crnogorski Telekom and m:tel operate under less stringent obligations regarding subscriber data handling
- **EU service access for remote workers** — Many digital nomads in Montenegro work for EU companies with tools and platforms that restrict non-EU IP addresses; connecting through an EU server location restores full access instantly

## Best VPN Protocols for Montenegro

WireGuard provides the best performance on Montenegro's networks, particularly on Crnogorski Telekom's fiber connections in Podgorica and the coastal fiber buildouts. The protocol's efficiency is noticeable on mobile connections via m:tel where bandwidth may be more constrained during summer tourist peaks. OpenVPN (UDP) is a solid fallback for older devices. IKEv2 is particularly well-suited for nomads moving between hotel Wi-Fi and mobile data along the coast, as it reconnects seamlessly without dropping active sessions. Montenegro does not employ DPI or VPN blocking of any kind.
