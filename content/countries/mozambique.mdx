## Internet Freedom & Privacy Landscape

Mozambique scores 50 out of 100 on Freedom House's Freedom on the Net index, placing it in the "Partly Free" category. The government has a history of throttling social media during politically sensitive moments, including elections and protests. The telecommunications market is served by three major operators: Vodacom Mozambique (the largest), Movitel, and Tmcel (state-owned). Internet penetration is approximately 21%, with mobile data accounting for the overwhelming majority of connections. Coverage is concentrated in Maputo, Beira, and Nampula, while northern provinces affected by the Cabo Delgado insurgency face severe connectivity disruptions. Portuguese is the primary language, and most international content accessed is from Portugal and Brazil.

## VPN Legality

VPNs are legal in Mozambique. There is no legislation restricting VPN usage, downloads, or installation. While the government has throttled social media platforms during protests, it has not specifically targeted VPN protocols or providers.

## Server Coverage & Speed Performance

No major VPN provider operates physical servers in Mozambique. The nearest server locations are South Africa (Johannesburg) and, for some providers, Kenya (Nairobi). Vodacom 4G in Maputo delivers 15-30 Mbps, with VPN connections to Johannesburg adding 30-50ms latency thanks to direct fiber links between the two countries. This provides comfortable performance for streaming, video calls, and general browsing. Connections to European servers (Lisbon) add 150-180ms but remain usable for non-real-time activities. Beach resort areas in Vilankulo and Tofo rely on limited backhaul and will experience slower speeds regardless of VPN choice.

## Best VPN Protocols for Mozambique

Standard protocols operate without interference in Mozambique. WireGuard delivers the best performance on mobile connections, particularly on Vodacom and Movitel 4G networks where bandwidth efficiency matters. OpenVPN (UDP) serves as a reliable backup with stronger compatibility across older Android devices common in the market. During any future throttling events, switching to TCP port 443 can help bypass basic traffic management.
