## Myanmar: Severe Internet Restrictions Since 2021

Myanmar's internet landscape changed dramatically after the February 2021 military coup. The junta has imposed extended internet shutdowns, blocked social media platforms, and attempted to create a China-style firewall. VPN use has become essential for millions of Myanmar citizens.

## What's Restricted

- **Social media:** Facebook (Myanmar's most-used platform), Twitter, Instagram blocked since the coup
- **Communication:** Messaging apps periodically restricted. VPN essential for WhatsApp, Signal, Telegram
- **News:** Independent media outlets blocked. VPN needed to access real news
- **Internet shutdowns:** Complete blackouts have been imposed, especially in conflict areas and during political events
- **VPN blocking:** The military government has ordered ISPs to block VPN services with varying success

## VPN Usage in Myanmar

VPN adoption exploded after the coup — Myanmar became one of the top VPN-downloading countries globally. Despite government attempts to block VPN services:
- Most premium VPN providers still work, though reliability varies
- The government's technical capability to block VPNs is less sophisticated than China's
- Multiple VPN options provide redundancy when one is blocked

## Preparation

If visiting or in Myanmar:
- Download multiple VPN apps before restrictions intensify
- Configure obfuscation/stealth protocols
- Have Shadowsocks and Tor as backup options
- Download manual VPN configuration files
- Keep multiple communication channels configured
