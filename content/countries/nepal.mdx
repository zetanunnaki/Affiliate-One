## Internet Freedom & Privacy Landscape

Nepal scores 51/100 on Freedom House's Freedom on the Net index, placing it in the "Partly Free" category. The government has demonstrated willingness to ban platforms outright, with TikTok banned in November 2023 and remaining inaccessible through 2024. The Information Technology Bill introduced in 2024 proposes expanded powers for content takedowns and data access, while the Electronic Transactions Act already criminalizes vaguely defined online offenses. Social media restrictions during political protests have occurred repeatedly.

Local ISPs: Nepal Telecom (state-owned), Ncell (Axiata Group), and Smart Cell (previously UTL) provide the majority of internet access. Nepal Telecom dominates fixed broadband through its ADSL and fiber network in urban areas, while Ncell leads mobile data with superior 4G coverage. ISPs implement government-mandated website blocks through DNS filtering and have complied with directives to block TikTok, porn sites, and various platforms deemed harmful by the Nepal Telecommunications Authority (NTA).

## VPN Legality

VPNs are technically legal in Nepal, but the legal landscape is ambiguous. While no law explicitly prohibits VPN use, the government has publicly discouraged VPN usage for bypassing platform bans. During the TikTok ban, officials discussed blocking VPN services but ultimately lacked the technical capacity to do so. There have been no prosecutions of individual VPN users, and VPN adoption remains widespread among tech-savvy Nepalis.

## Best VPN Protocols for Nepal

WireGuard is recommended for maximum speed on Nepal's bandwidth-constrained international links, where protocol efficiency directly impacts usable throughput. For bypassing government DNS blocks and potential VPN interference, OpenVPN with obfuscation (TCP port 443) provides the most reliable access, appearing indistinguishable from regular HTTPS traffic to Nepal Telecom's filtering systems. This is particularly important given government discussions about blocking VPN protocols.
