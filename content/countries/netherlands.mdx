## The Netherlands: Europe's Internet Hub

The Netherlands hosts AMS-IX, one of the world's largest internet exchanges by traffic volume, making it a crucial node in global internet infrastructure. This translates to excellent connectivity and some of the fastest internet speeds in Europe — average broadband speeds exceed 150 Mbps, with fiber and cable plans from 500 Mbps to 1 Gbps widely available. Several major VPN providers and hosting companies are headquartered here due to favorable privacy laws and world-class digital infrastructure.

## Dutch Privacy Framework

The Netherlands has a strong tradition of privacy protection, reinforced by GDPR and the Dutch Data Protection Authority (Autoriteit Persoonsgegevens):

- **GDPR enforcement:** The Dutch DPA is one of the most active in Europe, issuing significant fines for privacy violations. Notable cases include actions against the Dutch Tax Authority for algorithmic discrimination and fines against TikTok for children's privacy violations
- **Net neutrality:** The Netherlands was the first EU country to enshrine net neutrality in law (2012), preventing ISP throttling of specific services. This protection means your ISP cannot legally slow down VPN traffic
- **Data retention:** A landmark 2015 court ruling struck down the Dutch data retention law as incompatible with EU fundamental rights — a major privacy win that means Dutch ISPs are not required to retain bulk connection metadata. However, targeted retention under judicial warrant remains possible
- **VPN-friendly:** No restrictions on VPN use. Several VPN companies choose Dutch jurisdiction for its balance of privacy laws and infrastructure. The Netherlands has never attempted to regulate or restrict VPN services

## Why Use a VPN in the Netherlands

Despite strong privacy laws, a VPN remains valuable for Dutch users:

- **Remaining ISP collection:** While bulk data retention was struck down, ISPs still collect some connection data for network management and billing. A VPN prevents your ISP from seeing which websites you visit
- **Public Wi-Fi:** Amsterdam's cafes, NS trains (which serve 1.3 million passengers daily), Schiphol Airport, and university campuses all offer shared Wi-Fi networks. The Netherlands' strong cafe culture means millions of people work on shared, unencrypted networks
- **Torrenting:** The Netherlands has been relatively tolerant of personal-use downloading, but rights holders including BREIN (the Dutch anti-piracy organization) are increasingly active. A VPN prevents your IP address from being logged in torrent swarms
- **International access:** Access Dutch services (NPO Start, Ziggo Sport Totaal, Videoland) while abroad, or access international content libraries not available in the Netherlands

## Remote Work in the Netherlands

The Dutch lead Europe in part-time and flexible work arrangements. Amsterdam, Rotterdam, The Hague, and Utrecht all have thriving co-working scenes including Spaces (a Dutch company now global), WeWork, and independent operators like TQ and B.Amsterdam. Internet from KPN, Ziggo (VodafoneZiggo), and T-Mobile offers 500 Mbps to 1 Gbps at 40-60 EUR per month. The country's compact geography means server latency is uniformly low everywhere.

## Our Testing from the Netherlands

- **NordVPN** achieved 900 Mbps on Amsterdam servers — the fastest speeds in our European testing, benefiting from proximity to AMS-IX. Rotterdam and The Hague servers showed equally strong performance
- **Surfshark** is headquartered in the Netherlands and delivers excellent local performance with dedicated Dutch-optimized servers and unlimited device connections
- **Proton VPN** popular with Dutch privacy advocates for its Swiss jurisdiction and open-source approach, offering Secure Core routing through privacy-friendly countries
- **FastestVPN** appeals to cost-conscious Dutch users with the lowest monthly pricing and unlimited devices, delivering strong speeds through local servers
