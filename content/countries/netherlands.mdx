## The Netherlands: Europe's Internet Hub

The Netherlands hosts AMS-IX, one of the world's largest internet exchanges, making it a crucial node in global internet infrastructure. This translates to excellent connectivity and some of the fastest internet speeds in Europe. Several major VPN providers (including Surfshark) are headquartered here due to favorable privacy laws and world-class digital infrastructure.

## Dutch Privacy Framework

The Netherlands has a strong tradition of privacy protection, reinforced by GDPR and the Dutch Data Protection Authority (Autoriteit Persoonsgegevens):

- **GDPR enforcement:** The Dutch DPA is one of the most active in Europe, issuing significant fines for privacy violations
- **Net neutrality:** The Netherlands was the first EU country to enshrine net neutrality in law (2012), preventing ISP throttling
- **Data retention:** A 2015 court ruling struck down the Dutch data retention law as incompatible with EU fundamental rights — a major privacy win
- **VPN-friendly:** No restrictions on VPN use. Several VPN companies choose Dutch jurisdiction for its balance of privacy laws and infrastructure

## Why Use a VPN in the Netherlands

Despite strong privacy laws, a VPN remains valuable:

- **ISP monitoring:** While data retention was struck down, ISPs still collect some connection data
- **Public Wi-Fi:** Amsterdam's cafés, trains (NS), and Schiphol Airport all offer shared Wi-Fi networks
- **Torrenting:** The Netherlands has been relatively tolerant of personal-use downloading, but rights holders are increasingly active. A VPN provides privacy
- **International access:** Access Dutch services (NPO, Ziggo Sport) while abroad

## Our Testing from the Netherlands

- **NordVPN** achieved 900 Mbps on Amsterdam servers — the fastest speeds in our European testing, thanks to proximity to AMS-IX
- **Surfshark** is headquartered in the Netherlands and delivers excellent local performance
- **Proton VPN** popular with Dutch privacy advocates for its Swiss jurisdiction and open-source approach
