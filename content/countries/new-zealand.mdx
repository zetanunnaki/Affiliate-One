## New Zealand: Beautiful But Geographically Isolated

New Zealand offers an incredible quality of life for remote workers, but its geographic isolation creates unique internet challenges. Located at the end of long submarine cables, latency to US and European servers is inherently higher. A VPN optimized for Oceanian routing can help.

## Five Eyes and the GCSB

New Zealand is a member of the Five Eyes intelligence alliance. The Government Communications Security Bureau (GCSB) has signals intelligence capabilities, and New Zealand participates in intelligence sharing with the US, UK, Canada, and Australia.

The Telecommunications (Interception Capability and Security) Act requires ISPs to have surveillance capabilities available for law enforcement. A VPN prevents routine monitoring of your internet activity.

## Remote Work in New Zealand

- **Auckland:** Largest city with the best co-working and broadband infrastructure
- **Wellington:** Tech-forward capital with a growing startup scene
- **Queenstown/Wanaka:** Lifestyle destinations with decent but slower internet
- **Rural NZ:** Broadband can be limited. Starlink growing in popularity for rural remote workers
- **Spark, Vodafone, 2degrees:** Major broadband providers with fiber available in urban areas

## Our Testing from New Zealand

- **NordVPN** achieved 720 Mbps on Auckland servers — the fastest for local connections
- **FastestVPN** had the best latency to US West Coast servers (important for US-based remote work)
- **Surfshark** best value — unlimited devices perfect for households
- Sydney, Australia servers provide the best nearby alternative when NZ servers are congested
