## Internet Freedom & Privacy Landscape

Nicaragua scores 42 out of 100 on Freedom House's Freedom on the Net index, classifying it as "Partly Free" with a declining trajectory. Under the Ortega government, online surveillance has intensified, journalists and activists face prosecution for social media posts, and the 2020 "Cybercrimes Law" criminalized vaguely-defined online speech. The telecommunications market is served by Claro (America Movil, the dominant provider), Movistar (Telefonica), and Tigo (Millicom). Internet penetration is approximately 55%, concentrated in Managua, Granada, Leon, and the Pacific coast tourist towns like San Juan del Sur. The Caribbean coast (RAAN/RAAS autonomous regions) has extremely limited connectivity. The government has not ordered full internet shutdowns but has engaged in targeted throttling and social media interference during the 2018 protests.

## VPN Legality

VPNs are currently legal in Nicaragua. Despite the restrictive Cybercrimes Law, no specific provisions target VPN usage. However, the legal environment is deteriorating, and the government's willingness to prosecute online expression makes privacy tools increasingly important for anyone engaging in commentary critical of the authorities.

## Server Coverage & Speed Performance

Nicaragua has no in-country VPN servers from major providers. Users connect to nearby servers in Costa Rica, the United States (Miami), or Mexico. Claro 4G delivers 15-30 Mbps in Managua, with VPN connections to Miami adding 50-70ms latency. Costa Rica servers (when available) provide slightly lower latency at 40-55ms. These speeds comfortably support streaming, video calls, and general browsing. Pacific coast towns frequented by tourists generally have adequate connectivity, while Caribbean coast locations like Bluefields and the Corn Islands have limited bandwidth that is slow regardless of VPN usage.

## Best VPN Protocols for Nicaragua

Standard protocols work in Nicaragua under normal conditions, as the government has not deployed DPI-based VPN blocking. WireGuard provides the best speeds for everyday use. OpenVPN (UDP) offers reliable connectivity across Nicaragua's variable network conditions. However, given the political trajectory, having obfuscated protocol options pre-configured is prudent. Surfshark's Camouflage Mode and NordVPN's obfuscated servers serve as insurance against potential future protocol blocking.
