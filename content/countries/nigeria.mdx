## Nigeria: Africa's Largest Digital Market

Nigeria is Africa's most populous country and its largest internet market, with over 100 million internet users. Lagos has a booming tech scene ("Yaba Valley"), and the country's BPO and tech sectors are growing rapidly. However, government restrictions on social media and elevated cybercrime rates make VPN use particularly important.

## The Twitter Ban That Changed Everything

In June 2021, Nigeria banned Twitter for over 7 months after the platform removed a presidential tweet. VPN downloads in Nigeria surged by over 1,400% overnight. The incident demonstrated both the government's willingness to restrict internet access and Nigerians' savvy in using VPNs to maintain connectivity.

Since then, VPN awareness in Nigeria has remained high, and many Nigerians maintain VPN subscriptions as a precaution against future restrictions.

## Cybersecurity Considerations

Nigeria faces elevated online fraud risks:
- **Phishing attacks** targeting both individuals and businesses are common
- **Banking fraud** is a significant concern — always use a VPN for banking on public or mobile networks
- **Payment challenges:** NGN exchange rate volatility makes international VPN pricing complex. Many Nigerians prefer providers accepting Flutterwave or Paystack

- **ExpressVPN** had the most consistent performance across Nigerian mobile networks (MTN, Airtel, Glo)
