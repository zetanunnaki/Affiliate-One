## North Korea: The World's Most Isolated Internet

North Korea has the world's most restricted internet. Citizens have no access to the global internet — only a domestic intranet called Kwangmyong. Foreign visitors may get limited, monitored internet access through Koryolink 3G, but this is the exception, not the rule.

## Internet Reality

- **No public internet:** North Korean citizens cannot access the global internet
- **Kwangmyong:** Domestic intranet with government-approved content only
- **Koryolink:** Foreign visitors can purchase 3G SIM cards (~€150 for 2 weeks) with limited internet access
- **All traffic monitored:** Every connection is surveilled by the state
- **Content:** Access to most global services blocked. Even Koryolink access is heavily restricted

## For Visitors

If visiting North Korea through a state-approved tour:
- **Don't expect internet access** — plan for complete disconnection
- **Koryolink SIM** may provide basic data but is monitored and expensive
- **VPN may work** on Koryolink but is monitored — assume anything you do is visible
- **Download everything offline** before entering — maps, translation apps, entertainment
- **Photography and device use** are restricted in many areas
- **All tourism is state-controlled** through approved tour operators

## The Bottom Line

North Korea is effectively offline for visitors. This is not a VPN recommendation page — it's a warning that internet access does not meaningfully exist in the DPRK. If you visit, prepare for complete digital disconnection and respect local laws regarding electronic devices.
