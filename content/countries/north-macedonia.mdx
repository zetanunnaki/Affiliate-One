## Internet Freedom & Privacy Landscape

North Macedonia is not formally assessed by Freedom House's Freedom on the Net report, but the country generally maintains open internet access without systematic content filtering. The broadband market is served by three main providers — Makedonski Telekom (Deutsche Telekom subsidiary), A1 MK (A1 Telekom Austria Group), and One (formerly VIP, owned by e&/Etisalat). Fiber deployment is expanding rapidly in Skopje and Bitola, though rural areas still rely on DSL and mobile data. North Macedonia's data protection legislation has been updated to align with EU standards as part of its ongoing EU accession process, but enforcement capacity remains limited. The Directorate for Personal Data Protection adopted a new framework modeled on GDPR in 2020, but staffing shortages mean compliance audits of ISPs are infrequent, and the country's 2015 wiretapping scandal — which exposed mass surveillance of over 20,000 citizens — highlighted ongoing gaps in oversight of state interception capabilities.

## VPN Legality

VPNs are completely legal in North Macedonia with no restrictions. The country's regulatory framework does not limit encryption tools, and ISPs do not interfere with VPN protocols. Personal and commercial VPN use is widespread, particularly in the IT outsourcing sector.

## Why You Need a VPN in North Macedonia

North Macedonia's growing IT sector and affordable living costs attract remote workers and outsourcing companies, but the country's developing data protection enforcement and regional network vulnerabilities make VPN protection a smart choice.

- **Limited data protection enforcement** — While North Macedonia has updated privacy laws for EU alignment, the regulatory body lacks resources for comprehensive enforcement; a VPN ensures your browsing privacy regardless of how ISPs like Makedonski Telekom or A1 MK handle subscriber data
- **Tourist area Wi-Fi vulnerabilities** — Lake Ohrid (UNESCO World Heritage), Skopje's Old Bazaar, and Bitola's cafe strips offer free Wi-Fi that attracts both tourists and opportunistic attackers; VPN encryption neutralizes packet sniffing risks on these networks
- **Accessing EU-restricted services** — As a non-EU country, Macedonian IP addresses are blocked from many EU digital services, streaming platforms, and financial tools that remote workers depend on daily

## Best VPN Protocols for North Macedonia

WireGuard is the optimal protocol for North Macedonia's fiber networks, offering the lowest overhead and fastest handshake times. Makedonski Telekom's fiber infrastructure supports high throughput that WireGuard can fully utilize. OpenVPN (UDP) serves as a reliable alternative for legacy devices. North Macedonia does not employ DPI, protocol blocking, or any form of VPN interference on its networks.
