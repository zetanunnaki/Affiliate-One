## Internet Freedom & Privacy Landscape

The Northern Mariana Islands (CNMI) is a Commonwealth of the United States, operating under US federal law for internet governance, telecommunications regulation, and digital privacy. The internet is fully unrestricted and protected by First Amendment and Fourth Amendment principles. The telecommunications market is served primarily by IT&E (formerly Pacific Telecom) and Docomo Pacific (NTT subsidiary). IT&E provides fixed broadband, fiber, and mobile services across Saipan, Tinian, and Rota, while Docomo Pacific competes in both fixed and mobile segments. Connectivity is strongest on Saipan, the capital and most populated island, with Tinian and Rota having more limited infrastructure. The territory's proximity to Guam (a major Pacific telecommunications hub) benefits its international bandwidth via submarine cable connections.

## VPN Legality

VPNs are completely legal in the Northern Mariana Islands. As a US Commonwealth, all federal laws regarding internet freedom and encrypted communications apply without restriction.

## Server Coverage & Speed Performance

No VPN providers have servers in the Northern Mariana Islands. The nearest server locations are in Guam (limited availability), Japan (Tokyo), and the US West Coast (Los Angeles). Tokyo servers typically offer the lowest latency at 30-50ms due to the CNMI's Pacific proximity to Japan, while Los Angeles servers provide access to mainland US content at 90-120ms. IT&E fiber subscribers on Saipan can expect VPN speeds of 30-70 Mbps, supporting HD streaming and video conferencing. Docomo Pacific mobile users on 4G will see speeds of 10-30 Mbps through an encrypted tunnel. Connectivity on Tinian and Rota is more limited but still serviceable.

## Best VPN Protocols for Northern Mariana Islands

WireGuard is the recommended protocol for the Northern Mariana Islands, delivering efficient encrypted connections across the Pacific with minimal overhead. The protocol handles the moderate latency to mainland US servers better than OpenVPN, providing a smoother experience for real-time applications. IKEv2 works well for mobile users moving between IT&E and Docomo Pacific networks, providing seamless handoff without connection drops.
