## Internet Freedom & Privacy Landscape

Oman scores 30 out of 100 on Freedom House's Freedom on the Net index, placing it firmly in the "Not Free" category. The internet market is controlled by two state-linked operators — Omantel (55% government-owned) and Ooredoo Oman — which implement content filtering at the ISP level as directed by the Telecommunications Regulatory Authority (TRA). Blocked content categories include VoIP services (Skype, WhatsApp calls, FaceTime), pornography, gambling sites, and content deemed critical of the Sultan or Islam. The TRA mandates URL filtering across both ISPs, and the IT Crime Law (Royal Decree 12/2011) criminalizes online content that threatens public order or religious values.

## VPN Legality

VPNs are technically legal for personal use in Oman, but the situation is nuanced. The TRA has stated that using a VPN to access blocked content or commit crimes is illegal, while using one for legitimate business purposes is permitted. In practice, personal VPN use is widespread and enforcement against individuals is rare, but users should be aware that circumventing government-mandated blocks carries theoretical legal risk.

## Why You Need a VPN in Oman

Oman's extensive content filtering and VoIP blocking make a VPN practically essential for expatriates, business travelers, and visitors who need unrestricted communications. The restrictions directly impact daily productivity and personal communications.

- **VoIP blocking by Omantel and Ooredoo** — WhatsApp calls, Skype, FaceTime, Zoom (sometimes), and other voice/video services are blocked at the ISP level to protect the revenue of licensed telecom operators; a VPN tunnels this traffic past the filters, restoring free communication with family and colleagues
- **Extensive content filtering** — The TRA maintains a broad blocklist enforced by both Omantel and Ooredoo that extends beyond what many visitors expect, including news sites, forums, and social media content critical of regional governments; a VPN restores open access to the global internet
- **Surveillance and data retention** — Oman's IT Crime Law combined with government ownership stakes in both ISPs creates an environment where browsing data may be monitored; VPN encryption ensures your traffic is unreadable at the ISP level

## Best VPN Protocols for Oman

Oman employs deep packet inspection (DPI) technology that can identify and sometimes throttle standard VPN protocols. For reliable performance, obfuscated protocols are recommended — NordVPN's obfuscated servers and Surfshark's Camouflage mode disguise VPN traffic as regular HTTPS, preventing detection. Standard WireGuard and OpenVPN may work intermittently but can be disrupted during periods of increased filtering. Always keep multiple protocol options configured as a fallback strategy in case one method is temporarily blocked.
