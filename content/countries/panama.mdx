## Internet Freedom & Privacy Landscape

Panama scores 61/100 on Freedom House's Freedom on the Net index, placing it in the "Partly Free" category. While the country generally respects freedom of expression online, Panama's legal framework allows broad government surveillance. Law 51 of 2009 grants intelligence services the ability to intercept communications, and the country lacks comprehensive data protection legislation equivalent to GDPR, leaving user data vulnerable to both commercial exploitation and government access.

Local ISPs: Cable & Wireless Panama (CWP, now part of Liberty Latin America), Tigo Panama, and Claro Panama provide the majority of broadband and mobile services. Cable & Wireless dominates fixed broadband with approximately 60% market share, while Tigo and Claro compete aggressively in mobile data. ISPs are required to cooperate with government surveillance requests, and there is minimal transparency about how frequently such requests are made.

## VPN Legality

VPNs are fully legal in Panama and widely used by the international banking and business community. There are no restrictions on VPN usage, encryption tools, or privacy software. Panama's status as an international financial center means VPN adoption is common among professionals, and the government has no interest in restricting these tools given their importance to the banking sector.
