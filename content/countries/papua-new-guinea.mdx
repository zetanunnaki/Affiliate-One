## Internet Freedom & Privacy Landscape

Papua New Guinea has not been formally assessed by Freedom House's Freedom on the Net report, but the country maintains a generally open internet without systematic censorship. The primary ISPs are Digicel PNG (the dominant mobile provider) and bmobile-vodafone, with DataCo providing wholesale backbone services via the Coral Sea Cable System connecting to Sydney. Internet penetration remains among the lowest in the Pacific at approximately 30%, with coverage concentrated in Port Moresby, Lae, and Mt Hagen while vast highland and island regions remain largely disconnected.

## VPN Legality

VPN use is legal in Papua New Guinea. There is no legislation restricting or regulating VPN technology. Users are free to encrypt their connections for personal privacy, business security, or any other lawful purpose without risk of penalties.

## Server Coverage & Speed Performance

No VPN provider operates servers within Papua New Guinea. The nearest server locations are Australia (Sydney), connected via the Coral Sea Cable System that provides the bulk of PNG's international bandwidth. Users can expect latency of 60-90ms to Sydney servers, which is acceptable for web browsing, standard-definition streaming, and voice calls. NordVPN and Surfshark both operate extensive Australian server networks (50+ servers) that serve PNG users well. Singapore servers offer an alternative path with similar latency.

## Best VPN Protocols for Papua New Guinea

WireGuard is strongly recommended for Papua New Guinea due to its efficiency on bandwidth-constrained connections. The protocol's lower overhead compared to OpenVPN means less wasted bandwidth on a network where every kilobyte matters. IKEv2 is a good alternative for mobile users on Digicel's network, offering fast reconnection when signal quality fluctuates. Since PNG does not employ deep packet inspection or VPN blocking, obfuscation features are not needed under normal circumstances.
