## Internet Freedom & Privacy Landscape

Paraguay scores 63 out of 100 on Freedom House's Freedom on the Net index, placing it in the "Partly Free" category. While the government does not engage in systematic censorship, concerns exist around online harassment of journalists, occasional content removal requests, and proposed surveillance legislation. The telecommunications market is competitive, served by Personal (Telecom Argentina subsidiary), Tigo (Millicom), and Claro (America Movil), with robust competition driving improving service quality. Internet penetration stands around 72%, with mobile data dominating access. Asuncion and Ciudad del Este have strong 4G coverage, while the Chaco region (western Paraguay) remains vastly underserved with minimal connectivity across its sparse population.

## VPN Legality

VPNs are fully legal in Paraguay. There are no laws restricting VPN usage, and the government does not block VPN protocols or interfere with encrypted communications. Users can freely install and use any VPN service without legal concerns.

## Server Coverage & Speed Performance

A small number of VPN providers offer servers in Paraguay, though most users connect to nearby servers in Brazil (Sao Paulo) or Argentina (Buenos Aires) for better performance and more server options. Tigo and Personal 4G deliver 25-45 Mbps in Asuncion and Ciudad del Este, with VPN connections to Buenos Aires or Sao Paulo adding 30-50ms latency. This provides excellent performance for streaming, gaming, video calls, and large downloads. The competitive three-operator market has driven network improvements, making Paraguay's mobile speeds above average for the region.

## Best VPN Protocols for Paraguay

All standard VPN protocols work perfectly in Paraguay. WireGuard is the recommended default for its superior speed on mobile connections, especially when switching between Tigo, Personal, and Claro towers. OpenVPN (UDP) provides reliable performance across all network types. IKEv2 handles network transitions well for users in vehicles or moving between Wi-Fi and cellular connections in urban areas.
