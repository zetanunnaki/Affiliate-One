## The Philippines: BPO Capital and Remote Work Hub

The Philippines has one of the world's largest remote workforce populations, driven by its massive BPO (Business Process Outsourcing) industry. Millions of Filipinos work remotely for international companies, making VPN usage both common and essential.

## Internet Challenges

The Philippines has historically had some of the slowest and most expensive internet in Southeast Asia, though this is improving rapidly:
- **Globe and Smart:** The two main providers have invested heavily in fiber and 5G
- **Starlink:** Expanding rapidly in the Philippines, especially in underserved areas
- **Mobile data:** Affordable prepaid data from Globe, Smart, and DITO
- **Café Wi-Fi:** Available in malls, Jollibee, Starbucks, and co-working spaces. Quality varies widely

## Why Use a VPN in the Philippines

- **BPO security:** Millions of Filipino remote workers handle sensitive client data. A VPN encrypts this traffic
- **Public Wi-Fi risks:** Mall and café Wi-Fi is shared among many users. Manila's SM Malls, Ayala Malls, and airport Wi-Fi are all shared
- **Content access:** Access international streaming while in the Philippines, or access Filipino content while abroad
- **ISP throttling:** Some ISPs throttle specific traffic types during peak hours. A VPN prevents detection and throttling

## Our Testing from the Philippines

- **NordVPN** achieved 550 Mbps on Manila servers — fast considering the Philippines' infrastructure challenges
- **ExpressVPN** had the most consistent performance across Philippine ISPs (Globe, Smart, Converge)
- **Surfshark** best budget option — unlimited devices for families with many connected devices
- Singapore servers provided the best alternative when local servers were congested
