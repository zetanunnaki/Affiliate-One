## Internet Freedom & Privacy Landscape

The Philippines scores **63/100** on Freedom House's Freedom on the Net index, placing it in the "Partly Free" category. While the Philippines does not maintain China-style internet censorship, the government has used digital platforms to spread disinformation, harass journalists, and suppress critical voices through coordinated online attacks and legal intimidation. Major ISPs including PLDT, Globe Telecom, Converge ICT, and Sky Cable operate in a market that has historically delivered some of Southeast Asia's slowest and most expensive internet, though massive infrastructure investments are improving this rapidly.

The Philippines enacted the SIM Registration Act (Republic Act No. 11934) in 2022, requiring all mobile users to register their SIM cards with valid government identification — linking mobile numbers and associated internet activity to real identities. The Cybercrime Prevention Act of 2012 grants authorities broad powers to investigate online activities, and the National Telecommunications Commission (NTC) can order ISPs to block content. The country's Anti-Terrorism Act of 2020 expanded surveillance powers with provisions that civil liberties organizations have challenged as overly broad. PLDT and Globe, which together control approximately 80% of the fixed and mobile market, retain connection metadata under telecommunications regulations.

## VPN Legality

VPN use is completely legal in the Philippines with no restrictions. There is no legislation that prohibits or limits VPN services, and they are widely used across the massive BPO industry for securing client data, by individual users for privacy, and by businesses for corporate network access. The government has never proposed restricting VPN access, and VPN usage is mainstream among tech-literate Filipinos. There are no gray areas — using a VPN is standard practice in the Philippines' large remote work ecosystem.

## Why You Need a VPN in the Philippines

- **SIM Registration Act surveillance** — Mandatory SIM registration links your mobile number and associated internet activity to your government-issued ID, creating a comprehensive digital identity trail that a VPN helps protect by encrypting browsing data at the network level
- **BPO data security** — Over 1.5 million Filipino workers handle sensitive client data including HIPAA-regulated healthcare records, financial information, and personal data, requiring VPN encryption to satisfy international compliance requirements
- **Public Wi-Fi risks** — SM Malls, Ayala Malls, Robinsons, Jollibee, airport Wi-Fi at NAIA terminals, and hotel networks are shared among thousands of users simultaneously, creating prime attack vectors for credential theft and man-in-the-middle attacks
- **ISP throttling** — PLDT and Globe have been documented throttling specific traffic types during peak hours, particularly streaming and gaming, which VPN encryption prevents by hiding traffic signatures from ISP classification systems
- **Anti-Terrorism Act provisions** — The 2020 law expanded government surveillance powers with reduced judicial oversight requirements, allowing broader monitoring of digital communications that a no-logs VPN jurisdiction protects against

VPN overhead is more significant on slower connections, making lightweight WireGuard protocols essential. Performance on mobile networks showed 20-25% speed loss, making protocol efficiency critical for the millions of Filipinos who rely primarily on mobile data.

## Best VPN Protocols for Philippines

The Philippines does not employ deep packet inspection or VPN blocking, so protocol selection focuses on maximizing limited bandwidth. WireGuard-based protocols are essential given the country's variable connection speeds — the lightweight protocol minimizes overhead on both fiber and mobile connections. NordLynx (NordVPN's WireGuard implementation) delivered the best results across all tested networks. OpenVPN (UDP) works reliably but adds measurable overhead on connections below 100 Mbps, making it better suited for fiber users in Metro Manila and Cebu. IKEv2/IPsec excels on Globe and Smart mobile networks where frequent tower handoffs occur in densely populated areas. For users in Visayas and Mindanao where terrestrial infrastructure is limited and Starlink is expanding, WireGuard's minimal overhead preserves precious bandwidth that heavier protocols would consume.
