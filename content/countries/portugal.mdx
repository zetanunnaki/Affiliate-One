## Portugal: The Digital Nomad Capital of Europe

Portugal has emerged as perhaps the most popular destination for remote workers and digital nomads in Europe. Affordable cost of living, excellent weather, widespread English proficiency, a dedicated D7 visa for remote workers, and strong internet infrastructure make it an ideal base for location-independent professionals.

## The Lisbon and Porto Tech Scenes

- **Lisbon:** Dozens of co-working spaces (Second Home, Heden, Outsite), vibrant startup ecosystem (Web Summit moved here), excellent café culture with reliable Wi-Fi. Fiber from MEO and NOS delivers 500 Mbps+ in most areas
- **Porto:** Growing tech hub with more affordable pricing than Lisbon. Porto Digital district, strong university-linked innovation. Excellent co-working options
- **Algarve:** Beach-based remote work with growing digital nomad infrastructure. Slower internet in some coastal areas but improving
- **Madeira:** Ponta do Sol established one of Europe's first digital nomad villages with dedicated co-working and community infrastructure

## Portuguese Digital Infrastructure

- **MEO, NOS, Vodafone:** Competitive fiber market with speeds up to 10 Gbps in some areas. Pricing is reasonable (€30-50/month for 500 Mbps+)
- **MB Way:** Portugal's dominant mobile payment app, accepted everywhere. Works alongside international cards
- **GDPR enforcement:** Portugal's CNPD enforces GDPR actively, providing strong consumer data protection
