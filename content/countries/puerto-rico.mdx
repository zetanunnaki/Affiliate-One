## Internet Freedom & Privacy Landscape

As a US territory, Puerto Rico falls under US federal internet regulations and is not separately assessed by Freedom House. The island enjoys the same internet freedoms as mainland United States, protected by the First Amendment and FCC oversight. Major ISPs include Liberty Puerto Rico (formerly Liberty Cablevision), Claro (owned by America Movil), and T-Mobile, offering fiber, cable, and 5G services. Internet penetration exceeds 80% in the San Juan metropolitan area, though rural mountain communities and smaller islands like Vieques and Culebra sometimes face connectivity challenges, particularly after hurricane season.

## VPN Legality

VPN use is completely legal in Puerto Rico, governed by the same US laws that apply on the mainland. There are no restrictions on encryption technology, and both residents and visitors can use VPN services without any legal concerns.

## Server Coverage & Speed Performance

Puerto Rico benefits from excellent VPN server proximity, with major providers operating servers in Miami (approximately 1,600 km away) and across the US East Coast. NordVPN and Surfshark both maintain large server fleets in Miami and Atlanta, delivering latency of 25-45ms from San Juan. Some providers also offer servers in nearby Caribbean locations. The island's fiber infrastructure from Liberty ensures that VPN connections rarely bottleneck local speeds, with users regularly achieving 200+ Mbps through WireGuard-based protocols.

## Best VPN Protocols for Puerto Rico

WireGuard delivers the best performance on Puerto Rico's modern fiber and 5G networks, maintaining near-native speeds with minimal CPU overhead. OpenVPN (UDP) remains a solid alternative for compatibility with older routers and corporate VPN setups common among Act 60 businesses. Since the US does not block VPN traffic or employ deep packet inspection on consumer connections, obfuscation is unnecessary for normal use.
