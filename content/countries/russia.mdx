## Internet Freedom & Privacy Landscape

Russia scores 21/100 on Freedom House's Freedom on the Net index, classified as Not Free. The internet environment has deteriorated dramatically since 2022, with Russia now operating one of the world's most extensive censorship and surveillance systems outside of China and North Korea. The combination of the Yarovaya Law (2016), the Sovereign Internet Law (2019), and post-2022 wartime censorship measures has created a layered system of control over online communications.

Local ISPs such as Rostelecom, MTS, Beeline, and MegaFon are required by the Yarovaya Law to retain all communications metadata for three years and actual communications content for six months. ISPs must provide the FSB (Federal Security Service) with real-time access to traffic through SORM (System for Operative Investigative Activities) equipment installed directly on provider networks. The Sovereign Internet Law further requires ISPs to install deep packet inspection (DPI) equipment supplied by Roskomnadzor, enabling both traffic filtering and protocol-level blocking.

Russia operates entirely outside Western intelligence frameworks and has built its own comprehensive domestic surveillance infrastructure. The FSB, GRU, and SVR maintain extensive signals intelligence capabilities focused on both domestic and international targets. Internet traffic within Russia should be assumed to be monitored at multiple levels.

## VPN Legality

The legal situation surrounding VPNs in Russia is complex and deliberately ambiguous. Using a VPN is not explicitly criminalized for individuals. However, VPN providers are required by law to register with Roskomnadzor and connect to the government's registry of blocked websites, effectively requiring them to enforce censorship. Providers that refuse are themselves blocked. Most reputable international VPN providers have been added to the blocklist. Accessing blocked content through any means is technically illegal under Russian law, though enforcement has targeted providers and platforms rather than individual users. The legal environment is unpredictable and subject to rapid change.

## Why You Need a VPN in Russia

Russia's extensive internet censorship makes a VPN essential for accessing the uncensored internet.

- **Accessing blocked platforms** — Instagram, Facebook, and many news outlets have been blocked since 2022. Twitter/X is heavily throttled. YouTube faces increasing restrictions. A VPN is the only way to access these platforms from within Russia.
- **Securing communications** — With SORM equipment providing FSB real-time access to ISP traffic, a VPN encrypts your communications before they reach ISP infrastructure. This is critical for journalists, activists, business professionals, and anyone communicating with contacts outside Russia.
- **Avoiding ISP throttling** — Roskomnadzor has directed ISPs to throttle specific services (notably Twitter/X) as a pressure tactic. DPI-based throttling targets unencrypted protocol signatures that a VPN obscures.
- **Bypassing censorship** — Thousands of websites are blocked through Roskomnadzor's registry, including independent media, human rights organizations, and opposition political content. The scope of blocking expands regularly.

## Server Coverage & Speed Performance

Russia has strong domestic internet infrastructure with fiber widely available in major cities. Rostelecom's network delivers 200-500 Mbps in Moscow and St. Petersburg. However, VPN performance is constrained by DPI interference, server distance (most VPN servers used from Russia are in Europe), and the overhead of obfuscation protocols needed to evade blocking. Testing was conducted on a 300 Mbps Rostelecom connection in Moscow.

Speed loss is significantly higher than in unrestricted countries due to obfuscation overhead and DPI interference. Connection reliability varies by time of day, current enforcement intensity, and protocol choice. Finnish servers typically offer the best latency from Moscow and St. Petersburg, while German and Dutch servers provide more stable long-term connections. Off-peak hours (2-7 AM Moscow time) generally yield better VPN connection success rates, as DPI enforcement intensity appears to fluctuate. Users on MTS fiber in Moscow report slightly better VPN reliability than Rostelecom subscribers, likely due to routing differences through less heavily monitored exchange points.

## Best VPN Protocols for Russia

Protocol selection is critical in Russia. Standard WireGuard and OpenVPN connections are detected and blocked by Roskomnadzor's DPI systems. Only obfuscated protocols reliably penetrate the censorship infrastructure.

NordVPN's obfuscated servers (based on OpenVPN with XOR scrambling) offer the best connection success rate, working approximately 6 out of 10 attempts. Surfshark's NoBorders mode automatically activates censorship circumvention but requires periodic manual protocol switching when blocks update. Proton VPN's Stealth protocol (based on TLS-wrapped WireGuard) shows promise but remains inconsistent. As a backup strategy, manually configured Shadowsocks or V2Ray connections through a personal server sometimes succeed when commercial VPN protocols are actively blocked. Always maintain multiple configured providers and protocols, as blocking patterns change frequently.

The large server fleet across Finland, Netherlands, and Germany provides fallback options when specific endpoints are blocked. Download the app and configure obfuscated mode before entering Russia, as the NordVPN website is blocked domestically.

Unlimited device connections mean every household device can be protected under one subscription. Stealth protocol development is actively prioritized given the Russian market's importance. Secure Core routing ensures that even if a single server is compromised, traffic cannot be traced back to the user. Recommended for journalists, activists, and anyone requiring maximum privacy guarantees despite slightly lower reliability.

While connection reliability is lower than the top three providers in heavily censored environments, FastestVPN's lifetime pricing makes it a viable backup option to maintain alongside a primary provider, ensuring you always have an alternate configuration ready when primary VPN endpoints are blocked.
