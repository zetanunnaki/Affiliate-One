## Internet Freedom & Privacy Landscape

Saint Martin (the French northern half of the island shared with Dutch Sint Maarten) operates as a French overseas collectivity, benefiting from France's robust telecommunications regulations and EU-aligned digital privacy standards including GDPR protections. The telecommunications market is served by Orange Caraibe and Dauphin Telecom, which provide 4G mobile services and fixed broadband through fiber and DSL connections. Internet penetration is high at approximately 75%, reflecting French infrastructure investment. The island has rebuilt much of its telecommunications network following Hurricane Irma in 2017, resulting in modernized equipment. Marigot and the beach resort areas along Orient Bay and Grand Case have strong connectivity, while hillside residential areas may experience variable coverage.

## VPN Legality

VPNs are fully legal in Saint Martin. As French territory, the island follows French and EU digital rights frameworks that explicitly protect citizens' rights to use encryption and privacy tools. No restrictions of any kind apply to VPN usage.

## Server Coverage & Speed Performance

No VPN providers operate servers directly on Saint Martin. The nearest options include French Caribbean servers (Martinique or Guadeloupe, offered by some providers), Miami (US), and mainland France (Paris). Orange Caraibe 4G delivers 20-40 Mbps across the French side, with VPN connections to Miami adding 55-75ms latency. Connections to Paris add 100-130ms but may be preferred for accessing French content and services. The island's post-Hurricane Irma infrastructure rebuild incorporated modern submarine cable connections, providing stable international bandwidth. Overall VPN performance is reliable for streaming, video calls, and general internet use.

## Best VPN Protocols for Saint Martin

All VPN protocols work without any restrictions on Saint Martin. WireGuard delivers the best performance for both speed and battery life on mobile devices used throughout the day at the beach and in town. OpenVPN (UDP) provides broader compatibility for older devices. IKEv2 handles the frequent Wi-Fi-to-cellular transitions common when moving between beach bars, restaurants, and resort areas.
