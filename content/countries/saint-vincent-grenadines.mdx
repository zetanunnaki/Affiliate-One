## Internet Freedom & Privacy Landscape

Saint Vincent and the Grenadines (SVG) is a Caribbean island nation of approximately 110,000 people comprising the main island of Saint Vincent and a chain of smaller Grenadine islands stretching southward. The country operates as a parliamentary democracy with strong press freedom traditions and no history of internet censorship. Flow (Liberty Latin America) and Digicel serve as the primary telecommunications providers, offering mobile, fixed broadband, and fiber services. The National Telecommunications Regulatory Commission (NTRC) oversees the sector. Internet penetration exceeds 70% on the main island, though some smaller Grenadine islands rely on limited wireless connections. The Eastern Caribbean Fiber System (ECFS) submarine cable provides international connectivity. There is no content filtering, government surveillance, or politically-motivated internet restrictions.

## VPN Legality

VPNs are completely legal in Saint Vincent and the Grenadines. The country's British-derived legal framework protects digital privacy, and there are no restrictions on encryption tools, VPN software, or privacy technologies.

## Why You Need a VPN in Saint Vincent and the Grenadines

SVG's Caribbean island environment and growing tourism sector create practical needs for VPN protection.

- **Secure resort and marina Wi-Fi** — The Grenadine islands (Bequia, Mustique, Canouan, Union Island) attract yacht charters and luxury tourism; resort and marina Wi-Fi networks are shared among many guests on basic infrastructure vulnerable to interception
- **Access home-country streaming** — Tourists from the US, UK, and Canada visiting SVG need VPN connections to home-country servers to access their streaming subscriptions and banking platforms blocked by Caribbean geo-restrictions
- **Privacy from concentrated ISPs** — With only Flow and Digicel serving the market, browsing activity is concentrated through limited providers; VPN encryption ensures privacy regardless of ISP data retention practices

## Server Coverage & Speed Performance

No VPN provider operates servers in Saint Vincent and the Grenadines. The nearest options are in Trinidad and Tobago, Barbados, and the US East Coast (Miami). The ECFS submarine cable connects SVG to the wider Caribbean and onward to the US and Europe. Baseline speeds on Flow fiber in Kingstown average 20-80 Mbps, with 4G mobile delivering 10-30 Mbps on the main island. Grenadine islands receive lower speeds through inter-island links. VPN connections to Miami servers add 40-60ms latency with 10-20% throughput reduction. Caribbean regional servers (where available) offer 20-35ms latency. Performance is generally sufficient for HD streaming, video calls, and standard internet use when connected to Miami or regional servers.

## Best VPN Protocols for Saint Vincent and the Grenadines

No censorship or protocol restrictions exist in SVG. Users should select protocols based on their connection quality. WireGuard delivers the fastest performance on Flow fiber connections on the main island. IKEv2 handles transitions between Digicel mobile and resort Wi-Fi networks effectively — particularly valuable for yacht visitors moving between islands. OpenVPN (UDP) provides reliable performance for remote workers requiring corporate VPN compatibility.
