## Internet Freedom & Privacy Landscape

San Marino is a microstate of approximately 34,000 residents entirely surrounded by Italy. The republic does not have its own Freedom on the Net rating, but its digital environment mirrors Italy's (scored 76/100, Free). San Marino Telecom is the primary ISP, providing fixed-line fiber and ADSL services, while mobile connectivity piggybacks on Italian networks from TIM and Vodafone through roaming agreements. Internet penetration exceeds 90%, with quality broadband widely available across the compact 61-square-kilometer territory. While San Marino is not an EU member, it has adopted GDPR-equivalent data protection standards through bilateral agreements, and its citizens frequently use Italian digital services subject to EU regulations.

## VPN Legality

VPNs are entirely legal in San Marino. There are no restrictions on encryption, privacy tools, or VPN protocols. The republic's legal framework aligns with European standards on digital rights, and neither San Marino Telecom nor Italian mobile operators interfere with VPN traffic in any way.

## Why You Need a VPN in San Marino

San Marino presents no censorship challenges, but its unique position as a non-EU microstate relying heavily on Italian infrastructure creates specific privacy and access considerations for residents and visitors.

- **Access EU-only digital services** — Some EU digital platforms and government services restrict access to EU member states; connecting through Italian or other EU servers resolves these blocks for San Marino-based users
- **Public Wi-Fi protection** — Tourist areas around the historic center, Mount Titano, and museums offer shared networks where encryption prevents credential theft and session hijacking
- **Streaming geo-restrictions** — Residents and visitors wanting access to content libraries from their home countries need VPN servers in their origin region to bypass geographic licensing restrictions

## Server Coverage & Speed Performance

No VPN provider operates servers specifically within San Marino due to its tiny population and market size. However, Italian servers — particularly those in nearby Bologna, Milan, and Rome — provide excellent performance with latency under 10ms. NordVPN operates 60+ Italian servers, Surfshark offers multiple Italian locations, and Proton VPN maintains servers in Rome and Milan. Given San Marino's robust fiber infrastructure through San Marino Telecom, users can expect baseline speeds of 50-200 Mbps, with VPN connections reducing throughput by only 5-15% when connecting to Italian servers.

## Best VPN Protocols for San Marino

With no censorship or DPI concerns, users should prioritize speed-optimized protocols. WireGuard provides the fastest performance with modern cryptographic standards, making it ideal for streaming and large file transfers. OpenVPN (UDP) offers strong compatibility with corporate networks, while IKEv2 excels for mobile users transitioning between San Marino Telecom Wi-Fi and Italian cellular networks without connection drops.
