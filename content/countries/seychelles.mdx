## Internet Freedom & Privacy Landscape

Seychelles has not been assessed by Freedom House for internet freedom, but the island nation generally maintains an open internet environment consistent with its democratic governance. The telecommunications market is served primarily by Cable & Wireless Seychelles (now operating as Intelvision) and Airtel Seychelles. Cable & Wireless provides the primary fixed-line broadband and fiber infrastructure, while Airtel dominates mobile communications. Internet connectivity is well-established on the three main islands of Mahe, Praslin, and La Digue, supported by submarine cable connections that provide reasonable international bandwidth. The Seychelles' offshore financial services sector has driven investment in telecommunications infrastructure, resulting in better connectivity than many comparably-sized island nations. Internet access drops significantly on outer islands, many of which are uninhabited nature reserves.

## VPN Legality

VPNs are fully legal in Seychelles. There are no laws restricting VPN use, and the country's liberal approach to internet governance extends to encrypted communications and privacy tools.

## Server Coverage & Speed Performance

No VPN providers operate servers in Seychelles. The nearest available server locations are in South Africa (Johannesburg), India (Mumbai/Chennai), and the United Arab Emirates (Dubai). South African servers typically offer the best latency at 40-70ms from Mahe, as Seychelles' submarine cable routes connect southward along the East African coast. Cable & Wireless fiber subscribers can expect VPN speeds of 20-50 Mbps, adequate for HD streaming and video conferencing. Airtel mobile users on 4G will see speeds of 8-20 Mbps through a VPN. Connectivity on Praslin and La Digue is slightly slower than Mahe but still serviceable for encrypted browsing.

## Best VPN Protocols for Seychelles

WireGuard provides the optimal experience for Seychelles users, delivering fast encrypted connections to regional servers without noticeable performance degradation. The protocol's quick session establishment is particularly beneficial for mobile users moving between resort Wi-Fi zones and cellular coverage. IKEv2 serves as an excellent mobile alternative with seamless network switching capabilities when traveling between islands by ferry.
