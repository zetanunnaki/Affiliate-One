## Internet Freedom & Privacy Landscape

Sierra Leone scores 56 out of 100 in Freedom House's Freedom on the Net report, classified as Partly Free. While the country generally respects online expression, the government has imposed internet shutdowns during elections and political tensions, and the 2020 Cybercrime Act introduced concerning provisions regarding online speech. The primary ISPs are Africell (the dominant mobile operator) and Orange Sierra Leone, providing 4G LTE coverage in Freetown and major towns like Bo, Kenema, and Makeni. Internet penetration is approximately 22%, with mobile data accounting for nearly all connections in this English-speaking West African nation.

## VPN Legality

VPN use is legal in Sierra Leone. There are no laws specifically prohibiting VPN technology, and citizens and visitors can use encrypted connections freely. However, users should be aware that the Cybercrime Act contains broad provisions about online communication that could theoretically be applied in political contexts.

## Server Coverage & Speed Performance

No major VPN provider operates servers within Sierra Leone. The nearest server locations are in Europe (London, UK) and South Africa (Johannesburg), connected via the ACE submarine cable that lands in Freetown. UK servers deliver the best performance due to Sierra Leone's historical Commonwealth ties and direct submarine cable routing, with latency of 80-120ms. This is adequate for browsing, messaging, voice calls, and standard-definition streaming. NordVPN operates hundreds of UK servers, while Surfshark provides strong coverage across both UK and South African locations.

## Best VPN Protocols for Sierra Leone

WireGuard is recommended for daily use on Africell and Orange mobile networks, minimizing overhead on bandwidth-constrained 4G connections where typical speeds range from 5-20 Mbps. During election periods or political shutdowns, obfuscated protocols become critical. OpenVPN over TCP port 443 provides baseline circumvention, while NordVPN's obfuscated servers and Surfshark's Camouflage mode offer more sophisticated DPI bypass capabilities. Users should maintain obfuscation-ready configurations given the unpredictable timing of Sierra Leone's internet restrictions.
