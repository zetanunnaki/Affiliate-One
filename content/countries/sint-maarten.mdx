## Internet Freedom & Privacy Landscape

Sint Maarten (the Dutch southern portion of the island shared with French Saint Martin) is a constituent country within the Kingdom of the Netherlands, benefiting from Dutch-standard digital rights and telecommunications regulations. The market is served by United Telecommunication Services (UTS, operating as Chippie) and TelEm Group (the local government-owned provider), offering 4G mobile and fixed broadband services. Internet penetration exceeds 70%, with strong coverage across the compact 34-square-kilometer territory. Philipsburg (the capital), Simpson Bay, and the Maho Beach area have excellent connectivity supported by submarine cable connections. The island's post-Hurricane Irma rebuild modernized much of the network infrastructure.

## VPN Legality

VPNs are fully legal in Sint Maarten. Under Dutch Caribbean legal frameworks, there are no restrictions on the use of encryption tools or VPN services. The government does not engage in internet censorship or protocol blocking of any kind.

## Server Coverage & Speed Performance

No VPN providers maintain servers on Sint Maarten. The primary server options are Miami (United States) and Amsterdam (Netherlands), with Miami providing lower latency for most use cases. UTS and TelEm 4G networks deliver 25-50 Mbps across the island, with VPN connections to Miami adding 45-65ms latency. Connections to Amsterdam add 110-140ms but may be preferred for accessing Dutch content or services. The island's rebuilt submarine cable infrastructure provides reliable international bandwidth with minimal packet loss. Performance easily supports HD streaming, video calls, and all standard internet activities.

## Best VPN Protocols for Sint Maarten

All VPN protocols operate without restriction on Sint Maarten. WireGuard provides the fastest connections for maximizing the island's solid infrastructure. OpenVPN (UDP) offers reliable performance with wide device compatibility. IKEv2 is particularly useful for cruise visitors and beachgoers who constantly switch between cellular and various Wi-Fi hotspots throughout the day.
