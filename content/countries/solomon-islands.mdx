## Internet Freedom & Privacy Landscape

The Solomon Islands is a Pacific archipelago of over 900 islands with approximately 720,000 people. The country operates as a parliamentary democracy with generally free press protections, though political tensions occasionally spill into the digital space. Our Telekom Solomon Islands is the dominant telecommunications provider, operating both fixed and mobile services, while Bemobile offers competing mobile coverage. The Coral Sea Cable System, completed in 2020 with Australian government funding, connects Honiara to Sydney and has dramatically improved speeds. Internet penetration remains around 35%, heavily concentrated in the capital Honiara on Guadalcanal island. Outer provinces rely on satellite or limited mobile coverage. The government has occasionally discussed social media regulation following political unrest but has not implemented systematic censorship.

## VPN Legality

VPNs are legal in the Solomon Islands. There are no laws restricting encryption tools, VPN software, or privacy technologies. The government has not taken any enforcement actions against VPN users, and the technology is commonly used by international organizations, NGOs, and businesses operating in the country.

## Why You Need a VPN in Solomon Islands

The Solomon Islands' developing digital infrastructure and concentrated ISP market create practical security and access needs.

- **Secure shared connections** — Honiara's hotels, cafes, and the few public Wi-Fi points operate on basic network infrastructure where traffic interception is straightforward; VPN encryption provides essential protection
- **Access international content** — Solomon Islands IP addresses are rarely recognized by streaming platforms, often resulting in minimal content libraries or outright blocks; connecting to Australian servers unlocks full catalogs
- **Privacy from concentrated ISP** — With Our Telekom dominating the market, all browsing activity passes through a single provider; VPN encryption prevents centralized traffic logging and potential government data requests

## Server Coverage & Speed Performance

No VPN provider operates servers in the Solomon Islands. The nearest locations are in Australia (Sydney), Papua New Guinea, and Fiji. The Coral Sea Cable System provides direct connectivity to Sydney, making Australian servers the optimal choice. Baseline speeds in Honiara have improved significantly since the cable's activation, averaging 10-30 Mbps on Our Telekom fiber and 4G connections. VPN connections to Sydney servers add 30-50ms latency with 15-25% throughput reduction. Outer islands still relying on satellite experience 400ms+ base latency where VPN overhead becomes more noticeable. The direct cable to Australia means Sydney consistently delivers the best VPN performance from Honiara.

## Best VPN Protocols for Solomon Islands

No censorship or protocol restrictions exist, allowing users to optimize purely for performance. WireGuard provides the fastest experience, minimizing overhead on the relatively new Coral Sea cable link. IKEv2 handles transitions between Our Telekom mobile and fixed networks smoothly. OpenVPN (UDP) offers corporate compatibility for international organizations operating in the country. For outer island satellite users, lighter protocols like WireGuard are particularly important given the severe bandwidth constraints.
