## Internet Freedom & Privacy Landscape

Somalia lacks a Freedom House Freedom on the Net rating due to its complex governance situation, but the internet environment reflects decades of state collapse and ongoing conflict. Paradoxically, the absence of centralized government control has created a surprisingly competitive private telecommunications sector. Hormuud Telecom, Golis Telecom, and Telesom dominate the market, offering mobile data services that reach much of the population. Internet penetration has grown to approximately 20%, primarily through mobile connections. However, the Al-Shabaab militant group controls significant territory and imposes its own internet restrictions in areas under its authority, including complete bans on internet access in some regions. The federal government and regional authorities also conduct surveillance and occasionally order shutdowns during military operations or political crises.

## VPN Legality

Somalia has no formal legislation regulating VPN usage, and the technology is widely used by businesses, international organizations, and diaspora-connected Somalis. However, in Al-Shabaab-controlled areas, any unauthorized communications technology can attract severe punishment. In government-controlled areas including Mogadishu, VPN use is common and unrestricted, with many users relying on VPNs to access VoIP services that some operators have throttled for commercial reasons.

## Why You Need a VPN in Somalia

The combination of multiple armed actors conducting surveillance, commercially-motivated throttling, and unpredictable shutdowns makes VPN protection essential.

- **Bypass VoIP throttling** — Somali ISPs frequently throttle or block VoIP services like WhatsApp calls and Skype to protect their lucrative international calling revenue; VPN encryption defeats protocol-specific throttling
- **Protection from surveillance** — Both government intelligence services and non-state actors monitor communications on Somali networks; encrypted VPN tunnels prevent interception that could endanger users
- **Access during partial shutdowns** — During military operations against Al-Shabaab, authorities order targeted social media blocks and communication platform restrictions that VPNs can circumvent

## Server Coverage & Speed Performance

No VPN provider operates servers within Somalia. The nearest server locations are in Kenya (Nairobi), Djibouti, and the UAE (Dubai). Somalia's mobile-first internet delivers speeds averaging 5-20 Mbps on 4G connections in Mogadishu and other major cities, though consistency varies. Hormuud's 4G network in Mogadishu offers the best baseline performance. VPN connections to Kenyan servers add 15-30% overhead with latency of 40-80ms, while UAE servers deliver 60-120ms latency. For users in northern Somaliland, connecting through Djibouti servers may provide slightly lower latency due to submarine cable routing.

## Best VPN Protocols for Somalia

Standard VPN protocols generally function without interference on most Somali networks, as ISPs lack sophisticated DPI infrastructure. WireGuard and OpenVPN operate reliably on Hormuud, Golis, and Telesom networks during normal conditions. However, during government-ordered shutdowns or throttling events, obfuscated protocols provide better reliability. Having Stealth or Camouflage modes pre-configured ensures connectivity when standard protocols face interference. IKEv2 is particularly effective for maintaining connections on Somali mobile networks with frequent tower handoffs.
