## South Africa: The Remote Work Hub of Africa

South Africa is the most developed internet market on the African continent. Cape Town and Johannesburg have thriving tech scenes with world-class co-working spaces. However, unique challenges like load shedding (rolling blackouts) and historically high data costs affect the remote work experience.

## Load Shedding Impact

Eskom's rolling blackouts directly affect internet connectivity:
- **Fiber routers lose power** during load shedding stages
- **Mobile towers** may have battery backup for 4-8 hours depending on stage
- **UPS (Uninterruptible Power Supply):** Essential for remote workers — keeps your router and laptop running during outages
- **Starlink and Fibre:** Growing alternatives, though both require power

## Data Privacy: POPIA

South Africa's Protection of Personal Information Act (POPIA) is Africa's most comprehensive data protection law, modeled on GDPR. It gives South African citizens rights over their personal data and imposes obligations on organizations. Despite POPIA, a VPN adds an important encryption layer that ISPs cannot bypass.
