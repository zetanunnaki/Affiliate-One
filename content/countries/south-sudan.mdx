## Internet Freedom & Privacy Landscape

South Sudan has not been assessed specifically for internet freedom by Freedom House, but it is rated "Not Free" in the broader Freedom in the World index. The government has imposed internet shutdowns and social media blocks during periods of political crisis and civil unrest. The telecommunications market is served by three mobile operators: Zain South Sudan (the market leader), MTN South Sudan, and Vivacell (owned by Lebanese group Digicell). Internet access is extremely limited and concentrated almost exclusively in Juba, with the vast majority of the country having no connectivity due to ongoing conflict, destroyed infrastructure, and extreme poverty. Mobile 3G through Zain and MTN provides the primary internet access for the small online population. Journalists and media outlets face severe repression, surveillance, and threats.

## VPN Legality

VPNs are legal in South Sudan, though the government has imposed internet shutdowns that can block all traffic including VPN connections. There are no specific anti-VPN laws, but the repressive environment means users should exercise caution.

## Best VPN Protocols for South Sudan

WireGuard is critical in South Sudan's extremely constrained bandwidth environment, where protocol efficiency directly impacts usability. During internet restrictions, obfuscated protocols (NordVPN obfuscated servers, Surfshark Camouflage, Proton Stealth) become essential for bypassing government blocks. Users should pre-install VPN apps and download manual configuration files before traveling to South Sudan, as app stores may be inaccessible during shutdowns.
