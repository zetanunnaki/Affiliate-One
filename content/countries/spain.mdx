## Internet Freedom & Privacy Landscape

Spain scores 79 out of 100 on the Freedom House Freedom on the Net index, placing it firmly in the "Free" category. Internet access is widespread and largely unrestricted, with over 85% fiber-to-the-home penetration — one of the highest rates in Europe. The market is served by major ISPs including Movistar (Telefonica), Orange, Vodafone, and MasMovil, which collectively provide competitive pricing and gigabit-speed plans in most urban and suburban areas.

However, Spain's intelligence apparatus raises legitimate privacy concerns. The Centro Nacional de Inteligencia (CNI) has broad digital surveillance capabilities under the Ley Organica 2/2002. The 2022 Pegasus spyware scandal revealed that the Spanish government had deployed NSO Group's spyware against dozens of Catalan politicians, journalists, and activists — demonstrating willingness to conduct targeted digital surveillance on domestic soil. Additionally, the Ley 25/2007 de Conservacion de Datos requires Spanish ISPs to retain connection metadata for up to 12 months, meaning your ISP logs which IP addresses you connect to, when, and for how long. The Agencia Espanola de Proteccion de Datos (AEPD) enforces GDPR vigorously against private companies but has limited authority over national security operations.

## VPN Legality

VPN usage is completely legal in Spain. There are no laws restricting, regulating, or requiring registration for VPN services. Spanish citizens and visitors can freely download, install, and use any VPN provider without legal consequence. The only gray area involves using a VPN to access content that is itself illegal — the VPN itself remains legal, but the underlying activity may not be. Spanish law enforcement cannot compel foreign VPN providers to hand over user data without international cooperation agreements, making offshore no-logs providers particularly effective for privacy.

## Why You Need a VPN in Spain

- **Public Wi-Fi protection** — Renfe train stations, airports (El Prat, Barajas, Malaga-Costa del Sol), beach towns, and hundreds of co-working spaces offer open, unencrypted Wi-Fi vulnerable to packet sniffing and man-in-the-middle attacks
- **ISP metadata retention** — Under the Ley 25/2007, Movistar, Orange, Vodafone, and MasMovil retain your connection logs for 12 months, creating a browsing profile accessible to authorities with a court order
- **Digital nomad security** — Spain's digital nomad visa (Law 28/2022) has attracted tens of thousands of remote workers who handle sensitive client data on shared cafe and co-working networks in Barcelona, Valencia, and the Canary Islands
- **Government surveillance concerns** — The Pegasus scandal proved Spanish agencies conduct sophisticated digital surveillance; a VPN with no-logs policy prevents traffic correlation
- **Geo-restricted content access** — Access Spanish streaming services (RTVE, Atresplayer, Mitele) while traveling abroad, or international content libraries not available on Spanish IP addresses
- **Torrenting privacy** — Spanish anti-piracy enforcement has increased through the Seccion Segunda courts; a VPN masks your IP in torrent swarms

## Best VPN Protocols for Spain

Spain has no protocol-level restrictions or deep packet inspection targeting VPN traffic. WireGuard delivers the best performance for most users, offering the highest speeds with minimal overhead on Spain's fast fiber connections. OpenVPN (UDP) remains a reliable fallback and works well on mobile networks from Movistar, Orange, and Vodafone. For users concerned about the CNI's surveillance capabilities following the Pegasus revelations, NordVPN's NordLynx (WireGuard-based) or Proton VPN's Secure Core routing through Switzerland provides additional protection against traffic correlation attacks. IKEv2 is recommended for mobile users who frequently switch between Wi-Fi and 4G/5G networks, as it handles connection changes seamlessly.
