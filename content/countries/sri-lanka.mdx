## Internet Freedom & Privacy Landscape

Sri Lanka scores 49/100 on Freedom House's Freedom on the Net index, placing it in the "Partly Free" category. The government blocked social media platforms during the 2022 economic crisis and imposed internet shutdowns during civil unrest. The Computer Crimes Act and proposed Online Safety Act grant authorities broad powers to restrict content and compel ISPs to block websites without judicial oversight.

Local ISPs: Sri Lanka Telecom (SLT-Mobitel), Dialog Axiata, and Hutch (Hutchison Lanka) are the primary providers. SLT dominates fixed broadband while Dialog leads mobile data. ISPs comply with government blocking orders and have implemented DNS-level filtering of designated websites. Deep packet inspection equipment has been deployed on backbone networks since 2019.

## VPN Legality

VPNs are legal in Sri Lanka and widely used, particularly since the 2022 social media blocks made VPN awareness mainstream. There is no legislation specifically prohibiting VPN use, though the Telecommunications Regulatory Commission has the authority to order ISPs to block specific services. During the April 2022 crisis, some VPN websites were temporarily inaccessible, but VPN connections themselves continued to function.

## Best VPN Protocols for Sri Lanka

WireGuard delivers the best speeds through Sri Lanka's international links, minimizing protocol overhead on already bandwidth-constrained submarine cable connections. During periods of government interference, OpenVPN with TCP port 443 (disguised as HTTPS traffic) provides the most reliable fallback, as authorities have not deployed the deep packet inspection needed to distinguish it from regular encrypted web traffic.
