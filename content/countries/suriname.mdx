## Internet Freedom & Privacy Landscape

Suriname has not been formally assessed by Freedom House for internet freedom, but the country generally enjoys an open internet without government-imposed censorship or systematic content filtering. The telecommunications market is served primarily by Telesur (state-owned Telecommunicatiebedrijf Suriname) for fixed-line services and Digicel for mobile communications. Telesur maintains a dominant position in broadband and landline services, while Digicel has expanded mobile data coverage significantly across the coastal population belt. ## VPN Legality

VPNs are completely legal in Suriname. There are no regulations restricting VPN use, and the government does not block or throttle VPN traffic.

## Server Coverage & Speed Performance

No major VPN providers have servers located in Suriname. The nearest server locations include Brazil (Sao Paulo), Trinidad and Tobago, the Netherlands (Amsterdam, popular due to cultural ties), and the United States (Miami). Miami servers generally offer the lowest latency at 50-70ms from Paramaribo, while Amsterdam servers provide access to Dutch content at higher latency of 130-160ms. Telesur fiber subscribers in Paramaribo can expect VPN speeds of 15-35 Mbps, while Digicel mobile users will see more variable throughput depending on network congestion.

## Best VPN Protocols for Suriname

WireGuard delivers the best performance for Surinamese users, minimizing protocol overhead on connections that are already bandwidth-limited compared to international standards. For users who need to access Netherlands-based services through Amsterdam servers, WireGuard's efficient handling of higher-latency connections provides a smoother experience than OpenVPN. IKEv2 works well as an alternative for mobile users on Digicel's network.
