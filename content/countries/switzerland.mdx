## Switzerland: The Privacy Capital of the World

Switzerland has earned its reputation as the global hub for privacy technology. Proton VPN, ProtonMail, Threema, and numerous privacy-focused companies are headquartered here, drawn by Switzerland's constitutional right to privacy, strong data protection laws (revDSG/nFADP), and political neutrality.

## Why Switzerland Matters for Privacy

- **Constitutional privacy right:** Article 13 of the Swiss Federal Constitution guarantees the right to privacy
- **Not in the EU:** Switzerland is not subject to EU data retention directives, though the revDSG (revised Federal Act on Data Protection) aligns with GDPR principles
- **Not in Five Eyes or Fourteen Eyes:** Switzerland is not part of any intelligence-sharing alliance
- **Strong judicial oversight:** Swiss courts have a high bar for approving surveillance requests
- **Bank secrecy tradition:** Though diminished for tax purposes, Switzerland's culture of privacy extends to digital services

## Swiss Internet Infrastructure

Switzerland has excellent internet infrastructure:
- **Swisscom:** The dominant provider offering fiber with speeds up to 10 Gbps
- **Sunrise, Salt:** Competitive alternatives with strong coverage
- **SBB Wi-Fi:** Swiss Federal Railways offers Wi-Fi on trains, though quality varies
- **Public Wi-Fi:** Available in cities, train stations, and many cafés

## Proton VPN: The Swiss Connection

Proton VPN is headquartered in Geneva, under Swiss law. This means the company benefits from Switzerland's privacy protections and cannot be compelled to log user data by foreign governments. For privacy-conscious users, choosing a Swiss-based VPN provider adds a meaningful layer of legal protection.
