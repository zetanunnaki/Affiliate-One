## Thailand 2026: Nomad Infrastructure Matures

Thailand's digital nomad infrastructure has matured significantly. Chiang Mai, Bangkok, and the islands now offer reliable connectivity, abundant co-working spaces, and a well-established remote work community. The Long-Term Resident visa makes extended stays easier.

## Connectivity Improvements

- **5G rollout:** AIS, DTAC (now merged with True as True Corp), and DTAC expanding 5G in Bangkok and Chiang Mai
- **Fiber expansion:** True and AIS offering 1 Gbps fiber in major cities for under 1,000 THB/month ($30)
- **Café Wi-Fi quality:** Chiang Mai's café scene now competes on Wi-Fi speed as a selling point
- **Starlink:** Available in Thailand since 2024, improving rural connectivity

## Content Landscape Update

- **Lèse-majesté content:** Still strictly blocked and illegal to access
- **Gambling sites:** Blocked (legal reform being debated)
- **Some VPN websites:** Occasionally blocked but VPN apps work
- **Social media:** Generally accessible but monitored during political events

## 2026 Testing Update

- **NordVPN** delivers 720 Mbps on Thai servers — significant improvement over previous years
- **FastestVPN** most consistent across Thai mobile networks including new True Corp merged network
- **FastestVPN** remains the digital nomad favorite at $2.29/mo matching Thailand's affordable lifestyle
