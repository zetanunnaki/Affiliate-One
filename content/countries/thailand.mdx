## Thailand: Digital Nomad Hub with Caveats

Thailand is one of the world's most popular digital nomad destinations, with affordable living, excellent co-working spaces, and generally good internet. However, the government actively monitors and censors internet content under the Computer Crime Act, and certain websites and content are blocked.

## What's Restricted in Thailand

- **Lèse-majesté content:** Content critical of the monarchy is strictly blocked and illegal to access
- **Gambling websites:** Online gambling is illegal in Thailand
- **Some news and political sites:** Government critics and certain media outlets may be blocked
- **Adult content:** Some adult websites are blocked

The restrictions are less comprehensive than countries like China, but they exist. VPN use is legal and extremely common among Thailand's large expat and digital nomad community.

## The Digital Nomad Scene

Thailand's infrastructure for remote workers is excellent, particularly in these cities:

### Bangkok
The capital has world-class co-working spaces (Hubba, The Hive, WeWork), fast fiber internet at cafés, and reliable 5G coverage. Internet speeds at co-working spaces typically range from 100-500 Mbps.

### Chiang Mai
The unofficial digital nomad capital of the world. Dozens of co-working spaces (Punspace, CAMP, Yellow), affordable accommodation, and a massive community of remote workers. Café Wi-Fi speeds are generally good (30-100 Mbps).

### Koh Phangan / Islands
Beach-based co-working is popular on islands, but internet quality drops compared to mainland cities. Beachside cafés may have 10-30 Mbps connections. Having a mobile data backup is important.

### Phuket
Growing digital nomad scene with co-working spaces and reliable internet in tourist areas. Good balance between work and beach lifestyle.

## Internet Infrastructure

- **Home/office broadband:** AIS Fibre, TRUE, and 3BB offer fiber plans up to 1 Gbps in major cities. Pricing is very affordable (500-1,000 THB/month for 100-500 Mbps)
- **Mobile data:** AIS, DTAC, and TRUE offer affordable 4G/5G data packages. Tourist SIM cards are available at airports and 7-Eleven stores
- **Café Wi-Fi:** Most cafés have free Wi-Fi, quality varies from 10-100 Mbps. Some cafés have purchase requirements
- **7-Eleven Wi-Fi:** Available at most of Thailand's 13,000+ stores, useful as a backup

## Why Use a VPN in Thailand

1. **Public Wi-Fi protection:** Cafés and co-working spaces use shared networks. A VPN encrypts your traffic
2. **Content access:** Bypass government blocks on certain websites
3. **Banking security:** Protect online banking sessions on shared networks
4. **Work tool access:** Ensure unrestricted access to all work collaboration tools
5. **Privacy:** Prevent ISP monitoring under the Computer Crime Act
