## Internet Freedom & Privacy Landscape

Togo scores 45 out of 100 on the Freedom House Freedom on the Net index, classifying it as "Partly Free." The government has repeatedly imposed internet shutdowns during politically sensitive periods, including elections and protests, and has used targeted social media throttling to limit the spread of opposition messaging. The telecommunications market is dominated by two mobile operators: Togocel (state-affiliated) and Moov Africa (formerly Moov Togo, owned by Maroc Telecom). Togocel holds the larger market share and operates under significant government influence. Internet access is primarily mobile-based and concentrated in the capital Lome and secondary cities like Kara and Sokode, with rural northern regions experiencing extremely limited connectivity.

## VPN Legality

VPNs are legal in Togo, though the government has imposed internet shutdowns that affect VPN accessibility during periods of political unrest. There are no specific laws prohibiting VPN use, but access may be technically disrupted during shutdowns.

## Server Coverage & Speed Performance

No VPN providers operate servers within Togo. The closest server locations are in Ghana (Accra), Nigeria (Lagos), and South Africa (Johannesburg). Accra servers typically provide the lowest latency for users in Lome at 20-40ms due to geographical proximity and shared submarine cable infrastructure. Mobile data speeds through Togocel or Moov on 4G can deliver 5-15 Mbps through a VPN in Lome, though speeds drop significantly outside major urban areas. During internet throttling events, obfuscated VPN connections may experience further speed reductions.
