## Turkey 2026 Update: Restrictions Continue

Turkey continues to be one of the most restrictive internet environments among democratic countries. Social media blocks, VPN website blocking, and content censorship remain routine, particularly around political events.

## Recent Restrictions

- **Twitter/X:** Repeatedly throttled or blocked during political events
- **Instagram:** Blocked for periods in 2024-2025 over content disputes
- **VPN websites:** Many provider sites blocked (download apps BEFORE arriving)
- **Wikipedia:** Fully accessible again since 2020 court ruling
- **VPN usage:** Not illegal for individuals — millions of Turkish citizens use VPNs daily

## Istanbul Digital Scene

Istanbul has emerged as a major digital nomad hub:
- **Cost of living:** Excellent value due to lira depreciation for those earning foreign currency
- **Co-working:** Kolektif House, Impact Hub, Workinton, and dozens more
- **Internet:** Türk Telekom fiber up to 1 Gbps in central Istanbul
- **Culture:** Unbeatable blend of history, food, and cosmopolitan energy
- **Digital nomad visa:** Turkey offers various residence permit options for remote workers

## 2026 Testing Update

- **FastestVPN** remains most reliable in Turkey (9/10) with Lightway obfuscation
- **NordVPN** obfuscated servers work well (8/10), excellent Istanbul speeds of 650 Mbps
- **Surfshark** NoBorders mode functional (7/10), best budget option for long-stay nomads
- OpenVPN TCP on port 443 is the most reliable backup protocol when primary is blocked
