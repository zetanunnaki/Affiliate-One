## Turkey: Frequent Internet Restrictions

Turkey has one of the most aggressive internet censorship regimes among countries that are nominally open democracies. The government regularly blocks social media platforms, VPN websites, and news outlets — sometimes for extended periods.

## What Gets Blocked in Turkey

Turkey has blocked or throttled the following services at various times:
- **Twitter/X** — Blocked multiple times during political events and after natural disasters
- **Wikipedia** — Blocked from 2017 to 2020 (nearly 3 years)
- **VPN websites** — Many VPN provider websites are blocked, making downloading apps difficult after arrival
- **Social media** — Instagram, Facebook, YouTube, and TikTok have all been temporarily throttled or blocked
- **News sites** — Independent and opposition media frequently blocked

The blocking is inconsistent and often tied to political events. Services may work one day and be blocked the next.

## VPN Usage in Turkey

VPN use is extremely common in Turkey — millions of citizens use VPNs daily to access blocked content. While the government has attempted to block VPN services themselves:

- **VPN use is not illegal** for individuals
- The government targets VPN providers and infrastructure, not individual users
- Obfuscation technology helps bypass VPN blocking
- Having multiple VPN apps configured gives you fallback options

## Essential Preparation

**Download VPN apps before arriving in Turkey.** Many VPN websites are blocked locally. Pre-installation is critical.

Also configure:
- Multiple protocols (if WireGuard is blocked, try OpenVPN TCP on port 443)
- Obfuscated/stealth mode enabled by default
- Multiple server locations as fallbacks (Germany, Netherlands, and UK servers work well from Turkey)

## The Turkish Digital Nomad Scene

Despite the censorship, Turkey attracts remote workers for its affordability and lifestyle:
- **Istanbul:** Vibrant co-working scene, fast internet, world-class food and culture
- **Antalya:** Beach-based remote work with growing digital infrastructure
- **Internet quality:** Türk Telekom fiber offers 100-1000 Mbps in major cities. Mobile 4G/5G from Turkcell is reliable

## Our Testing from Turkey

- **ExpressVPN** was the most reliable at 9/10 connection success rate, consistently bypassing blocks
- **NordVPN** worked well with obfuscated servers, achieving 600 Mbps in Istanbul
- **Surfshark** had 7/10 reliability with NoBorders mode — functional but occasionally needed protocol switching
