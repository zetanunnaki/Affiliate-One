## Internet Freedom & Privacy Landscape

Turkmenistan scores 7 out of 100 in Freedom House's Freedom on the Net report, making it one of the least free internet environments on Earth, second only to China and Myanmar. The state controls all telecommunications through Turkmentelecom (the sole ISP for fixed internet) and Altyn Asyr (the state-owned mobile operator). The government blocks virtually all independent news websites, social media platforms, messaging apps, and VPN provider websites. Internet speeds are deliberately throttled, connections are extremely expensive, and the state monitors all traffic through centralized chokepoints. Only an estimated 25% of the population has internet access, with meaningful connectivity limited to Ashgabat.

## VPN Legality

VPN use is heavily restricted in Turkmenistan. The government actively blocks VPN connections through deep packet inspection and has been reported to detain individuals found using circumvention tools. While no specific law explicitly criminalizes VPN use, authorities treat it as a security concern and enforcement is unpredictable. Users face real personal risk, and any VPN use should be approached with extreme caution and thorough operational security practices.

## Server Coverage & Speed Performance

No VPN provider operates servers in Turkmenistan, and the country's single international gateway through Turkmentelecom creates a severe bottleneck. The nearest useful server locations are in Turkey (Istanbul), UAE (Dubai), or Russia (Moscow), but connectivity to all of these is extremely slow due to deliberate throttling. Baseline internet speeds in Turkmenistan rarely exceed 1-3 Mbps even without a VPN, and VPN overhead reduces this further. Latency to Turkish servers is typically 150-300ms when connections succeed. Users should set extremely conservative expectations: basic text communication and email may work, but streaming or video calls are often impossible.

## Best VPN Protocols for Turkmenistan

Standard VPN protocols are blocked in Turkmenistan. WireGuard, standard OpenVPN, and IKEv2 connections are immediately identified and terminated by the state's DPI systems. Only heavily obfuscated protocols have any chance of functioning. Proton VPN's Stealth protocol, NordVPN's obfuscated servers (using OpenVPN over SSL/TLS tunneling), and Surfshark's Camouflage mode represent the primary options. Even these may require manual configuration with custom bridges, Shadowsocks front-ends, or domain fronting techniques. Users must download and configure VPN software before entering Turkmenistan, as all provider websites are blocked within the country. Multiple backup protocols and server addresses should be prepared, as working configurations can be blocked at any time.
