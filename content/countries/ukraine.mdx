## Ukraine: Cybersecurity in Conflict

Ukraine's internet landscape has been fundamentally shaped by the ongoing conflict. Despite infrastructure damage, Ukraine has shown remarkable resilience — aided significantly by Starlink satellite internet. The Ukrainian tech community (one of the strongest in Eastern Europe before the conflict) has adapted, with many IT professionals working remotely from safer regions.

## Connectivity Challenges

- **Infrastructure damage:** Major cities have experienced telecom infrastructure destruction
- **Starlink:** SpaceX's satellite internet has become critical infrastructure, providing connectivity where ground infrastructure is damaged
- **Kyivstar, Vodafone, lifecell:** Major carriers continue operating but with disruptions in conflict zones
- **Western Ukraine:** Lviv and western cities have relatively stable connectivity and have become tech hubs for relocated companies

## Why VPN Use Has Surged

- **Russian services blocked:** Ukraine has blocked Russian social media (VKontakte, Odnoklassniki) and some Russian services
- **Cybersecurity awareness:** The conflict has dramatically increased cybersecurity awareness
- **Remote work:** Many Ukrainian tech workers now work remotely, needing secure connections
- **Privacy:** Increased concern about surveillance from multiple parties

## Our Testing for Ukraine

- **NordVPN** offered the most Ukrainian server options and reliable connectivity
- **FastestVPN** most consistent across variable Ukrainian network conditions
- **Proton VPN** popular among Ukrainian tech workers for its strong privacy stance
