## Understanding the UAE's Internet Landscape

The United Arab Emirates has world-class internet infrastructure but significant content restrictions. The Telecommunications Regulatory Authority (TRA) regulates internet content, and ISPs Etisalat and du implement government-mandated blocking.

The most impactful restriction for remote workers and visitors: **VoIP services are heavily restricted.** WhatsApp calls, FaceTime, Skype, and Google Meet have historically been blocked or degraded. This makes a VPN essential for anyone who needs to make voice or video calls.

## What's Blocked in the UAE

- **VoIP services:** WhatsApp calls, FaceTime, Skype, Google Voice — many are blocked or throttled
- **Content:** Gambling, pornography, LGBTQ+ content, and content critical of the government/religion
- **Some VPN websites:** VPN provider websites may be blocked, but the VPN apps themselves typically work
- **VPN usage:** Using a VPN for lawful purposes is generally tolerated. Using a VPN to commit fraud or access illegal content carries penalties

**Important:** The UAE's cybercrime law carries severe penalties. Using a VPN to access legally available services (like VoIP calls to family) is widely practiced and generally tolerated, but technically falls in a gray area. We recommend using VPNs for legitimate purposes only.

## Essential for Remote Workers

The VoIP restrictions make a VPN practically mandatory for remote workers in the UAE:

- **Video conferencing:** Zoom, Microsoft Teams, and Google Meet may be partially blocked without a VPN
- **Voice calls:** WhatsApp and FaceTime audio calls require a VPN
- **Collaboration tools:** Some features of Slack, Discord, and other tools may be restricted
- **Access to company resources:** If your company uses VoIP or video conferencing, you'll need a VPN

## Setup Before Arrival

**Critical:** Download and install your VPN before arriving in the UAE. Some VPN provider websites are blocked locally. Pre-installation is the standard advice for all UAE-bound travelers.

Steps:
1. Subscribe to a VPN known to work in the UAE
2. Download apps on all devices
3. Enable obfuscation / stealth mode in settings
4. Test the connection before your flight
5. Set up both WireGuard and OpenVPN TCP protocols (if one is blocked, try the other)

## Our Testing from the UAE

Tested from Dubai and Abu Dhabi:

- **FastestVPN** was the most reliable in the UAE, with 9/10 connection success rate and VoIP access working consistently
- **NordVPN** worked well with obfuscated servers enabled, achieving 650 Mbps in Dubai
- **FastestVPN** had slightly lower reliability (7/10) but still functional with NoBorders mode enabled
- OpenVPN TCP on port 443 was the most reliable protocol when other protocols were blocked
- Hotel Wi-Fi networks sometimes had additional restrictions that required protocol switching
