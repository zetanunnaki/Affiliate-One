## Internet Freedom & Privacy Landscape

Uruguay scores an impressive 87/100 on the Freedom House "Freedom on the Net" index, making it one of the highest-rated countries in Latin America for internet freedom. The telecommunications market is uniquely structured around Antel, the state-owned operator that holds a monopoly on fixed-line broadband while mobile services are contested by Movistar and Claro alongside Antel. Uruguay's 2008 Data Protection Law (No. 18.331) is among Latin America's strongest, modeled on European GDPR principles, and the country's URCDP (data protection agency) actively enforces privacy rights. Despite this strong framework, Antel's monopoly position means all fixed-line traffic routes through a single operator.

## VPN Legality

VPN usage is fully legal in Uruguay with strong constitutional protections for communication privacy. The country places no restrictions on encryption technology, and Uruguay's progressive approach to digital rights makes it one of the most VPN-friendly jurisdictions in South America.

## Best VPN Protocols for Uruguay

WireGuard is the ideal protocol for Uruguay given the moderate latency to nearest servers and Antel's modern fiber network. The protocol's efficiency compensates for the additional distance to Buenos Aires or Sao Paulo servers. For users who need to bypass potential throttling on Antel's network during peak hours, OpenVPN with TCP on port 443 is indistinguishable from regular HTTPS traffic and avoids any QoS-based VPN deprioritization.
