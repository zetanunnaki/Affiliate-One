## Internet Freedom & Privacy Landscape

The US Virgin Islands is an unincorporated territory of the United States, meaning it operates under US federal law regarding internet governance, privacy, and telecommunications regulation. The internet is unrestricted and benefits from the same First Amendment protections as the US mainland. The telecommunications market is primarily served by Viya (formerly Innovative Communications Corporation) and AT&T. Viya provides fixed-line broadband, fiber, and mobile services across St. Thomas, St. Croix, and St. John, while AT&T offers mobile coverage throughout the territory. Infrastructure was significantly damaged by Hurricanes Irma and Maria in 2017 but has since been largely rebuilt with modernized equipment. Connectivity is generally strong in Charlotte Amalie, Christiansted, and Frederiksted, with more limited service in rural hillside areas.

## VPN Legality

VPNs are completely legal in the US Virgin Islands. As a US territory, all federal laws regarding internet freedom apply, and there are no additional local restrictions on VPN use or encrypted communications.

## Server Coverage & Speed Performance

No VPN providers maintain servers specifically in the US Virgin Islands. However, the nearest US mainland servers in Miami and Atlanta offer excellent connectivity at 30-50ms latency from the territory. Viya fiber subscribers can expect VPN speeds of 50-100 Mbps, providing a seamless experience for streaming, video calls, and remote work. AT&T LTE users typically see 15-40 Mbps through a VPN connection. The territory's rebuilt infrastructure and submarine cable connections to the mainland ensure reliable VPN performance year-round, with potential weather-related disruptions during hurricane season.
