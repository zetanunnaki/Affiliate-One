## Internet Freedom & Privacy Landscape

Uzbekistan scores 11 out of 100 on Freedom House's Freedom on the Net index, classified as "Not Free." Despite reforms under President Mirziyoyev since 2016 that have eased some restrictions, Uzbekistan remains one of the most heavily censored internet environments in Central Asia. The state maintains pervasive control over the digital space through Uztelecom (the state-owned backbone operator) and exerts regulatory pressure on private operators including Ucell, Beeline Uzbekistan, and UzMobile. The government employs deep packet inspection to filter content and throttle specific services.

Internet speeds in Tashkent have improved significantly, with fiber connections delivering 30-80 Mbps in the capital. However, outside major cities — including the tourist corridor of Samarkand, Bukhara, and Khiva — speeds drop considerably to 5-20 Mbps. Mobile 4G coverage from Ucell and Beeline covers urban areas adequately but remains patchy in rural regions. The government's UZINFOCOM agency actively monitors online activity, and bloggers and journalists have faced prosecution for critical posts.

## VPN Legality

VPN use exists in a legal gray area in Uzbekistan. While there is no explicit ban on VPN software, the government has blocked access to numerous VPN provider websites and periodically disrupts VPN protocols through DPI. Authorities have signaled displeasure with circumvention tools, and individuals using VPNs to access banned content could theoretically face penalties under broad internet regulation laws. Download and configure VPN applications before entering the country.

## Why You Need a VPN in Uzbekistan

Uzbekistan's "Not Free" classification and active censorship infrastructure make a VPN essential for unrestricted internet access.

- **Bypass extensive content blocking** — The government blocks political opposition sites, independent news outlets, social media platforms intermittently, and various international services; without a VPN, significant portions of the global internet are inaccessible from within Uzbekistan
- **Evade government surveillance** — Uztelecom's state-controlled backbone routes all traffic through monitoring infrastructure; a VPN with strong encryption prevents the authorities from inspecting your browsing activity, messaging content, and search queries
- **Protect sensitive communications for Silk Road tourism** — Growing numbers of tourists in Samarkand, Bukhara, and Khiva rely on hotel and cafe Wi-Fi that passes through state-monitored infrastructure, making VPN essential for banking, email, and personal communications

## Server Coverage & Speed Performance

No VPN providers operate servers inside Uzbekistan. The nearest server locations are in Kazakhstan (Almaty), Turkey (Istanbul), Russia (Moscow), and the UAE (Dubai). Due to Uzbekistan's landlocked position and routing through limited international transit links, latency tends to be higher than in coastal countries — expect 50-80 ms to Kazakhstan servers, 70-100 ms to Turkish nodes, and 60-90 ms to Russian servers. Given Tashkent's baseline of 30-80 Mbps, VPN connections typically retain 50-70% of base speed through nearby servers. Outside Tashkent, where baseline speeds are 5-20 Mbps, VPN overhead is more noticeable. Budget for slower connections and plan downloads accordingly.

## Best VPN Protocols for Uzbekistan

Obfuscation is critical in Uzbekistan. The government's DPI infrastructure actively identifies and throttles standard VPN protocols including standard OpenVPN and WireGuard traffic. Use obfuscated protocols (OpenVPN with obfsproxy, NordVPN's obfuscated servers, or Surfshark's Camouflage mode) as the primary connection method. Shadowsocks and V2Ray-based protocols also work effectively. Configure multiple protocol options before entering the country, as the filtering environment changes without notice. Standard WireGuard may work intermittently but should not be relied upon as the sole protocol.
