## Internet Freedom & Privacy Landscape

Vanuatu is a South Pacific archipelago of 83 islands with approximately 320,000 residents. The country maintains a democratic government with strong press freedom protections and no history of internet censorship. Telecommunications Radiocommunications and Broadcasting Regulator (TRBR) oversees the sector, with Digicel Vanuatu and Vodafone Vanuatu serving as the primary mobile operators while Telsat provides fixed broadband in urban areas. Internet penetration has grown to approximately 35%, concentrated in the capital Port Vila on Efate island and the secondary hub of Luganville on Espiritu Santo. The Interchange Cable Network (ICN) submarine cable connects Vanuatu to Fiji, significantly improving speeds since its 2014 activation. Outer islands rely on satellite connections with severely limited bandwidth.

## VPN Legality

VPNs are completely legal in Vanuatu. The government places no restrictions on encryption tools, privacy software, or VPN protocols. Vanuatu's offshore financial services sector actually encourages secure communications, and VPN use is common among businesses and expatriates.

## Why You Need a VPN in Vanuatu

Vanuatu's developing infrastructure and growing tourism sector create practical needs for VPN protection.

- **Secure resort and cafe Wi-Fi** — Port Vila's tourist-oriented businesses offer shared Wi-Fi networks vulnerable to interception; encryption protects banking credentials, personal data, and communications
- **Access home-country content** — Tourists and expatriates from Australia, New Zealand, and France (former colonial power) need VPN connections to access geo-restricted streaming services and banking platforms from their home countries
- **Protect against limited ISP privacy** — With only a few operators serving a small market, concentrated data collection is a concern; VPN encryption prevents ISP-level browsing activity logging

## Server Coverage & Speed Performance

No VPN provider operates servers in Vanuatu. The nearest locations are in Australia (Sydney), New Zealand (Auckland), and Fiji. The ICN submarine cable routes through Fiji to Australia, providing the primary international link. Baseline speeds in Port Vila average 5-20 Mbps on Digicel 4G and Telsat fixed connections. VPN connections to Australian servers add 40-70ms latency with 15-25% throughput reduction. New Zealand servers offer similar performance at 50-80ms. Outer islands on satellite connections experience 500ms+ latency regardless of VPN usage, making encrypted connections functional only for basic browsing and messaging.

## Best VPN Protocols for Vanuatu

No protocol restrictions exist in Vanuatu, so optimization for bandwidth-limited connections is the priority. WireGuard provides the most efficient protocol with minimal overhead, ideal for preserving speed on constrained submarine cable links. IKEv2 handles mobile network transitions well as users move between Digicel cellular and Wi-Fi hotspots around Port Vila. OpenVPN (UDP) offers reliable performance for those requiring compatibility with corporate VPN configurations.
