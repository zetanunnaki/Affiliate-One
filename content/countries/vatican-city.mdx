## Internet Freedom & Privacy Landscape

Vatican City is the world's smallest independent state at just 0.44 square kilometers, home to approximately 800 residents. The Vatican does not appear on Freedom House's Freedom on the Net index as a separate entity, but its internet infrastructure is closely tied to Italy's telecommunications networks. The Vatican maintains its own domain (.va) and internal network managed by the Vatican Internet Service, but public Wi-Fi access points for the millions of annual visitors route through Italian ISPs including TIM and Vodafone Italia. Italy scores 76 out of 100 (Free) on the Freedom on the Net index, and EU data protection standards under GDPR apply to the Italian infrastructure serving the territory. However, the Vatican itself is not an EU member state, creating a unique jurisdictional gap for data protection enforcement.

## VPN Legality

VPNs are completely legal to use in Vatican City and in the surrounding Italian territory. There are no restrictions on encryption tools, privacy software, or VPN protocols. Italy's legal framework, which effectively governs the telecom infrastructure visitors use, explicitly permits VPN usage for both personal and commercial purposes.

## Why You Need a VPN in Vatican City

While Vatican City presents no censorship concerns, the unique visitor environment creates genuine privacy and security needs. Millions of tourists and pilgrims visit annually, connecting to shared public networks in an extremely dense area.

- **Public Wi-Fi security** — The Vatican Museums, St. Peter's Square, and surrounding areas offer public Wi-Fi networks used by thousands simultaneously, creating prime conditions for man-in-the-middle attacks and packet sniffing
- **Geo-restricted content access** — Visitors staying in Rome wanting to access home-country streaming services, banking apps, or work platforms blocked by geographic restrictions need a VPN to connect to servers in their home region
- **Privacy from data collection** — Public networks operated by Italian telecoms are subject to Italy's data retention laws requiring ISPs to store connection metadata for up to six years, far longer than most EU countries

## Server Coverage & Speed Performance

Major VPN providers operate extensive server networks in Italy, with Rome being a primary location. NordVPN maintains over 60 servers in Italy, Surfshark offers multiple Italian locations, and Proton VPN operates servers in both Rome and Milan. Connecting to Italian servers from Vatican City yields negligible latency (under 5ms) since traffic routes through the same physical infrastructure. Expect speeds of 100-300 Mbps on hotel fiber connections, with VPN overhead reducing throughput by only 5-15%. For accessing content from other regions, connections to major European hubs in Frankfurt, Amsterdam, or London deliver latency under 30ms.

## Best VPN Protocols for Vatican City

With no censorship or protocol blocking to contend with, visitors can use the fastest available protocols without concern for obfuscation. WireGuard delivers the best speed-to-security ratio, offering near-native throughput with modern encryption. OpenVPN remains a reliable fallback for corporate compatibility requirements. IKEv2 is particularly effective for mobile users moving between cellular and Wi-Fi networks around the Vatican complex, as it handles connection switching seamlessly without dropping the tunnel.
