## Venezuela: Censorship Amid Crisis

Venezuela's internet landscape reflects the country's broader crisis. Government-controlled CANTV (the main ISP) provides unreliable service, independent media is blocked, and social media has been restricted during political events. VPN use is essential for accessing independent information.

## What's Restricted

- **News sites:** Independent media outlets blocked by CANTV
- **Social media:** Twitter, Instagram, and YouTube have been temporarily blocked during political events
- **Streaming:** Some international services throttled
- **VPN websites:** Some provider sites blocked, though VPN apps generally work

## Infrastructure Challenges

- **CANTV:** Government-owned broadband provider. Frequent outages, slow speeds
- **Private mobile:** Movistar, Digitel, Movilnet offer 4G in cities but quality varies
- **Power outages:** Nationwide blackouts have occurred, taking internet down with them
- **Speeds:** Among the slowest in Latin America when infrastructure is available

## VPN as a Lifeline

For Venezuelans, VPN is more than privacy — it's access to information:
- Independent news only accessible via VPN
- Communication with diaspora requires uncensored access
- Remote workers (growing) need VPN for client communication
- USD and crypto increasingly used — VPN protects financial transactions
