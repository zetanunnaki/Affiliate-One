## Vietnam: Digital Nomad Favorite With Content Restrictions

Vietnam has become one of Southeast Asia's most popular digital nomad destinations, with Ho Chi Minh City, Hanoi, Da Nang, and Hoi An attracting thousands of remote workers. The combination of extremely low cost of living, excellent food, fast internet, and abundant café Wi-Fi makes it ideal — as long as you navigate the content restrictions.

## What's Restricted in Vietnam

Vietnam's government monitors and censors internet content under the 2018 Cybersecurity Law:
- **Facebook:** Occasionally throttled or restricted during sensitive political periods
- **News sites:** Independent and foreign news outlets periodically blocked
- **Political content:** Critical political commentary is censored
- **Some VPN websites:** VPN provider websites may be blocked, though the apps themselves typically work

Unlike China, Vietnam's censorship is less comprehensive and less technically sophisticated. Most VPN services work reliably.

## The Digital Nomad Scene

Vietnam offers incredible value for remote workers:
- **Ho Chi Minh City:** Vibrant, fast-paced with dozens of co-working spaces. Fiber internet 100-500 Mbps for $10-20/month
- **Da Nang:** Beach city with growing nomad community. Excellent café Wi-Fi culture along the coast
- **Hanoi:** Cultural capital with affordable living and strong co-working infrastructure
- **Hoi An:** Charming old town with a relaxed nomad community and surprising connectivity

Café Wi-Fi is ubiquitous and often free — but always use a VPN on these shared networks.
