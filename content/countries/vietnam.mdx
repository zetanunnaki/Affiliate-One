## Vietnam: Digital Nomad Favorite With Content Restrictions

Vietnam has become one of Southeast Asia's most popular digital nomad destinations, with Ho Chi Minh City, Hanoi, Da Nang, and Hoi An attracting thousands of remote workers. The combination of extremely low cost of living (a comfortable lifestyle for 800-1,200 USD per month), excellent food, fast internet, and abundant cafe Wi-Fi makes it ideal — as long as you navigate the content restrictions that come with operating under one of Asia's most active censorship regimes.

## What's Restricted in Vietnam

Vietnam's government monitors and censors internet content under the 2018 Cybersecurity Law and the 2024 amendments that expanded platform liability:

- **Facebook:** Occasionally throttled or restricted during sensitive political periods, though it remains Vietnam's most popular social network with over 70 million users. The government pressures Facebook to remove content rather than block the platform outright
- **News sites:** Independent and foreign news outlets periodically blocked, particularly those covering human rights, political dissent, or territorial disputes in the South China Sea
- **Political content:** Critical political commentary is actively censored. Bloggers and social media users have been prosecuted under Article 117 of the Penal Code for "anti-state propaganda"
- **Some VPN websites:** VPN provider websites may be blocked or throttled, though the apps themselves typically work once installed. Always download VPN apps before arriving in Vietnam

Unlike China, Vietnam's censorship is less comprehensive and less technically sophisticated. The blocking relies primarily on DNS filtering and IP blocking rather than deep packet inspection. Most VPN services work reliably without requiring special obfuscation protocols, though having OpenVPN TCP as a fallback is prudent.

## The Digital Nomad Scene

Vietnam offers incredible value for remote workers at every budget level:

- **Ho Chi Minh City:** Vibrant, fast-paced with dozens of co-working spaces (Dreamplex, Toong, CirCO). Fiber internet from FPT, VNPT, and Viettel delivers 100-500 Mbps for 200,000-400,000 VND per month (8-16 USD). District 1 and District 2 (Thu Duc) are popular nomad neighborhoods
- **Da Nang:** Beach city with a growing nomad community and excellent cafe Wi-Fi culture along the coast. My Khe beach area combines ocean living with reliable internet. Co-working spaces like Enouvo and Hub Hoi An serve the region
- **Hanoi:** Cultural capital with affordable living and strong co-working infrastructure in the Old Quarter and Tay Ho (West Lake) areas
- **Hoi An:** Charming old town with a relaxed nomad community and surprising connectivity for a small city

Cafe Wi-Fi is ubiquitous and often genuinely free (no purchase requirement at many local cafes). Speeds range from 20-80 Mbps — but always use a VPN on these shared networks, as basic network security is often nonexistent.

## Payment and Practical Considerations

Vietnamese dong (VND) is the local currency, and international credit cards work at major establishments. VPN subscriptions should be purchased with international cards or PayPal before arrival, as Vietnamese payment methods (MoMo, ZaloPay, domestic bank cards) are not typically supported by international VPN providers. Mobile SIM cards from Viettel, Mobifone, or Vinaphone cost under 5 USD with generous data packages — always run VPN on mobile data as well.
