## Internet Freedom & Privacy Landscape

Yemen scores just 10 out of 100 on Freedom House's Freedom on the Net index, making it one of the least free internet environments globally. The ongoing civil conflict has fractured internet governance between the internationally recognized government in Aden and Houthi authorities controlling the north, including the capital Sanaa. YemenNet, the state-owned provider historically controlling all fixed-line internet, operates under Houthi control in the north, while Aden Net and newer mobile operators serve southern areas. Internet penetration hovers around 27%, with most connections occurring over mobile data on 3G networks. Infrastructure damage from years of airstrikes means entire regions lack any connectivity whatsoever.

## VPN Legality

VPNs are not explicitly banned by law in Yemen, though Houthi authorities have taken steps to restrict access to VPN websites and block common protocols. In practice, enforcement is inconsistent due to the fractured governance situation. However, users in Houthi-controlled areas should exercise caution, as online activity deemed politically sensitive has led to arrests. Foreign aid workers and journalists routinely use VPNs, but downloading and configuring them before entering the country is strongly advised.

## Why You Need a VPN in Yemen

Internet access in Yemen is subject to pervasive censorship, surveillance, and unpredictable shutdowns. Both warring factions monitor communications and restrict access to content they deem threatening to their authority.

- **Bypass censorship blocks** — Access news outlets, social media platforms, and VoIP services like WhatsApp and Telegram that face recurring blocks, particularly in Houthi-controlled territories
- **Protect against surveillance** — Shield browsing activity from ISP-level monitoring mandated by both competing authorities, which have detained individuals based on their online communications
- **Maintain communications during throttling** — VPN encryption can help circumvent protocol-specific throttling that targets VoIP and video calling services, keeping you connected to family and colleagues abroad

## Server Coverage & Speed Performance

No VPN provider maintains physical servers inside Yemen due to the conflict and infrastructure constraints. Users connect to nearby servers in Saudi Arabia, the UAE, or Djibouti. On a functioning 3G connection in Sanaa or Aden, expect baseline speeds of 2-8 Mbps, with VPN overhead reducing throughput by 20-40%. Latency to Dubai-based servers typically ranges from 80-150ms. During active conflict escalations, authorities have imposed complete internet blackouts lasting days or weeks, during which no VPN can restore connectivity. When the network is operational, connecting to UAE or Bahrain servers generally provides the most stable experience.

## Best VPN Protocols for Yemen

Given active deep packet inspection deployed by YemenNet and partner ISPs, standard OpenVPN and WireGuard protocols are frequently detected and throttled. Obfuscated connections are essential for reliable access. NordVPN's obfuscated servers, Surfshark's Camouflage Mode, and Proton VPN's Stealth protocol disguise VPN traffic as regular HTTPS, bypassing DPI systems. Users should pre-install VPN apps and download manual configuration files before arriving in-country, as VPN provider websites are intermittently blocked.
