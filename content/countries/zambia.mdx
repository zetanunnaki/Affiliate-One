## Internet Freedom & Privacy Landscape

Zambia scores 52 out of 100 on Freedom House's Freedom on the Net index, classified as "Partly Free." The country experienced notable internet restrictions during the 2021 general elections when authorities ordered ISPs to throttle social media platforms. The Cyber Security and Cyber Crimes Act of 2021 expanded government surveillance powers and criminalized certain forms of online speech. Major ISPs include Zamtel (state-owned), MTN Zambia, and Airtel Zambia, which together provide mobile coverage to most urban areas. Lusaka and the Copperbelt enjoy the best connectivity, with average mobile speeds of 15-30 Mbps on 4G, while rural areas rely on 2G/3G with minimal bandwidth.

## VPN Legality

VPN use is legal in Zambia. No legislation specifically prohibits VPN software or encryption tools. During the 2021 election restrictions, VPN downloads surged dramatically as citizens sought to maintain social media access, with no legal consequences for users.

## Server Coverage & Speed Performance

No major VPN provider operates physical servers in Zambia. The nearest server locations are in South Africa (Johannesburg, Cape Town), connected through terrestrial fiber via Zimbabwe and Botswana. Johannesburg servers typically provide latency of 30-50 ms from Lusaka, offering the best balance of proximity and server quality. Additional options include Kenya (Nairobi) at 40-60 ms.
