## Internet Freedom & Privacy Landscape

Zimbabwe scores 31 out of 100 on Freedom House's Freedom on the Net index, classified as "Partly Free." The government has a documented history of imposing internet shutdowns during political protests â€” most notably the complete shutdown in January 2019 during fuel price demonstrations. The Interception of Communications Act grants authorities broad surveillance powers, and the Cyber and Data Protection Act of 2021 has been used to prosecute online critics. Major ISPs include Econet Wireless (dominant mobile provider), NetOne (state-owned), and Telecel Zimbabwe. Mobile internet dominates, with EcoCash mobile money integrated into daily life. Average speeds reach 10-25 Mbps on 4G in Harare and Bulawayo.

## VPN Legality

VPN use is legal in Zimbabwe. No specific law prohibits VPN software, and many citizens adopted VPNs during the 2019 shutdown, normalizing their use. However, the broadly-worded Cyber and Data Protection Act could theoretically be applied against VPN users in extreme circumstances, though this has not occurred in practice.

## Server Coverage & Speed Performance

No major VPN provider operates servers in Zimbabwe. The nearest server locations are in South Africa (Johannesburg), connected via terrestrial fiber through Botswana or direct links. Johannesburg servers offer latency of 25-40 ms from Harare thanks to well-established cross-border fiber infrastructure. Kenya (Nairobi) serves as an alternative at 60-80 ms. Given Zimbabwe's moderate mobile speeds, expect to retain 50-65% of base bandwidth when connecting to South African servers. The proximity to Johannesburg makes VPN performance relatively strong compared to many African nations.
