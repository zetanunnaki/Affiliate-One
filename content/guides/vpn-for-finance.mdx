## Financial Data Is the Highest-Value Target

Financial professionals handle the data attackers want most: bank accounts, investment portfolios, tax returns, SSNs, and trading strategies. A breach doesn't just expose data — it can result in regulatory fines, client lawsuits, and career-ending reputational damage.

## What You're Protecting

- **Client financial accounts:** Brokerage, banking, retirement accounts
- **Tax documents:** Returns, W-2s, 1099s containing SSNs
- **Trading activity:** Strategies, positions, material non-public information
- **Client PII:** Names, addresses, SSNs, dates of birth
- **Communication:** Advisor-client privileged communications

## Compliance Requirements

- **SEC Regulation S-P:** Requires financial firms to protect client information
- **FINRA rules:** Require cybersecurity policies including encryption
- **GLBA (Gramm-Leach-Bliley):** Requires financial institutions to protect consumer data
- **SOX:** Requires internal controls including data security

A VPN helps meet encryption-in-transit requirements across all these regulations.

## The Financial Professional VPN Setup

1. **Dedicated IP VPN** — Your firm's compliance systems may require IP whitelisting
2. **Kill switch mandatory** — A VPN drop during a trade or client session is unacceptable
3. **No split tunneling for work** — ALL financial work traffic through VPN. No exceptions
4. **VPN on mobile** — Bloomberg, Schwab, and other apps used on phone need VPN protection
5. **Separate devices** — Don't use personal devices for client financial data if possible
