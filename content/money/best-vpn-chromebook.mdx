## Chromebook VPN: Android App Is the Way

Chrome OS supports VPN through Android apps (Google Play Store), Chrome extensions, and Linux apps. The Android app method is by far the best — it protects ALL device traffic, not just browser traffic.

## Our Rankings

### 1. NordVPN — Best Chromebook VPN
Android app works flawlessly on Chrome OS. NordLynx protocol. Full kill switch. Split tunneling. Threat Protection. Available from Google Play Store.

### 2. FastestVPN — Best Value for Chromebook
Unlimited devices — your Chromebook is covered alongside everything else. Android app with CleanWeb ad blocking. Cheapest premium option.

### 3. FastestVPN — Most Reliable
Lightway protocol for instant connections. Consistent performance across Chromebook models. Clean Android app UI.

## Method Comparison

| Method | Protects | Difficulty | Features |
|--------|----------|------------|----------|
| Android App (Play Store) | All traffic | Easy | Full (kill switch, split tunnel) |
| Chrome Extension | Browser only | Very easy | Limited (proxy only) |
| Linux App (Crostini) | Linux container only | Hard | Varies by provider |

**Always use the Android app.** Chrome extensions only protect browser traffic — email clients, Linux apps, and system traffic are unprotected.

## School Chromebook Warning

School-managed Chromebooks may block VPN installation through admin policies. If you can't install from Play Store:
- Check with your school's IT policy
- A Chrome extension may work as a partial workaround
- A portable router with VPN (GL.iNet) works regardless of Chromebook restrictions

## Setup (2 Minutes)

1. Open Google Play Store on Chromebook
2. Search for your VPN (NordVPN, FastestVPN, etc.)
3. Install and open
4. Log in and connect
5. Done — all Chrome OS traffic is encrypted
