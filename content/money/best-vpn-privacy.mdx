## Privacy vs Speed: Different Priorities

Most VPN comparisons focus on speed. This one focuses on privacy. The fastest VPN isn't necessarily the most private. Here we rank providers by the metrics that matter for privacy: logging policy, jurisdiction, transparency, audit history, and anonymous payment options.

## Our Privacy Ranking

### 1. Mullvad — Best for Maximum Privacy

Mullvad is the privacy purist's VPN. No email, no name, no account — just a random number. Cash payments accepted. In 2023, Swedish police seized Mullvad equipment and found zero customer data.

**Privacy credentials:**
- No personal data collected whatsoever
- Cash and cryptocurrency payments accepted
- Swedish police seizure proved no-logs in practice
- Open-source apps on all platforms
- Independent audit by Assured AB

**Trade-off:** Only 5 devices, no streaming optimization, basic apps.

### 2. Proton VPN — Best Privacy With Usability

Proton VPN offers serious privacy with a polished experience. Swiss jurisdiction, open-source apps, audited no-logs, and Secure Core routing that bounces traffic through privacy-friendly countries.

**Privacy credentials:**
- Swiss jurisdiction (outside intelligence alliances)
- Fully open-source apps (audited by Securitum)
- Secure Core multi-hop through Switzerland, Iceland, Sweden
- Genuine free tier funded by subscribers, not data
- Transparency reports published regularly

**Trade-off:** Secure Core adds latency. Free tier limited to 3 countries.

### 3. NordVPN — Best Balance of Privacy and Features

NordVPN offers strong privacy with the best feature set. Panama jurisdiction, audited no-logs by Deloitte, RAM-only servers, and threat protection.

**Privacy credentials:**
- Panama jurisdiction (no data retention laws)
- Deloitte-audited no-logs policy
- RAM-only colocated servers
- Double VPN (multi-hop) option
- Active HackerOne bug bounty program

**Trade-off:** Not open-source. Owned by Nord Security (Lithuanian parent company).

## When Privacy Matters Most

A privacy-focused VPN is especially important when:
- You're a journalist, activist, or whistleblower
- You handle sensitive business or legal communications
- You're in a country with government surveillance
- You want to minimize your digital footprint
- You don't trust your ISP with your browsing data

For most remote workers, NordVPN or Proton VPN offer the right privacy/usability balance. For maximum anonymity, Mullvad is unmatched.
