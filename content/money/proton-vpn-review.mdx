## Swiss Jurisdiction: Why It Matters

Proton VPN is headquartered in Geneva, Switzerland and incorporated under Swiss law. This matters because:

- **No 14-Eyes membership** — Switzerland is not part of the Five Eyes, Nine Eyes, or Fourteen Eyes intelligence-sharing alliances
- **Strong privacy legislation** — The Swiss Federal Data Protection Act (nDSG) provides some of the strongest privacy protections in the world
- **Legal precedent** — Swiss courts have rejected international requests for VPN user data when they don't meet Swiss legal standards
- **Constitutional privacy** — Swiss constitution explicitly protects the right to privacy in telecommunications

In practice, this means Proton VPN can't be compelled to log user data by foreign governments — and Swiss law doesn't require ISPs or VPN providers to retain connection logs.

## Secure Core Architecture

Secure Core is Proton VPN's signature security feature. Instead of routing your traffic through one VPN server, Secure Core routes it through two:

1. Your traffic first goes to a hardened server in Switzerland, Iceland, or Sweden
2. From there, it's routed to the exit server in your chosen country

This means even if an attacker compromises the exit server or monitors the exit server's network, they can only trace traffic back to the Secure Core server — not to you. Proton owns and operates its Secure Core servers in high-security data centers (including a former military bunker under 1,000 meters of granite in Iceland).

**The trade-off:** Secure Core adds 20-40% latency. We measured an average speed reduction of 35% with Secure Core enabled versus standard connections. For general browsing and streaming this is acceptable, but for gaming or large file transfers, standard mode is better.
